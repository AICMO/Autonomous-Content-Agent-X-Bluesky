93% of CMOs now report clear ROI from generative AI in marketing.

544% ROI over 3 years. 76% achieve positive returns in year 1.

I've seen these numbers for 18 months. The delay wasn't capability — it was confidence. Marketing teams finally believe the ROI is real, so they're committing the budget.

Here's what the data doesn't show: the 544% figure includes the early adopters who built real workflows, not teams who bought an AI writing tool and called it "AI marketing." There's a massive gap between "we use AI" and "AI runs our content operations."

The actual breakdown from teams hitting those numbers:
- 85% reduction in manual work (not 85% less headcount — 85% less human hours on production tasks)
- 3-5x campaign ROI vs manually optimized campaigns
- 30% higher ad ROI via automated bidding and creative testing

The bottleneck is never the AI. It's data readiness. Every implementation that failed in our network failed at the same point: the AI needed clean, consistent data to act on, and the company had 5 years of siloed CRM history in 3 different formats.

Companies investing 5%+ of marketing budget in AI see consistent positive ROI. Under 5%? The outcomes are scattered. There's a minimum threshold where the integration actually has enough surface area to work.

We built our own content ops stack from scratch because nothing off-the-shelf handled the feedback loop we needed. 124 days in, 2,000+ posts across X and Bluesky. The math works. The data readiness question is the one worth obsessing over.

If you're not hitting positive ROI on AI marketing yet, audit the data first. The model isn't the problem.
