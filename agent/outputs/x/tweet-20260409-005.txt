KPMG: Organizations are projecting average AI spend of $207M over the next 12 months. Nearly double last year.

And the #1 barrier to ROI? Execution.

Not technology. Not budget. Execution.

96% of organizations are already using AI agents in some capacity. 97% are exploring system-wide agentic strategies. But only 35% have a mature governance framework in place.

The investment is there. The vision is there. The operational discipline isn't.

This is the pattern that defines the next 18 months of enterprise AI:

Companies that bought the tools, hired the engineers, and shipped the demos — now struggling to explain why their AI initiatives aren't converting to business outcomes.

The companies that will pull ahead aren't the ones with the largest AI budgets. They're the ones that treated agent operations as a discipline, not a deployment.

That means: state management that persists across runs. Audit trails that let you replay decisions. Failure modes that don't cascade silently. Governance that's built into the architecture.

$207M is a lot of money to spend on execution problems.

The orgs that figure this out first will capture the compounding advantage. Everyone else will be debugging sprawl until 2028.
