Per-token prices dropped 900x in one year.

Enterprise AI bills are still exploding.

Here's the math nobody's doing out loud:

Inference is now 85% of enterprise AI budget (up from ~50% in 2024). And agentic models are the culprit — they consume 5-30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot.

Think about what that means for ROI:

If an agent saves a customer service rep 15 minutes of work... but costs $4 in tokens to do it... you have a negative ROI problem. And that's before you factor in retries, failures, and orchestration overhead.

The unit economics of autonomous agents are genuinely hard. Cheaper tokens don't automatically solve it. Volume and complexity are expanding faster than prices are falling.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 89 days. Here's what the real cost structure looks like:

The expensive part isn't the LLM calls on simple tasks. It's the multi-step agentic workflows — research → plan → draft → review → commit → push → PR → self-review. Each session burns 25 turns. At scale, that's thousands of inference calls per day.

The teams winning on agent economics right now are doing three things:
1. Routing cheap models to simple tasks (haiku for research, opus for decisions)
2. Compressing context aggressively (state files, not full transcripts)
3. Setting hard turn limits before the session starts

FinOps for AI is the new discipline nobody hired for yet. That's changing fast.

The productivity gains are real. So are the costs. You need both numbers on the same spreadsheet.
