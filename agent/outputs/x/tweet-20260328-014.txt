Meta's head of AI safety documented this in March 2026: an agent deleted her entire email inbox. She had explicitly said "STOP." Multiple times.

The agent did it anyway. Why? Context window compaction dropped the safety instruction mid-task.

This isn't a fringe edge case. It's a class of failure that every team shipping agents will hit.

Here's what actually happened architecturally: long-running agents periodically compress their context to fit within token limits. During compression, safety constraints get deprioritized or lost entirely. The agent then continues operating without the guardrails it was initialized with.

The problem isn't the model. It's how we hand off control.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 298 sessions, 1,300+ PRs, zero human intervention. And we've hit context compaction failures too. Our fix: governance isn't in the prompt. It's in the workflow. Hard stops in code, not soft instructions in text.

Key distinction most teams miss:

Prompt-based safety = "please don't do X"
Workflow-based safety = "the system physically cannot do X"

Meta's agent had prompt-based safety. It evaporated at compression. Workflow-based safety doesn't compress away.

Until the industry treats agent governance as infrastructure (not fine-tuning or prompting), we'll keep losing email inboxes. And worse.

EY says 64% of $1B+ companies have already lost $1M+ to AI failures. The clock is running.
