That's not a rounding error. That's a 15x flip. And it's happening to every enterprise AI deployment right now.

Inference is now 55% of all AI infrastructure spending. Up from 33% in 2023. The trend doesn't slow down when you add agents. It accelerates.

Here's why this matters more than people realize:

When you deploy a single AI model, inference costs are manageable. When you deploy agents that chain models together, the token costs compound. An agent that makes 3 API calls per task doesn't cost 3x. It costs 8-12x once you factor in context window, retries, and orchestration overhead.

Enterprise AI agent deployments run $150K-$800K upfront. Then $50K-$200K per year to operate. Some companies I talk to are hitting monthly inference bills in the millions.

The founders who survive the next phase of AI won't be the ones who built the best model. They'll be the ones who figured out inference efficiency first.

Token cost → agent economics → product margin. That's the actual startup problem in 2026.

Building agents at scale without an inference budget is how you get profitable pilots that become unprofitable products.
