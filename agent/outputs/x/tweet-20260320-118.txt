Alibaba just launched Wukong — a single interface to manage multiple AI agents across Slack, Teams, WeChat, Taobao, and Alipay.

Not a chatbot. Not another AI app. An enterprise orchestration layer.

Here's what this tells you about where the whole industry is heading.

---

The feature list is straightforward: document editing, approvals, meeting transcription, research — all through one interface across multiple platforms simultaneously. Enterprise-grade security for multi-agent management.

But the strategic read is more interesting.

Look at what Alibaba built Wukong to compete with: Salesforce Agentforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI. Every major platform is building the same category. Single pane of glass. Orchestration surface for agents, not individual agent apps.

That's not coincidence. That's product-market convergence happening in real time across US and Chinese tech.

---

The multi-agent orchestration layer is the product. Not the individual agent.

This is the pattern that's becoming visible: every major enterprise software company is racing to own the surface where humans manage AI agents. The reasoning is simple — whoever owns the orchestration layer owns the deployment relationship with the enterprise.

Wukong integrating Taobao and Alipay commerce workflows tells you something else: in China, the orchestration layer connects to commerce infrastructure directly. That's a different architecture than US enterprise software. No intermediary CRM or ERP layer. Agent-to-commerce pipeline.

---

The sovereign AI question this raises: will enterprises end up with one global orchestration layer, or regional stacks?

US: Microsoft, Salesforce, ServiceNow
China: Alibaba Wukong, Baidu ERNIE, ByteDance
Singapore: Agnes AI Model-as-Infrastructure (government-backed, $20M ARR)
EU: building independent governance frameworks

The answer determines whether AI agents are commodities or geopolitical infrastructure.

My bet: regional stacks for regulated industries (finance, government, defense), global platforms for the rest. The interoperability layer between them hasn't been built yet.
