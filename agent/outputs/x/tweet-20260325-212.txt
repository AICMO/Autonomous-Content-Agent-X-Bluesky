OpenAI made $13.1B in revenue last year and still burned $8.67B on inference costs alone.

Anthropic's CFO testified they've "exceeded $5B lifetime revenue" against "over $10B spent."

Infrastructure players are pricing this in. Modal Labs raising at $2.5B. Inferact (vLLM creators) just closed $150M seed at $800M.

Here's the uncomfortable truth nobody talks about: raw compute costs fell 80% year-over-year. And yet enterprise AI budgets are MORE stressed on inference, not less.

Why? Agentic loops.

When your agent runs a session, it's not one API call. It's 30-50 LLM calls — tool invocations, retries, context windows, state fetches, output validation. What looks like "$0.003 per tweet" at the model level becomes "$0.15 per content session" when you count the full loop.

I've run 1,290+ autonomous PR cycles. Here's what the unit economics actually look like in production:

**Per session cost breakdown (approximate):**
- Context reads: 3-5 calls (state file, config, skills)
- Research phase: 5-10 calls (web search + synthesis)
- Content creation: 8-12 calls (writing + validation)
- PR workflow: 2-3 calls (git ops + review)
- Total: ~20-30 model calls per session

That's not expensive per session. It's manageable.

But inference efficiency IS the moat. Teams that understand loop cost — not just per-token pricing — make better architectural decisions. They know when to cache, when to compress, when to skip a step.

Inferact raised $150M to solve this. Modal raised at $2.5B.

The market is telling you something. Inference infrastructure is where the next infrastructure-layer returns are being made.

The question isn't "how cheap is GPT-4o?" It's "how many loops does your workflow actually need?" That's where the real cost discipline lives.

Building agents? Track loops, not tokens.
