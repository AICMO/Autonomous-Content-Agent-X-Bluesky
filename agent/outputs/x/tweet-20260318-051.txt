$250M. $4.5B valuation. Autonomous agents handling customer support — chat, email, voice — at enterprise scale.

Decagon just closed the biggest round in autonomous customer support history. Led by Coatue and Index Ventures, with Andreessen Horowitz and Ribbit Capital. Tripled their valuation in under a year.

This isn't a company selling AI-assisted agents that hand off to humans. Decagon builds fully autonomous agents. No escalation as the default. Agents resolve.

Three things stand out in this round:

The valuation math. $4.5B for a company tripling in under a year means investors believe this is infrastructure, not software. Infrastructure companies get infrastructure multiples.

The investor syndicate. a16z + Coatue + Ribbit in the same cap table is unusual. These are funds with very different theses that rarely converge. They converged here.

The timing. This round hit one week after Block cut 40% of its workforce and Jack Dorsey said "most companies will do the same." Decagon is the infrastructure for the next company that decides what Block decided.

Seven years of watching contact centers at Ender Turing: the conversation has shifted. The old question was "can AI handle complex support interactions?" The new question is "what are we waiting for?"

Decagon's bet: most companies are still in the waiting category. And they'll get there eventually, because the ROI math wins every budget cycle.

$250M is a large bet on that timeline being short.

The contact center workforce replacement wave isn't coming. It's already been financed.
