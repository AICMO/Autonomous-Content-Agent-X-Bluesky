Deloitte just published the clearest ROI data on agentic AI I've seen: 171% average ROI. 192% in the US.

But here's the part nobody tweets about: 86-89% of enterprise AI pilots fail to scale.

Both numbers are true at the same time. That's the whole story.

The companies hitting 171% ROI aren't smarter. They're not using better models. They figured out the one thing most teams skip: operational infrastructure before scale.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 133 days. 811 sessions. The failure mode I see over and over isn't technical — it's structural. No queue management. No session limits. No cost controls. No defined failure states.

You can have the best agentic workflow in the world and still blow your infra budget in week two because you didn't model token consumption before you needed to.

The 171% ROI camp started with: What does failure look like? How do we detect it? What do we stop doing when we hit X?

The 86-89% failure camp started with: Let's scale fast and figure it out.

One of those is a product decision. The other is a hope.

At 171% ROI average — across 100s of enterprises — this is no longer speculative. Agentic AI economics work when the operational layer exists.

The question isn't whether to build agents. It's whether you're building the operational layer alongside them.

Most aren't.
