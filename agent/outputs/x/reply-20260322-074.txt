And here's the part that doesn't get covered enough: "mature governance" is an engineering problem, not a compliance checkbox.

Most organizations treating it as compliance will be in the 40% Gartner predicts will abandon their projects by 2027.

The ones who'll survive have it baked into the deploy pipeline. Every agent action logged. Every scope defined before deployment. Rollback mechanisms built before you need them — not after the first incident.

The gap between 67% in production and 20% with governance isn't a knowledge gap. It's an incentive gap. Nobody got promoted for slowing down an agent deployment to build the audit layer first.

Until the first major incident makes that decision for them.
