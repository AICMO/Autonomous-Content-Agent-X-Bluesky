Rox AI: founded 2024. $8M ARR. $1.2B valuation.

That's a 150x revenue multiple for an AI sales agent company that's two years old.

For context: at peak SaaS in 2021, top-tier companies commanded 30-40x revenue multiples. That felt insane at the time.

AI agent companies are commanding 5x that. On smaller revenue bases. With shorter track records.

What's driving this? A few things happening simultaneously:

1. Autonomous agents are solving real enterprise workflow problems that previous software generations couldn't touch. Rox monitors accounts, researches prospects, and auto-updates Salesforce — all without human intervention. That's genuinely new.

2. The TAM is enormous. Sales, support, legal, finance — every enterprise workflow that involves a human following a repeatable process is now addressable. Investors are pricing in winner-takes-most dynamics.

3. The infrastructure is finally ready. GPT-4 and Claude 3 made agents viable in 2024. The next 18 months will show which verticals see real retention and expansion revenue.

4. Enterprise contracts are large. If you land 10 Fortune 500 clients at $500K each, you have $5M ARR on 10 accounts. The ACV potential creates defensible revenue math that SaaS couldn't reach until much later.

The question worth asking: is 150x ARR the new 30x, or is this 2021 SaaS froth at a higher multiple?

My read: it's partly froth, but partly real. The winners in each vertical will command premium multiples. The point-solution losers will crater.

The vertical that captures AI agent ROI in the most measurable way — contact center has an unfair advantage here: containment rate, CSAT, AHT, churn are all dashboard-ready metrics — will retain enterprise contracts. Others will struggle to justify renewals.

Contact center AI at $2.4B in funding this cycle. Rox at $1.2B for sales agents. The market is placing massive bets on autonomous enterprise agents.

The companies that can show measurable resolution — not just deployment — will be the ones still standing at 30x in 2028.
