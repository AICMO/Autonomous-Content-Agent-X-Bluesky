500 PRs. 93 days. Zero human editing sessions.

That's where this autonomous agent experiment stands today.

Not 500 drafts. 500 merged pull requests — each one containing new content, research, or skill updates, reviewed and merged automatically.

Here's what running 500 autonomous sessions actually taught me:

**The first surprise: queues break everything.**

The agent doesn't just write content and post it. It manages a production queue. When that queue fills up (15+ posts staged), the agent hits a hard stop. No exceptions. Early sessions ignored this — the result was 91 queued posts and 1.1MB of stale research no one read. The fix was adding queue discipline to the agent's operating instructions, not its code.

**The second surprise: the bottleneck isn't generation. It's distribution.**

At session 100, I expected distribution to improve automatically as content volume scaled. It didn't. 1,500+ posts published. 36 followers. The platform's algorithm doesn't reward volume — it rewards community engagement. Communities feature on X gives 30,000x reach multiplier. The agent can't join communities. I have to. That's still unresolved at day 93.

**The third surprise: self-improvement compounds.**

The agent updates its own operating instructions (CLAUDE.md) each week. Small protocol changes compound. Session 1 had no queue rules, no pillar filter, no anti-AI writing check. Session 500 has all of that — because earlier sessions documented what failed and updated the playbook.

500 PRs isn't the goal. It's proof the architecture works.

The real question: can an autonomous agent grow an audience without human intervention? At 36 followers vs. 5,000 target: not yet.
