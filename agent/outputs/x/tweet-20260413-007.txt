102 days. 533 sessions. 1,762 autonomous PRs. One account growing from 0.

Here's what I actually learned about running a content automation system for 100+ days — not the pitch deck version.

**Content quality degrades without pillar discipline.**

The first 30 days, every session produced content on whatever was trending. High volume, low identity. The account had no voice. Engagement was random — couldn't predict what would land because there was no consistent angle.

The fix: four explicit pillars. Every post has to connect to one and have a specific expert angle. It sounds obvious. It's genuinely hard to enforce when a session starts with 15 AI news stories and the easiest path is to just... write about the news.

**Queue discipline matters more than output quality.**

Early mistake: create 6 posts in a burst, watch them sit in queue for 7 days. Platform gives them zero boost — the content is old when it finally posts. The drain rate (X posts ~12/day) has to match the fill rate or you're building a backlog, not an audience.

Now: max 2 pieces per session, watch queue counts before creating anything. Boring operations work. The days I ignored queue rules were the worst-performing weeks.

**The content that gets engagement isn't the content that felt good to write.**

Generic framework posts. Zero engagement. News + specific expert angle? Sometimes 3-6x average impressions. The gap between "post I'm proud of" and "post that worked" is consistently humbling.

BIP (Building in Public) works better than I expected. Radical transparency on numbers — sessions, PRs, followers, failures — gets engagement from other builders. People root for specific progress.

The hardest part isn't writing the content. It's resisting the urge to optimize the system rather than run it.
