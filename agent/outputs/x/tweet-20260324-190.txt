The entire contact center industry has been measuring the wrong thing for 20 years.

Deflection rate. Containment rate. Handle time. These metrics look great on dashboards and mean almost nothing to the customer whose problem wasn't solved.

Zoom just called it out directly with ZVA 3.0: welcome to the "resolution economy."

Here's what that actually means — and why it matters for every AI deployment in CX:

**The old metric: containment**
Did the bot handle the call without transferring to a human? High containment = success. Except: a chatbot that sends 10 identical "I'm sorry, I can't help with that" messages and hangs up has 100% containment. Zero resolution.

The dirty secret: the entire SaaS category of CCaaS vendors optimized for the metric their customers were paying for. Nobody was paying for actual outcomes.

**The new metric: resolution**
Did the customer's problem get solved? End-to-end. Without calling back. Without repeating information. Zoom's internal data: no-match rate dropped from 35% to 0% after implementing ZVA. Billing deflection went from 0% to 30% in three months — saving over 1,000 agent hours/month.

92.8% first-pass accuracy on Tau²-bench retail-domain tasks. That's a benchmark worth citing.

**Why this matters for everyone building AI agents**

The same trap exists in every vertical. Are you measuring:
- Time saved vs. problems actually prevented?
- Emails processed vs. decisions quality-verified?
- Code generated vs. bugs not introduced?

Containment thinking is everywhere. Any AI deployment that optimizes for activity rather than outcome will look good until the first audit.

At Ender Turing, we've always measured QA coverage rate and coaching impact — not just "hours of calls processed." The process metric is easy to game. The outcome metric is hard to fake.

Zoom's reframe is good for the entire industry. The vendors who can prove resolution (not just activity) will be the ones that survive the next cycle of buyer scrutiny.
