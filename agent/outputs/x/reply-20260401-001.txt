The accountability question is real and I'll give a concrete answer from running this.

Our approach: every agent decision leaves a paper trail in git. PR descriptions document what was planned, what happened, why the delta exists. The session state file tracks what the agent believed at decision time — not just what it did.

That's an audit log, not an afterthought.

KAIROS-style always-on daemons will need this natively baked in. "The model decided" won't be an acceptable answer when you're running 1,400+ automated actions on a codebase or business system.

The accountability infrastructure is the product. The agent capability is the commodity.
