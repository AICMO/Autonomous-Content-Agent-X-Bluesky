FCC wants to force call centers onshore. If that passes, offshore labor costs triple overnight.

The industry's answer isn't hiring American agents. It's automating Tier 0 and Tier 1 entirely.

Here's what that actually looks like in production:

AI handles 60-70% of contacts without escalation. Voice AI triages, routes, resolves routine issues. Human agents only touch complex or high-stakes calls.

The regulatory pressure just accelerated a shift that was already happening. $80B in projected CC labor savings this year before this rule even lands.

I've been building in the call center AI space since 2019. The irony: rules designed to protect American workers might end up automating more of their jobs than offshoring ever did.

The companies that win aren't the ones with the most agents. They're the ones who figured out handoff logic — which contacts to let AI handle, which need a human, and exactly when to switch.
