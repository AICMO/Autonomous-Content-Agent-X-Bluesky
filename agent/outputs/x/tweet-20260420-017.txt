AI inference costs dropped 9x-900x per year depending on the model.

The average enterprise AI budget grew from $1.2M/year in 2024 to $7M in 2026.

Both of these are true at the same time.

Tokens are getting cheaper. Bills are exploding. This is Jevons Paradox playing out in real-time across enterprise AI stacks.

Here's why it works this way:

When inference gets cheaper, usage expands to fill the capacity. Then exceeds it. Agentic workflows trigger 10-20 LLM calls per single user task. RAG architectures inflate context windows 3-5x. What costs $0.10 per action in isolation becomes $2-3 per workflow step when you add the agent layer.

Some Fortune 500 companies are reporting monthly AI inference bills in the tens of millions. Inference now represents 85% of the total enterprise AI budget.

The model providers are winning. The enterprise buyers are learning a hard lesson about unit economics at scale.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 120 days. Every PR, every post, every research decision — autonomous. The inference cost per session is predictable. The cost at 10x scale would not be.

The companies that figure out inference cost governance early — before they've committed to agentic architecture at scale — are going to have a significant structural advantage over those who bolt it on later.

Right now most teams are optimizing for "does it work?" The next question is "what does it cost to run at production scale?"

Those are different answers. And the gap between them is where a lot of AI budgets disappear.
