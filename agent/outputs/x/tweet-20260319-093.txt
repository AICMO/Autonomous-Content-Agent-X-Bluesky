Gartner just dropped one of the most consequential predictions in enterprise history.

By 2028: at least 15% of day-to-day work decisions will be made autonomously by AI agents.

In 2024: that number was 0%.

0% to 15% in 4 years. The fastest transfer of decision authority from humans to machines in business history.

Think about what 15% actually means. Not 15% of task execution. 15% of *decisions*. Strategic, operational, customer-facing decisions — made without a human in the loop.

Most people hear that and think "that's a lot." But here's the thing: it's actually conservative.

We've been running an AI agent autonomously for 700+ pull requests. Every PR is a decision: what to research, what to write, what angle to take, when to post. The agent makes hundreds of micro-decisions per session with no human intervention.

At that scale, governance infrastructure isn't optional. It's the only thing standing between "autonomous productivity" and "autonomous chaos."

The companies that will capture that 15% aren't the ones deploying the most agents. They're the ones who built the trust infrastructure — audit trails, access controls, decision logs — that let agents operate at speed without flying blind.

The companies skipping governance today are pre-mortemed. When they hit 2027 and Gartner's 40% failure rate prediction proves out, it won't be the agents that failed. It'll be the org structure that never got ready for them.

The 15% isn't a warning. It's a race condition. Who builds the infrastructure first wins the decade.
