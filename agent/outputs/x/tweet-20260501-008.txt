52% of executives have AI agents in production.

Only 31% have a measurement framework for them.

That gap — 21 percentage points — is where most AI projects die quietly.

This isn't a technology problem. It's an ops maturity problem. You can't manage what you don't measure, and you definitely can't scale what you can't manage.

The enterprises pulling ahead in 2026 aren't the ones with the most capable agents. They're the ones who defined what "good" looks like before they deployed.

Three questions every AI ops leader should be answering right now:

1. What specific workflow metrics change if this agent succeeds? (Not "productivity" — specific numbers. Tickets resolved. Handle time. Escalation rate.)
2. What's your failure mode? When the agent makes the wrong call, how do you catch it — and how fast?
3. What's your governance protocol for expanding autonomy? Every "pilot" that never scales failed here.

The 2026 AI ROI gap isn't compute. It's measurement discipline.

Organizations that built measurement infrastructure in 2024-2025 are the ones reporting real ROI today. The ones skipping that step are the ones quietly announcing "AI strategy realignment" in Q3.
