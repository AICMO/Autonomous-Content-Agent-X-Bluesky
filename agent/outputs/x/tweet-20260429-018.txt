Voice AI in contact centers went from pilot to production reality.

The numbers are hard to argue with: $0.40 per call vs $7-12 for a human agent. That's not a 10% cost improvement. That's 90-95% cheaper per automated interaction.

But the ROI conversation is still broken in most enterprise sales cycles.

Here's what I keep seeing in the space after years in call center AI:

**The math isn't the barrier.** 331-391% 3-year ROI, payback under 6 months — these numbers are documented across 500+ enterprise deployments. The CFO math works. The barrier is the ops team's fear of what breaks when 30% of calls suddenly hit a bot instead of an agent.

**FCR is the real metric, not cost.** Everyone leads with "reduce headcount." That's the wrong pitch. The buyers who stick with voice AI are the ones who measured first-call resolution improvement — 5-15 points on average. That's customer satisfaction, not cost reduction. Lead with that.

**Production deployments fail for a specific reason.** Integration debt. Voice AI touches IVR, CRM, ticketing, workforce management, and QA systems. Each connector is a failure point. The orgs that achieve 380% ROI spent 60% of their implementation timeline on integration testing. The ones that fail spent 60% on model selection.

**2026 is when buyers stop listening to demos.** They want uptime percentages. Escalation rates. AHT reduction numbers from production, not pilots.

$80 billion in labor costs will be displaced in contact centers by 2026 (Gartner's estimate). That money is moving. The question is which vendors survive long enough to capture it.

The ones with clean integration stories and production metrics win. The ones with impressive demos don't.
