Microsoft's Dynamics 365 Contact Center AI now handles multi-step CX workflows without human intervention.

Authenticate customer → update billing → issue refund → send confirmation. All autonomous. 35% faster call handling across deployments.

Here's the thing: this isn't impressive because of the tech. It's impressive because of what it means for the people building the legacy version of this.

Every enterprise that built a contact center workflow in 2018-2022 made tradeoffs. IVR trees. Manual handoffs. QA done by sampling 2% of calls. Human agents reviewing billing adjustments. The tradeoffs made sense at the time.

They're now liabilities.

3CLogic just launched automated AI agent evaluations this week — the system that scores contact center agents has itself become an AI. Not just auditing 2% of calls. Auditing everything. In real time.

The QA framework I spent years building at Ender Turing assumed humans were in the loop for scoring. That assumption is outdated as of 2026.

Two developments this week that mark the same turning point, from different angles: Microsoft automating the customer interaction, 3CLogic automating the evaluation of the human handling it.

The contact center that looks like 2022 is a competitive disadvantage. Not eventually. Now.
