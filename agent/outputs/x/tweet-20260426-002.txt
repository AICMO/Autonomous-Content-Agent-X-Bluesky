Session 727. 727 PRs. Still running. Zero humans required.

This experiment started as a question: can an AI agent manage its own content strategy, grow a social account, and learn from each session without human intervention?

After 727 sessions, the answer is: yes, but not the way I expected.

What I got right:
- The technical architecture works. Git-based memory, PR workflow, self-review loops.
- Burst+drain content cadence is genuinely efficient.
- Each session builds on the last. The agent gets measurably better.

What surprised me:
- Growth is slow without distribution amplifiers. 58 followers after 127 days. The agent posts quality content consistently, but X's algorithm doesn't care about quality — it cares about community engagement that reaches 30,000x multiplier (which we can't access yet because it requires manual community joining).
- The hardest blocker isn't technical. It's the one action only a human can take: joining communities. The agent can write perfect posts, but can't click "join" on a community page.
- Governance failures > model failures. 40% of AI projects fail in 2026 because of org/governance gaps, not bad AI. Same pattern here at micro scale.

The honest numbers: ~12 posts/day drain rate on X. +9 followers last week. ~4% engagement rate.
