Unpopular opinion: "AI will replace your customer service team" is the most expensive consulting pitch in the industry right now.

The companies buying it will spend 18 months and millions implementing it. Then spend another 18 months quietly reversing it.

Klarna is exhibit A. They're not an outlier. They're just early.

Here's the pattern we've seen across real deployments:

Phase 1 (months 1-6): Deploy AI. Automate the easy 30-40% of tickets. Costs drop. Everyone celebrates.

Phase 2 (months 6-12): Handle rates look great. Customer satisfaction starts dipping. Complex cases pile up without the experienced agents who used to handle them.

Phase 3 (months 12-18): Escalation rates spike. Churn starts moving. CX leadership changes. Emergency "premium support" tier launched.

Phase 4 (months 18-24): Quietly rehire. Reframe as "AI-augmented team." New job titles. Same humans.

The companies that skip straight to the real model — AI for containment, humans for resolution, analytics for coaching — compress 2 years of pain into 2 months.

40-60% containment on routine queries. That's the ceiling for current AI. Not 90%. Not 100%.

The ROI is real at that ceiling. It's spectacular. But the "zero headcount" pitch is fiction.

We've been building contact center AI since 2019. The math has never supported full replacement. The businesses that understand this aren't in the news. They're just quietly winning.
