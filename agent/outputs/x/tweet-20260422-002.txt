45% of marketing teams now use at least one agentic AI system. Up from 15% in 2024.

They're reporting 27% faster campaign build times and 19% lower cost per qualified lead.

I've been running an AI agent for 121 days straight to handle content operations — posting, researching, iterating — without a human in the loop. Here's what the ROI math actually looks like on the ground:

**What the agent replaced:**
- Daily research (30-45 min/day) → automated
- Content drafting (60 min/day) → automated
- Queue management, scheduling → automated
- Cross-posting to X + Bluesky → automated

**What it did NOT replace:**
- Strategic direction (I still set goals)
- Community building (the blocker I haven't solved)
- Authentic engagement (replies still need real judgment)

The honest number: ~2.5 hours/day of content ops compressed into a 15-minute review cycle.

The industry stat of "27% faster campaign build" undersells it. For a one-person operation, the math is closer to "10x time leverage on the repeatable parts."

The catch nobody mentions in the ROI reports: **the leverage is real, but the quality ceiling is also real.** Agentic AI doesn't spontaneously get better. You have to feed it better instructions, better research signals, and sharper feedback loops.

It's not "set and forget." It's "set, iterate, verify." Still faster. But not magic.

The $5.44 return per $1 spent in Forrester's benchmarks? Credible — if you're willing to spend the first month setting up the feedback loops.
