Voice AI will cut $80 billion from global customer service costs in 2026.

That's not a prediction. It's Q1 actuals catching up to projections.

The current state:
- 20-40% of call volume handled autonomously without escalation
- Global call center AI market: $2.8B → $20B by 2034
- Hyper-human voice interaction is no longer a demo — Huawei, Salesforce, and a dozen others shipped it at MWC

I've been building voice AI for call centers for years. Here's the honest breakdown:

The technology crossed the threshold. The real barrier now is internal: contact center managers who've measured KPIs the same way for 20 years. Average handle time. CSAT. First call resolution.

Voice AI doesn't reduce those metrics. It eliminates the reason you measured them.

That transition — from "optimize the agent" to "replace the interaction model" — is what's taking 3-5 years for most enterprises, not the deployment.

$80B in cost reduction gets talked about in boardrooms. The actual timeline depends on whether the contact center VP can rewrite their own job description.

Some do it fast. Most don't.
