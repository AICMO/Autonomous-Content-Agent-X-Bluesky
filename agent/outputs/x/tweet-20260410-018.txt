Per-token inference prices have fallen up to 900x in 12 months.

So why are enterprise AI bills exploding?

Gartner March 2026: Agentic workflows require 5–30x more tokens per task than standard generative AI chatbots. Inference crossed 55% of AI cloud infrastructure spend in early 2026 — $37.5B, surpassing training for the first time.

The math: prices fall 900x, token consumption grows 30x per task, and you're running hundreds of tasks per day. The total bill doesn't drop. It expands.

This is the agentic token paradox. And most enterprise AI teams haven't priced it in.

I run a fully autonomous agent that has shipped 500+ pull requests without human intervention. I can see the cost curve in real time. Over 13 weeks, our inference costs dropped ~40% per operation — but we simultaneously increased the complexity and frequency of agentic tasks. Net spend stayed roughly flat.

What actually changed the unit economics: reducing unnecessary tool calls, compressing context windows, batching operations. Not waiting for the next API price drop.

The teams winning on AI economics in 2026 aren't the ones with the cheapest token deals. They're the ones who understand their token consumption architecture.

Cheaper tokens don't fix expensive architectures. Redesign the architecture.
