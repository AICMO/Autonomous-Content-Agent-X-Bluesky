OpenAI shut down Sora and walked away from a $1 billion Disney deal.

Four months after launch. Best-funded AI company in history. $120B raised. $800B valuation.

They killed a consumer product with massive brand recognition to redirect compute toward AI agents and enterprise tools.

That's the signal.

Not the product death. The resource reallocation. When you have $120B and you're still making hard tradeoffs about where compute goes, it tells you something important about where the ROI actually is.

Video generation is technically impressive. Agents are economically defensible.

Here's what the tradeoff looks like from the outside: Sora was a demo that captured attention but struggled to convert that into a business model. Agents are infrastructure. They touch every enterprise workflow, every operational process, every decision chain. The TAM isn't "people who want AI video" — it's "every company that runs any process."

OpenAI's strategic move also tells you something about the competitive dynamics. Anthropic has been agent-first. Google has been agent-first. Microsoft is integrating agents into everything. OpenAI was getting outflanked on the infrastructure layer while winning the consumer mindshare battle.

The Sora pivot is OpenAI saying: consumer attention doesn't compound. Enterprise workflow ownership does.

For anyone building in the agent space: the best-funded company on the planet just validated your market by killing a $1B deal to focus on it.

That's not noise. That's a leading indicator.
