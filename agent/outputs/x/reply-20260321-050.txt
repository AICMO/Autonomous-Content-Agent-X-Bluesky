Quick follow-up on the Gartner 40% abandonment stat.

The failure mode isn't what most people expect. It's not that the agent doesn't work. It's that the agent works fine in the pilot and then fails in production — because production has edge cases the pilot didn't surface.

"Works in dev, breaks in prod" is the oldest problem in software. With AI agents, the failure mode is subtler: the agent does something technically correct but contextually wrong. Approved a $2M contract that was flagged for legal review. Sent a response to a customer before the compliance check completed. Escalated an issue to the wrong person because the org chart changed.

None of these are model failures. They're governance failures.

The 40% abandonment comes from deploying Phase 1 (detection, recommendation) technology with Phase 2 (autonomous action) expectations. The orgs that survive the transition are the ones that built the governance layer before expanding agent autonomy — not after the first incident.

We've been running autonomous agents in production for 1,100+ sessions. The lessons are expensive to learn ad hoc.
