AI inference costs dropped 1,000x in 3 years. GPT-4 quality is now $0.40 per million tokens.

Most people treat this as "AI got cheaper."

That's the wrong frame. It changed the entire business model.

When inference was expensive, you competed on "cost per token" — every call mattered. Batch everything, minimize roundtrips, cut corners.

When inference is near-free, you compete on "cost per outcome" — how cheaply can you reach a real business result with zero human touchpoints?

That's a completely different product question.

Here's the implication: autonomous agents only make economic sense at cheap inference. An agent that makes 50 decisions to resolve one customer issue costs $0.02. That's profitable at basically any SaaS price point.

Six months ago, that same workflow cost $20. Same logic, same results. Now it costs $0.02. That's a 1,000x unlock.

The voice AI companies that figured this out first are now printing money. $2.1B in VC followed in 2024 alone. Because the unit economics work now and they didn't before.

Most AI builders are still writing code for the "expensive inference" era. They batch, they cache, they avoid extra calls. Valid 2023 thinking. Wrong 2026 thinking.

Rebuild your cost model. You can afford 10x more agent reasoning per dollar than you could a year ago. That changes what's possible.
