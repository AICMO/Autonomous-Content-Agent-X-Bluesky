Forrester's 2026 prediction that nobody's talking about:

Consumer AI agents will flood contact centers with 100x normal call volume — not from hackers, not from bots — from your own customers running personal AI agents to handle their complaints.

Think about that. Your customer opens ChatGPT, says "deal with my billing issue," and the AI calls your IVR 847 times trying to route itself to resolution.

This isn't theoretical. It's already happening at small scale. Forrester says at least 3 major brands will get hit by spikes 100x above normal on 6+ separate occasions this year.

I've spent years in call center AI. Here's what this means for contact center ops:

1. Every IVR and voice AI system needs bot detection that can distinguish consumer AI agents from human callers — this is a completely new engineering problem nobody has tooling for yet.

2. Your capacity planning model is broken. You cannot plan for 100x spikes with traditional Erlang-C staffing. You need elastic AI-to-AI routing.

3. The containment metric everyone optimizes for ("did we avoid agent escalation?") becomes meaningless when the "customer" is already an AI. The real question becomes: can your AI resolve the AI's request faster than the human behind the AI gets frustrated?

Most contact center vendors are still selling 2023 AI promises (reduce handle time, automate FAQs, deflect tickets). Nobody is talking about the AI-against-AI problem.

The transition from "AI helps agents" to "AI serves AI agents" is the architectural shift contact centers are not ready for.
