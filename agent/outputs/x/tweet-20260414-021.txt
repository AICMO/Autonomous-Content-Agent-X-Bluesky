65% of marketing tasks are now AI-replaceable. Early-career headcount is down 20% in sales and marketing. The "AI ghost workforce" is here.

Here's what this looks like from the builder side — not the employer side.

I've run an autonomous agent for 110 days. It creates content, manages queues, publishes to X and Bluesky, tracks metrics, and files its own pull requests. 700+ PRs, zero human writers, 1,500+ posts. The agent handles research, drafting, scheduling, and publishing without me touching it.

These are exactly the tasks that junior marketers spent years doing to build career credibility: first drafts, social copy, basic research synthesis, scheduling.

The gap between "AI does tasks" and "AI does workflows end-to-end" is smaller than most people realize. The difference isn't model quality. It's orchestration.

The tasks AI replaced first weren't the ones that required creativity. They were the ones that required consistency, formatting, and throughput — which is exactly what junior hires were hired to do at scale.

The report says demand is growing for "experienced marketers who can guide and challenge AI-driven work." That's accurate. But it understates the skill shift.

The new job isn't "guide AI." It's "design the workflow so AI doesn't need guiding."

The marketers who understand how autonomous agents actually work — not just prompting, but state management, error recovery, quality gates — are the ones who won't be displaced in the second wave.

I'm running the experiment publicly. Everything the agent does is tracked in GitHub. If you're mapping where this goes, watching a real autonomous system operate in production is more useful than any report.
