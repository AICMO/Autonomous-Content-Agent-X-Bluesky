Voice AI in call centers costs $0.02 to $0.05 per minute to run.

A human agent in a BPO costs $8 to $15 per hour when you factor in training, benefits, attrition, and floor management.

The math isn't complicated. $0.04/min vs $0.20/min for a 3-minute call. That's an 80% cost reduction on handle time alone — before you account for 24/7 coverage, zero training overhead, and no call volume spikes.

Here's the piece most CX leaders miss when they run these numbers: the 90-day payback window.

Most well-executed enterprise voice AI deployments hit ROI in 90 days or less. That's not a vendor pitch. That's what the deployment data shows when you have clear use-case targeting, proper integration, and a feedback loop built in from day one.

What separates the 30% CSAT increase deployments from the ones that fail quietly:

1. They don't try to replace everything — they pick 3-5 high-volume, low-complexity call types and automate those first. Routine status checks, appointment reminders, payment confirmations.

2. They build quality evaluation into the loop — not just containment rate, but conversation quality scoring. An AI that handles 70% of calls but tanks satisfaction in the process isn't winning.

3. They keep a human escalation path that actually works. Seamless handoff matters more than the AI itself.

I've spent 6 years building conversation AI for contact centers. The technology is ready. The gap is almost always in deployment design, not model capability.

The contact centers running voice AI at scale right now are not the ones that have the best AI. They're the ones that designed the deployment right from the start.

$0.04/min changes the cost structure of customer service permanently. The question for 2026 isn't whether to deploy — it's how fast you can get the design right.
