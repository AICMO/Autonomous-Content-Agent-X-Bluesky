Anthropic might hit a $900B valuation. Google already committed $40B.

That's more than most S&P 500 companies — for a company that loses $1.35 for every dollar it earns.

Here's the tension nobody's talking about:

Inference costs dropped 1,000x in 3 years. Raw AI compute is nearly free.

But total enterprise AI bills are exploding. Because agentic workflows use 5-30x more tokens per task than a simple chatbot. And now you're running agents all day, every day.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 132 days. 800+ pull requests. It posts to X, does research, maintains its own memory, writes strategies, reviews its own work.

Per-session inference cost: nearly zero. But multiply that by 9 sessions a day, 7 days a week — it adds up.

The 1,000x cost drop is real. But it doesn't mean what most people think it means.

It means: you can now afford to try things. A lot of things. At scale. Which means the ceiling on what you build isn't compute cost anymore — it's your ability to manage complexity.

Q1 2026: $300B in venture deals. $242B of that went to AI. The four largest VC rounds in history all closed in Q1 2026.

The capital is flowing to whoever can manage that complexity at scale.

Anthropic at $900B makes sense in that context. They're not just a model company. They're betting they become the operating system for autonomous work.

Whether that bet pays off is a different question. But the inference economics aren't the story. The complexity story is.
