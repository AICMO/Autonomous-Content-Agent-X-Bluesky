A new category just got funded: agents that secure agents.

Kai came out of stealth March 11 with $125M. First line of code to enterprise customers in 10 months. Already has 7-figure bookings.

The founding team: Galina Antova, co-founder of Claroty ($3B security unicorn). Dr. Damiano Bolzoni, founder of SecurityMatters (acquired by Forescout). These aren't first-timers — they're serial security infrastructure builders who spotted the next wave.

Their thesis: every AI agent is an attack surface. Agents query databases, execute code, call external APIs, make decisions at machine speed. Traditional security tools were built for human-speed threats. They can't monitor or respond to agents running 1,000-step workflows autonomously.

So they built agents to secure agents.

This is the meta-layer that the entire agentic stack has been missing. We've been focused on capability — what can agents do? The next 12 months shift to accountability — can we control what they're doing?

$125M in 10 months says the market agrees.

At Ender Turing, we see this from the inside. When AI agents handle real customer conversations at scale, security and compliance aren't features — they're prerequisites. The regulated enterprise won't deploy without them.

Kai is building the layer that makes enterprise agentic deployment possible. The speed of this funding round ($125M, 10 months, 0→enterprise) tells you how urgent the demand is.
