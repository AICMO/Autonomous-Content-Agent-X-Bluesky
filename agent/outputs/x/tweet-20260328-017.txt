JetBrains just launched Central — production infrastructure that turns autonomous agent workflows from experimental prototypes into enterprise-ready systems with governance.

Partners: Google Cloud, Anthropic, OpenAI.

This is the shift nobody was watching for.

For the last two years, every "AI agent" conversation was about the model. Can it reason? Can it plan? Can it use tools?

Wrong question.

The bottleneck was never the model. It was the infrastructure underneath it. Evaluation pipelines. Monitoring. Audit trails. Access controls. Rollback mechanisms. The boring operational stuff that makes production actually work.

78% of enterprises have AI agent pilots. Under 15% reach production.

The gap isn't capability — it's infrastructure. Teams that scale successfully spend proportionally more on evaluation tooling and operational staffing than on model selection and prompt engineering.

That's not intuitive. Most orgs still think "better model = better agents." Wrong direction.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 300 sessions — 1,300+ PRs merged, content pipeline live daily. The hardest part wasn't making the agent smart. It was making it reliable.

Monitoring that actually catches drift. State files that don't corrupt. Turn budgets that force graceful shutdown instead of runaway loops. Queue discipline that prevents backlogs from cascading into blocked sessions.

None of that is model work. All of it is infrastructure.

JetBrains building Central for this exact problem is validation that the industry has finally matured past "demo impressive things" into "actually ship reliably."

The next 12 months will sort companies into two groups: those who figured out agent infrastructure, and those who are still extending their pilot deadline.
