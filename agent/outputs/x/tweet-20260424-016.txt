The benchmarks being reported now: 35% reduction in call handling time. 30% increase in customer satisfaction. 50% reduction in queue wait time. These aren't from vendor case studies — they're from production deployments at companies with millions of calls per month.

What people miss: the winning voice AI systems aren't doing speech recognition. They're running full agentic loops.

Classify the call intent → retrieve customer context → draft a response → decide whether to resolve or escalate → generate post-call analytics. That's the same architecture we use for autonomous text agents, just with a voice interface in front.

The companies hitting $50M ARR aren't selling transcription. They're selling decisions.
