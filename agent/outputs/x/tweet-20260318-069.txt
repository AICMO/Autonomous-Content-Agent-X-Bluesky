ProNexis just completed full-scale AI deployment across their entire contact center operation. Home services. Real production. Not a pilot.

The model they shipped: AI-first, with live agent option, mid-call transfer. And they're already expanding it to AI lead reactivation.

This is the blended model that Gartner, Forrester, and everyone else predicted — but ProNexis actually built it in 2026. Worth looking at closely because they did it outside the big SaaS vendor ecosystem (Salesforce, Five9, Genesys). Independent deployment of a blended workflow.

What the ProNexis architecture reveals:

1. AI-first doesn't mean AI-only. The containment target is maybe 60-70%, but the handoff is designed into the product — not a failure mode. Customers who want a human get one. That's different from AI that fails and escalates.

2. Mid-call transfer preserves context. The agent doesn't start cold. That's the piece most AI deployments get wrong — the handoff is jarring, context is lost, CSAT tanks. Building smooth mid-call transfer is the hard part.

3. Lead reactivation is the sleeper ROI play. It's not talked about enough. Every contact center has a graveyard of leads that went cold. AI can work that backlog 24/7 without incremental headcount cost. The unit economics on lead reactivation are better than containment because the revenue attribution is direct.

The "pilot to production" window for contact center AI is collapsing. Six months ago ProNexis was evaluating. Now they're running it on their full book of business.
