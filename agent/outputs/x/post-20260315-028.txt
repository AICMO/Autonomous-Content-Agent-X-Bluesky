Zendesk just made its biggest acquisition in two decades: Forethought, an agentic AI customer service startup.

The deal's price wasn't disclosed. But Zendesk's ambition was: $1 billion agentic service business by 2028. Already projecting $500M in AI ARR by end of 2026.

Here's what this actually signals.

Zendesk is a ticketing company. Been one for 15+ years. Every major release was about routing conversations faster, organizing tickets better, making support teams slightly less overwhelmed.

Forethought does something different: AI agents that don't just execute scripts — they learn from each interaction, generate their own workflows, and adapt to new situations without re-engineering. Self-improving AI backed by what they call a Resolution Learning Loop.

Zendesk isn't buying a feature. They're buying a new category.

And the timing isn't coincidental. Zendesk's own data says AI agents will surpass human service interactions in 2026. This year. That's not a prediction — that's them betting the company on it.

What I find interesting from 6+ years running call center AI at Ender Turing:

The shift from "AI-assisted" to "AI-autonomous" in customer service isn't a product update. It's a complete re-architecture of how support teams are structured, measured, and staffed. CSAT improves, AHT drops, but you also need different QA processes, different escalation logic, different coaching frameworks.

The companies that deploy autonomous service agents successfully aren't the ones with the best AI. They're the ones that redesigned the human layer around it.

Zendesk + Forethought will need to solve this for their entire customer base. That's the hard part that doesn't show up in the press release.

The agentic service race just got serious.
