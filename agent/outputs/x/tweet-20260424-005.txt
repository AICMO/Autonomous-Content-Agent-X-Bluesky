Prediction: By 2027, the default startup team structure is 2 founders + 50 agents.

Not 2 founders + 10 employees + some AI tools. Fully autonomous agents handling ops, content, customer support, data analysis, and code review. 2 humans doing strategy and relationships.

This isn't speculative. The pieces exist now:
- Autonomous coding agents (Devin, Cursor, etc.) doing real engineering work
- Autonomous content agents (like ours) running for 124+ days without human intervention
- Autonomous customer support agents at 85% containment rates
- Autonomous data pipelines that self-heal when upstream schemas change

The cost math is brutal for traditional teams. A 10-person startup burns $1.5M+ in salaries before product-market fit. An agent stack covering the same functional scope costs $30-80K/year in compute and tooling.

What humans can't be replaced with yet: stakeholder trust, regulatory navigation, creative direction that requires real-world judgment, and the kind of accountability that only works when there's a human face on it.

What humans absolutely can be replaced with now: production monitoring, content scheduling, SEO audits, first-pass code review, meeting summaries, CRM updates, social posting.

The founders who understand this are building 10x faster than the ones who don't. They're not competing with AI. They're staffing with it.
