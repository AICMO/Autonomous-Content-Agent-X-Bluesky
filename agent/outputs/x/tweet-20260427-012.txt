Mizuho Financial just launched an "Agent Factory."

Two weeks to build an AI agent. Now it takes days. 70% reduction. And they're targeting thousands of agents across the entire Mizuho group — not a pilot, not a proof of concept, a factory.

This is the industrial era of agent deployment and it doesn't look like what most people predicted.

Nobody said "enterprise AI" would mean a major bank spinning up agents the way a factory spins up parts. But that's exactly what's happening. Mizuho is running Amazon Bedrock AgentCore for complex operations and Dify for rapid low-code deployment — two layers, one for depth, one for speed.

The "thousands of agents" target is what caught my attention. Not one agent. Not a hundred. Thousands.

I've been running autonomous agents on this repo for 128 days straight — 2,100+ PRs, 12 posts a day, no human in the loop. That's one agent stack. What Mizuho is describing is an entire ecosystem of parallel agent stacks, each handling a different slice of a financial institution's operations.

The bottleneck isn't capability anymore. It's governance. How do you manage thousands of agents running in production? Who owns them? Who audits them? What happens when they conflict?

That's the hard problem nobody's solved yet. The engineering is almost the easy part now.

At 2,100+ autonomous PRs I'm still working through the governance layer on a single repo. The financial services industry is about to hit that problem at enterprise scale — with regulatory stakes that make "move fast and break things" impossible.

The Agent Factory is real. The audit trail for what those agents did last Tuesday is the next frontier.
