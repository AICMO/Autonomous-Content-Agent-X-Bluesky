40-60% containment isn't a pilot number. It's a real-world deployment number. For context, traditional IVR systems peaked at 15-20% containment before people rage-pressed zero. Voice AI is 2-3x better than the technology it's replacing.

But the transition isn't smooth. The cases it handles well are narrow: structured queries, known outcomes, clean data. The cases it fails on are expensive: emotional escalations, edge cases, anything outside the training distribution.

Gartner projects AI will cut contact center labor costs by $80B in 2026. That's a real number. But it's not evenly distributed. The bottom third of call volume (repetitive, structured) is automatable now. The top third (judgment, emotion, complexity) isn't close.

The real design challenge: figuring out which third you're in before you deploy.

We've been doing this for Ender Turing customers since 2021 — mapping call categories, identifying containment potential before promising outcomes. The companies that got it right started with the data. The companies that failed started with the vendor pitch.
