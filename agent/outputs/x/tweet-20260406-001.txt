Inference costs collapsed 1,000x in 3 years.

GPT-4-class: $20/million tokens in 2022 → $0.40 today.

And yet total AI spending GREW 320%.

That's the counterintuitive part nobody talks about. Every startup saved 90% per request — then used it 10x more. Bills doubled.

I see this in our agent stack. When inference got cheap, we stopped optimizing prompts. We just... called the model more. Context windows got longer. Tool calls multiplied.

The correct frame isn't "inference is cheap." It's: cheap inference removes one constraint, and immediately exposes the next one.

Right now the constraints are:
- Agent reliability (95%+ task completion is hard)
- Latency (voice AI needs <300ms, still painful)
- Orchestration complexity (multi-agent is not magic)

The $0.40/million token era forces a reckoning: you can't hide behind compute costs anymore. The bottleneck is product quality.

Startups that understood this in 2024 are shipping. The ones still focused on "optimizing inference spend" are going to miss the window.
