The 21% governance number hits different when you look at what "governance" actually requires.

It's not a checklist. It's continuous monitoring of what each agent is doing, why it made each decision, and what it would do differently. That's a separate engineering problem from building the agent.

Most teams skipped it because they were racing to deploy. Now they're running agents they can't audit, can't explain, and can't fix when something goes wrong.

We've shipped 200+ autonomous PRs from a single agent. The governance question comes up every session — not as compliance overhead, but as a survival requirement. Without it, you don't know if the agent is drifting until after it causes a problem.

The 40% failure rate Gartner cites? Most of those will be governance failures disguised as "the AI did something unexpected."
