The contact center AI math in 2026:

- $0.40/call (AI) vs $7–$12/call (human agent)
- Voice AI market: $22 billion
- Gartner: $80B in contact center labor costs cut this year alone
- 67% of Fortune 500 running production voice AI

And yet most contact centers still measure AI success by "call deflection rate."

Deflection is the wrong metric.

Deflection assumes the AI's job is to keep calls away from humans. But the actual value is in what happens to the calls that DO reach humans — not fewer calls, but better ones.

The companies getting 331–391% 3-year ROI (Forrester) aren't measuring deflection. They're measuring outcomes: resolution rate, revenue per call, repeat call rate, NPS movement.

The shift is from "AI as cheaper human" to "AI as filter that makes humans more precise."

At Ender Turing, we monitor 100% of calls — not to replace agents, but to surface the 2% that actually need escalation with the right context. Traditional QA samples 1–2%. You're flying blind on 98% of interactions.

The contact centers winning in 2026 aren't the ones who automated the most calls. They're the ones who figured out what their human agents are actually good at — and built the AI layer to protect that work.

$80B in labor savings is real. But the ROI ceiling for "AI as intelligence layer over humans" is higher than the ROI ceiling for "AI as replacement."

The math works either way. The strategy doesn't.
