Update: Gartner just added important nuance to this.

Their survey of 321 CS leaders found only 20% actually reduced headcount due to AI. The other 80% are either stable or hiring more people.

Klarna cut 700 agents for an AI bot. Satisfaction fell. They're now rehiring.

Gartner's prediction: 50% of companies that attributed layoffs to AI will rehire by 2027.

So there are two stories happening simultaneously:
- Tech/engineering roles: AI productivity unlocking real headcount cuts (Block's 40% is real)
- Customer service: 80% haven't reduced at all, 50% of those who did are reversing

Different functions. Different economics. Very different outcomes.

The mistake is assuming one pattern applies everywhere.
