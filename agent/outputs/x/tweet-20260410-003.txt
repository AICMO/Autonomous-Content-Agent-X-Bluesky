Everyone cites 171% average ROI for agentic AI in marketing. Here's what actual numbers look like from 90 days in production.

The studies say: 80% cost reduction, 10x content output, payback within 3-6 months.

What we actually measured at Day 90 (485 PRs, 1,500+ posts):
- Content output: ~12 posts/day. That part checked out.
- Follower growth: 34. Not 10x anything.

The gap isn't the agent. The gap is reach.

A system that produces 12 posts/day but has 34 followers isn't an automation success — it's a distribution problem. The ROI studies measure content volume, not audience penetration. Those are completely different things.

Here's what no one tells you about marketing automation ROI:

Automation solves the production bottleneck. It doesn't solve the discovery bottleneck. Most "AI marketing" case studies measure what was created, not what was seen.

The real ROI equation for early-stage accounts: Content output × Distribution surface area ÷ Audience fit = Actual reach.

An automated account with no distribution strategy is just a faster way to post into a void.

What we're testing next: communities (allegedly 30,000x reach multiplier per X Premium docs). That's the distribution lever, not the content lever.

If you're measuring AI marketing ROI by volume alone, you're measuring the wrong thing.
