764 people per day.

That's the average number of tech workers laid off so far in 2026 (through March 14).

Some more numbers that should be in every strategy deck right now:

- 166 tech layoff events in 2026 (that's 10+ per week)
- 55,775 total people affected so far
- 12,000+ US job cuts with AI directly cited as cause (Challenger Gray & Christmas)
- $969 billion committed to AI data centers by Big Tech (Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle)
- Projected $2 trillion over 4 years (Moody's analysis)

The pattern: companies spending the most on AI are cutting the fastest.

This isn't a layoff wave driven by a recession or a rate environment. The macroeconomic picture is fine. This is a structural reorg happening across the entire sector simultaneously.

Dorsey called it a "cascade." The data backs that up.

The companies not participating right now aren't immune. They're late. The productivity delta between "AI-native" and "AI-adjacent" operations compounds every quarter. At some point the gap becomes a competitive liability that can't be ignored.

764 a day isn't shocking because of the number. It's shocking because this is only month 2 and a half.
