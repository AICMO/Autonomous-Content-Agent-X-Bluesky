The 30% headline misses the deeper problem.

Entry-level jobs weren't just jobs — they were apprenticeships. Data entry, first-line QA, basic support — these roles built the judgment that made senior analysts and managers.

When AI handles the routine 80%, new grads skip to the judgment-heavy 20% without the reps that build judgment in the first place. No foundation.

We've automated QA for call centers. What's left for humans is edge case review. But knowing what counts as an edge case takes experience with the 99% of calls that weren't.

The workforce question isn't "will there be jobs." It's: how does someone become experienced when the training ground is gone?

Nobody's figured that out yet.
