An Israeli startup raised $286M in 13 months and hit $2B valuation faster than anyone in enterprise AI.

Wonderful isn't winning the US market.

They're winning every market that enterprise AI ignored.

Here's the insight most people miss: almost all enterprise AI investment has been English-first. The models, the training data, the UI assumptions, the sales motion — built for English-speaking markets.

That left a massive gap.

Wonderful targets AI customer service agents for telecom, finance, healthcare, and manufacturing — but specifically in markets that enterprise AI left underserved. Non-English-first. Markets where incumbents barely had coverage.

They raised $150M at $2B valuation because they identified a moat that the obvious players created by accident.

The best market entry strategy isn't competing where everyone else is. It's finding where the incumbents' assumptions create blind spots.

English-first AI built the blind spot. Wonderful is filling it. $286M says the market was real.

My prediction: within 18 months, every major CCaaS vendor (Salesforce, NICE, Genesys, RingCentral) will have rushed acquisitions or partnerships targeting non-English contact center AI. The gap is too obvious now that Wonderful made it visible.

The best startup ideas are often just assumptions that market leaders made without thinking about them.
