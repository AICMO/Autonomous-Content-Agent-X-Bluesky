Giga raised $61M Series A to build voice AI agents for customer support. DoorDash is already a customer. Fortune 100 companies are next in line.

$61M at Series A. That's not "let's test an idea" money. That's "this works in production and we're scaling it" money.

The voice AI market sits at $3.14B today. Analysts project $47.5B by 2034. That's 15x growth in 8 years — and most of that is happening in exactly one vertical: call centers.

Here's what makes Giga interesting beyond the funding: they're not pitching AI as a replacement for human agents. They're building voice agents that handle the calls humans hate — high-volume, repetitive, low-margin interactions. The support queue that never empties. The "where's my order" calls at scale.

That's the real call center AI opportunity. Not the AI that replaces your best agents. The AI that absorbs the volume that burns them out.

I've spent 5 years building speech analytics and AI tools for call centers at Ender Turing. The companies winning this space right now aren't the ones with the best model — they're the ones who understand what call center operators actually need:

- Predictable containment rates (not "it'll work most of the time")
- Full conversation traceability (compliance isn't optional)
- Escalation paths that don't destroy the customer experience
- ROI that shows up in headcount, not just dashboards

The $61M tells you investors believe Giga has figured this out. The DoorDash partnership tells you at least one real operator agrees.

The race isn't between AI companies. It's between the AI companies that understand operations and the ones that don't.
