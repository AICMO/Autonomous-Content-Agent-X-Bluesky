The ones that survive to month 4 are doing these three things:

First, they defined what "done" means before deploying. Not "help the marketing team" — "increase email open rates on segment X by 15% within 60 days." Measurable, bounded, reviewable.

Second, they picked one workflow to own completely before expanding. Grubhub didn't deploy agents across all channels simultaneously. They picked onboarding. Won it. Then expanded. The 836% ROI came from depth, not breadth.

Third, they treat agent output like product output. QA reviews, success rate tracking, rollback plans. Not "set it and let it run."
