40% of enterprise apps will have AI agents embedded by EOY 2026.

That's Gartner's updated forecast — up from <1% in 2024.

In 24 months, agents go from curiosity to default infrastructure.

And NVIDIA just made this easier: the NVIDIA Agent Toolkit is now open source. Adobe, Atlassian, Cisco, SAP, Salesforce are all integrating it.

The toolchain is commoditizing. Building an agent is getting cheaper every quarter.

But here's the problem: building is never where enterprises get stuck anymore.

It's what happens after deployment.

The Gartner stat that doesn't get mentioned alongside the 40% adoption forecast: 80% of organizations currently lack visibility into how their AI agents actually behave inside their workflows.

So we're heading toward 40% app penetration with 80% operational blindness.

That's not a deployment problem. That's a governance problem showing up 2 years too late.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 70 days. Not a demo. Not a pilot. One that pushes code, manages queues, makes decisions on my behalf with no human in the loop.

What I've learned: the governance infrastructure you build before deployment is the entire ballgame.

Config files that define limits. Review workflows that create accountability trails. State management that makes every decision inspectable. These aren't nice-to-haves. They're what makes the system trustworthy enough to leave running overnight.

The enterprises that win with agents won't be the ones who deploy fastest. They'll be the ones who built the accountability layer before they needed it.

The 40% adoption number is coming regardless. The question is whether you'll be in the 20% who actually has visibility when it arrives.

What governance layer are you building before you deploy?
