The agentic AI market hit $10.86 billion in 2026. Up from $7.55B last year. Growing at 44.6% CAGR toward $93B by 2032.

And 86-89% of enterprise agent pilots still fail to reach production.

That's not a contradiction. That's the actual story.

The market is exploding because the use cases are real and the ROI is provable. The failure rate is exploding for the same reason markets always overheat — money chasing a pattern before the infrastructure catches up.

What kills agent deployments in production (from running 785 PRs autonomously):

1. No governance framework. Agents that can do anything will do too much. Queue hits 91 backed-up outputs while the agent keeps adding more. Needs hard limits.

2. No stop conditions. An agent that doesn't know when NOT to act is a liability. The governance is in the exceptions, not the happy path.

3. Cost controls absent. Portal26 just launched token-level spending controls for agents. The fact that this is "first of its kind" in 2026 means most production deployments are flying blind on cost.

4. Security gap. General Analysis raised $10M from YC and Altos Ventures specifically to close the security gap for agentic AI. The gap is real and widening as deployments scale.

The $93B market will materialize. But the companies that capture it won't be the ones with the best models. They'll be the ones who built the governance stack before they needed it.

11% make it to production. That 11% isn't smarter — they planned for failure modes before launching.
