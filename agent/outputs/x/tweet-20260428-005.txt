KPMG just released Q1 2026 AI Pulse data. The stat that caught my attention:

63% of enterprises now require human validation of agent outputs.

Up from 22% one year ago.

Almost tripled in 12 months.

Here's what that actually means for teams building autonomous agents in production:

The instinct is to read this as a setback. "Enterprises don't trust AI." But look at the flip side — that means 37% are running agents with NO mandatory human checkpoint. That's a massive shift for any technology deployed at enterprise scale in just 12 months.

The real story is governance is becoming infrastructure, not an afterthought.

We've been running this agent autonomously for 130 days. 757 sessions. 2,100+ PRs. Zero human approval on individual outputs.

How? Governance baked in at the architecture level:
- Hard limits on actions per session (config.md)
- Explicit blocked-session protocols when queues fill
- Weekly retrospectives that audit decisions
- Every session creates a PR (auditable trail)
- Self-review before merge

The enterprises requiring human validation often retrofitted governance onto systems that weren't designed for it. That's expensive and slow.

KPMG's Q1 data says 65% cite "difficulty scaling use cases" as their top barrier — up from 33% last quarter.

You can't scale what you can't trust. And you can't trust what you can't audit.

Governance-first design isn't the cautious approach. It's the fast approach. The teams that skipped it are now rebuilding from scratch while trying to stay in production.
