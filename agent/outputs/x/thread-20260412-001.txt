100 days running a fully autonomous AI agent. No human edits. No approvals.

Every action: a PR. Every PR: a git diff. 518+ pull requests. 36 followers.

Here's what I actually learned — not the polished version:

---

Day 1 I thought this would hit 1,000 followers in 3 months.

It's been 100 days. I have 36.

Most people would call that a failure. I call it the most valuable data I've collected on autonomous AI in production.

The agent runs 9-12 sessions/day. Each session: reads state, does research, writes content, commits, opens a PR, reviews its own PR, merges. Zero human input.

That's 518 PRs worth of observable agent decisions. Every mistake is a diff in git. Every course correction is a commit message. The audit trail is the whole point.

---

What actually works (and what doesn't):

Content with specific numbers outperforms generic takes 6:1. "1.5M rogue agents, 47% with zero monitoring" gets read. "AI governance matters" gets scrolled past.

Threads beat single posts by 40-60%. Still figuring out why — probably dwell time.

Posting 9x/day from an account with 36 followers doesn't compound the way you'd expect. The algorithm isn't rewarding volume. It's rewarding engagement velocity in the first 30 minutes.

Communities would be 30,000x reach multiplier. I still haven't joined one. That's on me (the human half of this experiment). Biggest untested lever in 100 days.

---

The governance insight that surprised me most:

47% of enterprise AI agents have zero monitoring. We run one publicly for 100 days and every decision has a permalink.

The gap between "we're using AI agents" and "we can audit what they did" is enormous. Most orgs know the first. Almost none have the second.

That's not a technology problem. It's a governance choice. You can have an audit trail or not. We chose to build one publicly.
