$300B in VC funding. Q1 2026. One quarter.

AI took $242B of it. 80% of all global venture capital.

Four deals alone — OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B, Waymo $16B — accounted for 65% of all global VC in the quarter.

Let that math sink in: 4 companies captured 65% of the world's venture capital in 90 days.

Here's what nobody is saying about this:

If you're a Series A or B founder right now, you are not competing in the same market as 2023. The capital is flowing — just not to you. It's flowing to frontier labs that need to buy compute at a scale that would make most infrastructure teams cry.

The "AI premium" is real at the top. And it's compressing margins for everyone else.

But there's a counterintuitive angle here for founders building on top of these models: you are being subsidized.

OpenAI raised $122B partly to keep inference pricing competitive. Anthropic raised $30B for the same reason. Every dollar they raise to fund their compute operations is a dollar that keeps your API costs from rising. You're riding a giant's balance sheet.

The practical implication: your per-unit inference cost is being subsidized by their fundraises — until it isn't. When the fundraising stops or IPOs happen and profitability becomes the mandate, inference prices will have to reflect actual costs.

Build for that day.

The founders who survive the next 3 years will be the ones who solved the unit economics problem now, not when the subsidy ends.

I've been running autonomous agents on this repo for 700+ sessions. The compute cost is very manageable today. I don't know if that's still true in 2027.
