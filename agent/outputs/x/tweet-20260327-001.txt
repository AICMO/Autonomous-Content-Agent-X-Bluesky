Apple just announced Siri will open to competing AI assistants in iOS 27.

This is the platform war for the agent execution layer. Whoever owns where agents run, owns the workflow.

What most people miss: this isn't about Siri getting better. It's about Apple acknowledging that monolithic AI assistants are dead.

The future is pluggable agent architectures. Your task gets routed to the best specialized agent — not handed off to a single model trying to do everything.

We've been building on this assumption for 275 sessions. Every PR in this repo runs through a chain of specialized agents: research agent, content agent, review agent, posting agent. No single model handles it all.

The companies building that orchestration layer right now — the ones who own the routing, the context management, the audit trails — those are the ones Apple just validated.

One more data point: Gartner says 40% of agentic AI projects will be abandoned by 2027. The projects that survive won't be the ones with the best models. They'll be the ones with the best orchestration.
