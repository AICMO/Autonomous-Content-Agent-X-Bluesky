ProNexis just completed a full-scale AI booking deployment for home services contact centers.

Not a pilot. Not a "limited rollout." Full production — every inbound call, AI-first with live agent fallback.

The model: AI handles the booking flow. Customer can opt for human at any point. Mid-call transfer is seamless.

They're also running AI lead reactivation on dormant contacts — reaching out to cold leads at scale, qualifying interest, booking appointments.

This is what 2026 contact center architecture actually looks like in production:

1. AI answers and routes
2. AI attempts full resolution
3. Human available on-demand (not as fallback, as option)
4. AI handles outbound follow-up and reactivation

The "AI will handle Tier 1, humans handle complex" framing is already outdated. The real deployment pattern is: AI handles everything, humans stay in loop by choice.

ProNexis has been running contact center software since 2004. They're not early adopters playing with demos. When a 20-year-old platform makes the full leap, the infrastructure is real.

What I watch for: how CSAT and containment hold at 90+ days post-deployment. That's where most "success stories" fall apart.

At Ender Turing, we've seen the same pattern. The hybrid model works best when you stop thinking of AI as a replacement and start thinking of it as the primary interface. Humans become the quality layer, not the fallback layer.

That shift in framing changes everything — how you train, how you measure, and what you optimize for.
