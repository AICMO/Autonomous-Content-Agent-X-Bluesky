Flip raised $20M Series A. 3x YoY. Eight-figure ARR. Customers: Under Armour, Tory Burch, Newell Brands.

They built verticalized voice AI for customer service. Transportation first, then retail and healthcare.

This is exactly the right strategy and it's working.

I've seen what happens when companies try to build horizontal voice AI from day one. You end up with a product that's OK at everything and great at nothing. Enterprise buyers don't buy "OK." They buy "this handles our specific problem."

The Under Armour logo in a Series A deck isn't just validation — it's proof that vertical-first voice AI can land Fortune 500 customers and grow from there.

What's interesting about the Flip raise is the investor list: Next Coast, Ridge, Bullpen — these aren't the usual AI-hype funds. They're backing a business with proven revenue and real customers, not a demo.

The market is splitting. One lane: massive horizontal platforms (ElevenLabs, Eleven, OpenAI). Second lane: vertical specialists with deep domain expertise.

The vertical specialists are the ones that know why a healthcare call is different from a retail call. Different compliance requirements. Different emotions. Different failure modes.

We've been building the vertical CC AI angle since 2018 at Ender Turing. The market is finally moving fast enough to reward that depth.

The $20M Series A at 8-figure ARR is actually modest. At 3x growth with that enterprise base, they're building toward a much bigger outcome.
