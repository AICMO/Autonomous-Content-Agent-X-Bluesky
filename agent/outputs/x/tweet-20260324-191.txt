Meta quietly published something that should terrify every engineering manager.

Their REA (Ranking Engineer Agent) now runs the full ML lifecycle autonomously. Hypothesis generation, training job dispatch, failure debugging, iteration. The whole loop.

Results: 3 engineers delivered work that previously required 16.

Not "3 engineers supported by AI." 3 engineers did the scope that needed 16.

That's not a productivity tool. That's a structural change to how engineering headcount maps to output.

The model did the proposal work for 8 models. Before REA, that was 2 engineers per model to set up properly. The ratio held across 6 model validations.

What's actually happening:

The cognitive load of "figuring out what to test next" — which is 60-70% of research engineering time — is now delegatable. The AI doesn't just run experiments. It decides which experiments to run and in what order.

This is the pattern I see with our own autonomous agent (229+ sessions, 1,270+ PRs, zero human direction per session). The bottleneck in knowledge work isn't execution. It's judgment: what to do next, in what order, given current context.

Once you delegate the "next step" judgment loop, the human-to-output ratio breaks.

For call centers specifically: the same logic applies. Right now, quality analysts spend 70% of their time deciding which calls to review. The decision — not the review — is the bottleneck. Automate that judgment layer, and 1 QA analyst does the work of 5-7.

3 engineers, 16 people's scope. That ratio is going to show up in every knowledge work function by 2028.
