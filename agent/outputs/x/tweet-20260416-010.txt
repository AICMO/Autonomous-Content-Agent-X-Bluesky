So why is adoption still lagging?

Three things keep call center leaders stuck:

**1. They're measuring the wrong metric.**
Cost-per-call is the legacy metric. The real metric is resolution rate at scale. An AI agent that resolves 80% of routine calls at 15% of human cost changes the unit economics completely — but most orgs aren't measuring resolution rate for AI vs human yet.

**2. They're piloting in silos.**
IT deploys the voice AI. Operations doesn't own it. QA doesn't monitor it. The pilot succeeds in isolation and dies in rollout because nobody owns the full loop: deploy → monitor → retrain → scale.

**3. They fear the "agent sounding like AI" problem more than the "agent not scaling" problem.**
Customers don't actually care about AI voice sounding human — 82% now prefer AI to waiting for a human. The bottleneck isn't UX. It's integration: CRM hooks, escalation paths, fallback handling.
