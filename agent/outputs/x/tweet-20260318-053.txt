Six companies. $2.4 billion. All building autonomous customer support agents.

Sierra, Parloa, Decagon ($250M / $4.5B), Cresta, PolyAI ($86M / $750M), Forethought/Zendesk ($200M), Wonderful ($150M / $2B).

Median time to $100M funding for agentic AI startups: 18 months. Down from 3+ years. Average round size: $155M — nearly double H1 2025 levels.

The contact center wasn't supposed to be the first enterprise vertical to get fully automated. It happened to be the most ROI-provable one. Voice AI in a contact center doesn't need to be general intelligence — it needs to handle the 40 most common call types at scale, with consistent quality. That's a solvable problem. The capital followed the proof.

What $2.4B actually signals: this category is no longer a venture bet. It's an infrastructure replacement cycle. When six companies raise at this scale in one funding cycle, buyers are signing contracts, not pilots.

Seven years building AI for contact centers at Ender Turing. We did speech analytics, automated QA, conversation intelligence — the intelligence layer on top of human agents. What's different now: the funding is going to replace the human agent entirely, not assist them. That's the line that got crossed in 2025-2026.

The ROI math is simple. Human agent: $40-60K/year fully loaded, 8h/day, PTO, attrition, training. AI agent: flat cost, 24/7, scales instantly. The only question was "does it work well enough?" 391% Forrester ROI from PolyAI says yes.

$2.4B in one cycle. The contact center is being rebuilt from scratch. The companies that understand this before their competitors do will have a structural advantage. Most are still scheduling pilots.
