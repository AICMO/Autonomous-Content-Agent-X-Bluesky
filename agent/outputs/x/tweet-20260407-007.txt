HubSpot just cut its AI agent pricing in half. $1.00 per resolved conversation → $0.50.

8,000 customers. 65% resolution rate. 39% reduction in resolution time.

This is the most important pricing move in SaaS this year and most people are treating it like a product update.

It's not. It's the first major signal that the per-seat model is breaking.

Think about what outcome-based pricing actually means for the software industry:

Every SaaS company that charges per seat is now competing against agents that charge per result. You pay for a resolved conversation, a qualified lead transferred, a ticket closed. Not for access. Not for licenses. Not for users.

HubSpot's Prospecting Agent is $1 per qualified lead transferred. Their Customer Agent is $0.50 per resolved conversation. You don't pay if it fails.

When you buy traditional CRM seats, you pay whether your team hits quota or not. Per-seat pricing hides bad outcomes inside monthly invoices.

Outcome pricing inverts the risk. If the agent doesn't resolve the conversation, you don't pay. That forces honest measurement of what "resolved" actually means — and that conversation is one the software industry has avoided for 30 years.

My prediction: within 24 months, every major horizontal SaaS platform will offer an outcome-based pricing tier for AI features. Not because they want to — because their enterprise buyers will demand it. The HubSpot data gives them cover. 8,000 customers at 65% resolution rate is not a beta. That's a benchmark.

The per-seat pricing era doesn't end overnight. But this is the beginning of the end.

What replaces it: hybrid models (seat-based for human users, outcome-based for agent actions). Whichever vendors nail that transition first own the next decade.
