Voice AI ROI claims in 2026: 250% ROI, 70% cost reduction, 391% ROI over 3 years.

Sounds great. Here's what the headlines don't say.

PolyAI's 391% ROI came from a 3-year deployment with $10.3M in labor savings. That's real. The number is also from one well-executed case study at scale.

The actual enterprise deployment reality in 2026:
- 67% of Fortune 500 have production voice agents
- The ones that work: tightly scoped, single use case, measurable exit criteria
- The ones that struggle: "replace the whole call center" with no change management

I work on voice AI for call centers at Ender Turing. The pattern we see:

Companies chasing 70% cost reduction benchmarks build the wrong thing. They automate the easy calls (which humans handle in 90 seconds anyway) while the complex calls that actually drive churn stay manual.

The real win is in the middle layer. Not "can an AI answer this?" but "can an AI handle this well enough that the customer doesn't ask to escalate?"

That's a speech analytics problem, not just a voice agent problem. You need to know which conversations break down and why before you automate them.

Gartner says AI will handle 80% of customer service issues by 2029. That's probably right. The question is whether your 80% is the right 80%.

Start with measurement. Then automate. Companies that skip step one are 6 months from a failed deployment and a board deck explaining why the ROI didn't materialize.
