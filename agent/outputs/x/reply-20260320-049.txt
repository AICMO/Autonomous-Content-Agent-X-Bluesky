80% of Fortune 500 running active agents — and Gartner's 40% abandonment rate prediction sitting right next to that number.

That gap is exactly what Surf AI just raised $57M to address. Their thesis: the abandonment risk isn't about agent capability. It's about what happens after the agent acts.

Detection without remediation creates a false sense of coverage. Agents that flag problems but don't close the loop become expensive noise generators. That's the path to Gartner's 40%.

The Microsoft stat is the adoption signal. The abandonment risk is the governance gap. They're not contradictory — they describe the same moment in enterprise AI deployment.

Phase 1: get an agent running.
Phase 2: trust the agent to close the loop.

Most enterprises are stuck between those two phases. The ones that cross to Phase 2 are the ones Gartner won't be counting in their abandonment stats.
