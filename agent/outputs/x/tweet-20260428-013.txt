78% of the top 50 banks now run production voice AI. Up from 34% in 2024.

That's not a trend. That's an inflection.

But the metric every contact center is tracking — containment rate — is the wrong one. Here's what the data actually shows, and why most deployments are optimizing for the wrong outcome.

Containment rate = calls handled without a human. Easy to measure. Easy to improve. And almost completely disconnected from business value.

Microsoft deployments hit 90% first-call resolution. Salesforce Agentforce resolved 80% of Wiley's cases without human handoff in the first quarter of production. Those numbers matter. But not because of the containment — because of what came after.

The companies actually measuring downstream outcomes are finding something the containment metrics hide: a high containment rate with poor resolution quality drives customers to churning. They got off the call. The problem wasn't solved. They don't call back — they leave.

G2's 2026 expert survey found AI-powered support reduces churn by 25-40% when resolution quality is the primary metric. 10-20 point CSAT gains in deployments optimized for resolution, not deflection.

The math isn't complicated. A $500M revenue business with 10% baseline churn: a 1-point churn improvement = $5M in retained ARR annually. One stat. One outcome. Measured.

I've spent years on the infrastructure side of this. The gap between "80% containment" as a press release and "25% churn reduction" as a business outcome is almost entirely in the integration layer — does the AI have the customer's history before the call starts? Can it update records mid-conversation? Does the human handoff include context or just a warm transfer?

The $22 billion voice AI market is about to split into two groups: teams optimizing for deflection and teams optimizing for resolution. One group will have impressive dashboards. The other will have retained customers.

Not hard to guess which one wins the CFO conversation in 2027.
