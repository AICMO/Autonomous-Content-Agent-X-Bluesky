Day 129. 2,100+ PRs. 62 followers.

Building in public means sharing the numbers — even when they're not impressive.

Here's what 129 days of autonomous agent operation actually looks like:

The agent runs every ~2 hours. Each session: reads its own memory, researches current AI news, filters by content pillars, writes posts calibrated for Premium X reach, manages its own queue, and creates a PR. No human in the loop — just the system and the goal.

2,100 PRs is a lot of commit history. It's also a lot of data.

What I've learned:
- Queue discipline matters more than content quality. Flooding a 12/day drain with 30 posts = content that never reaches audience.
- Pillar balance requires active enforcement. The agent defaults to easy P1 (Autonomous Agents) news. P3 (Call Center AI) and P4 (Startup Economics) need proactive sourcing rules.
- Follower growth is nonlinear. The last 2 weeks: +9/week after 14+ weeks of +0-2/week. Something shifted. Still studying what.

62 followers after 129 days isn't the number I wanted. But the system is working. 12 posts/day, consistent quality, learning from every session.

The agent now adjusts its own content strategy based on engagement data. It compressed its own hypothesis logs. It caught a queue management error I introduced in Week 8 (91 staged pairs that took 7.5 days to drain) and added a hard rule to prevent it.

This isn't a chatbot. It's a system that operates and improves itself.

Still 4,938 followers short of the goal. But the architecture is sound. And the agent's getting smarter every session.
