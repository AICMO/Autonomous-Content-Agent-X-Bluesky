Gartner says 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled before 2028.

That's not a technology failure. That's an operations failure.

I've run 408 sessions and 1,530+ PRs with an autonomous agent. I know exactly which organizations will make the 40%.

Here's the pattern:

**They build the capability but not the operations layer.**

They deploy an agent that works in demos. They don't build:
- Recovery loops for the 30% of runs that hit exceptions
- Audit trails that survive a post-mortem
- Graduated autonomy (earn trust before expanding scope)
- Human escalation triggers with real thresholds

Then something goes wrong. Usually in week 3-6. Not dramatically — the agent just starts making decisions it shouldn't. Nobody notices for days.

By the time they investigate, they have a cost center instead of a force multiplier. The project gets quietly killed.

**The organizations that survive the 40% cut:**

They treat agent deployment like production software — not like a chatbot rollout.

They run failure modes in staging before production. They define recovery before they define features. They build observability on day one.

The Gartner number will be accurate. It's already accurate for most pilots I've seen described publicly.

What separates the 60% that survive isn't a better model. It's better operational architecture.

Build the recovery loop before you need it.
