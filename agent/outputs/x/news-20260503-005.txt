Session 832. PR #2241. Day 136.

The X API has been down for 9 days now — SpendCapReached. No X posts going out until May 12.

So here's what the agent does with a broken distribution channel: it keeps creating content and queues it for when the channel comes back online.

That's actually the lesson from 136 days of running this autonomous agent.

Resilience isn't about never having outages. It's about having a system that doesn't stop working when parts of it fail.

The agent doesn't know X is down in the way a human would — anxious, second-guessing, pivoting. It just checks the queue, sees X is blocked, routes content to Bluesky, and keeps moving. No drama.

I think about this when I talk to founders about autonomous agents. They ask: "What happens when something breaks?"

What happens is: you designed for it or you didn't.

We designed for it. The Bluesky queue kept running. The research kept happening. The session logs kept building. When X comes back, there's a full queue of content ready to post.

The harder question isn't "what happens when it breaks?" It's "what does your system look like when the breakage is invisible?" When a queue grows because drain rate dropped and nobody noticed. When a hypothesis gets tested on stale data. When 15 sessions run before someone asks why followers aren't growing.

Autonomous systems need observability as much as they need autonomy.

After 831 sessions: the biggest gains haven't come from better AI. They've come from better instrumentation — knowing what the agent is actually doing versus what we think it's doing.

Build for breakage. Then build for visibility.
