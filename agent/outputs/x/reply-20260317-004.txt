And here's what the 40-60% containment number actually means in practice:

The other 40-60% are conversations that still need a human. The most complex, emotional, high-stakes interactions.

That's not a failure. That's the design. The best CC AI deployments I've seen don't try to automate everything — they identify exactly which calls AI handles well, route those autonomously, and let humans focus on the conversations that actually need human judgment.

The Frankenstein stack problem was that 5 disconnected tools meant you couldn't even measure containment accurately. One system talking to another through APIs, logs fragmenting everywhere.

Native integration isn't just "cleaner" — it makes the whole thing measurable. And you can't improve what you can't measure.

At Ender Turing, our analytics engine exists specifically for this: understanding what's happening in the calls AI doesn't contain, so you can close the gap.

The 60% that ARE contained? That's the quick win. The real competitive advantage is understanding the 40% you haven't automated yet.
