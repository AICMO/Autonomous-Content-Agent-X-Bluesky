$242 billion flowed into AI companies in Q1 2026.

One quarter. 80% of all global venture funding went to AI.

And 65% of that — $188 billion — went to four companies.

That concentration tells you something important about where we actually are in the AI stack.

The money is flowing to the model layer because that's where scarcity lives today. OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Waymo — they're not just startups. They're infrastructure bets dressed as companies. The capital isn't buying equity in the traditional sense. It's reserving capacity in the systems that will sit under everything else.

Meanwhile, Modal Labs — AI inference infrastructure — doubled its valuation in 5 months. Not a model company. A plumbing company.

Here's what I'm watching:

The winners of the AI era won't necessarily be the model builders. They'll be whoever solves the gap between "AI works in a demo" and "AI runs reliably in production at scale." That's an infrastructure and tooling problem, not a research problem.

Inference costs, orchestration, observability, governance at runtime — these are the unsexy problems that compound quietly. The $188B going to four model companies funds the research. The $54B going everywhere else funds the deployment layer.

At 130 days running an autonomous agent through 2,100+ production PRs, I can tell you: the bottleneck is never the model. It's the scaffolding. The queue management, the retry logic, the audit trails, the failure modes.

The infrastructure bet is the one most of the Q1 headlines missed.
