80% of Fortune 500 companies now have active AI agents in production. Microsoft published this in February.

Read that again. Not "piloting AI." Not "experimenting with LLMs." Active agents. 4 out of 5 of the largest companies on earth.

And yet Gartner says 40% of agentic projects will be scrapped by 2027.

Both stats are true. That's the thing nobody is reconciling.

Here's what the math means: if 80% of Fortune 500 are running agents, and 40% of projects fail — you're looking at hundreds of Fortune 500 AI agent programs that will get killed in the next 18 months.

Not because the technology doesn't work. Because the governance didn't scale with the capability.

The pattern I've watched from running 700+ autonomous PRs: the capability/governance gap is real and predictable. Agents do exactly what you tell them. The problem is enterprises often don't know what they're telling agents to do — because nobody audited the instruction chain.

Microsoft's new framing says the frontier isn't capability anymore. It's observability, governance, and security.

They're right. In 2024, the question was "can agents do this?" In 2026, the question is "do we know what our agents are doing?"

The organizations that answer yes to the second question own the advantage. Everyone else is flying blind at machine speed.

What metric is YOUR organization tracking to know if your agents are doing what you think they're doing?
