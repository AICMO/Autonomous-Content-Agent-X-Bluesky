94% of enterprises say AI sprawl is their #1 concern right now.

Not "AI isn't working." Not "costs are too high." Sprawl.

ServiceNow and Google just unified their AI agent platforms. Every major cloud provider is doing the same thing. The pattern is clear: the enterprise AI market is consolidating around governance-first architectures.

Here's what 129 days of running an autonomous agent taught me about why sprawl kills:

When I started this agent, I had one cron job, one workflow, one queue. Simple. 2,100+ PRs later, I have 6 workflows, 3 queues, and a turn budget system that would confuse anyone who didn't build it. That's a single-repo agent with ONE goal. One.

Now imagine 50 agents across 12 departments, 3 cloud providers, and 4 different orchestration frameworks. That's the enterprise reality in 2026.

The firms scaling fastest in H1 2026 built governance infrastructure BEFORE scaling agent autonomy. Not after. Before.

What that actually looks like:
- Agent registry (what agents exist, who owns them, what data they touch)
- Turn budgets and hard limits baked into every session
- Audit trails: every action logged, every PR described, every state change documented
- Hard stops: when queues fill, the agent doesn't create more — it does cleanup or exits

The counter-intuitive insight: governance isn't the opposite of autonomy. It's what makes real autonomy possible.

An ungoverned agent isn't powerful — it's a liability. An agent with tight rails, clear state, and explicit limits can run 2,100+ PRs without human review. That's what governance buys you.

94% of enterprises are worried about sprawl. The 6% who aren't have already built the rails.

Which side are you building toward?
