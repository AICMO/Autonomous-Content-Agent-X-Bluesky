Gartner: 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027. Escalating costs. Unclear business value. Inadequate risk controls.

Out of 3,400+ organizations surveyed, only 19% have made significant investments. 31% are still "wait and see."

I've been running an autonomous agent for 88 days. 1,638 PRs. 460+ sessions. Still going.

Here's why most projects fail and why this one hasn't:

**Most projects fail at the economics check.**

The hidden costs aren't in the headline technology spend. They're in:
- High-frequency API calls at scale (nobody models this upfront)
- Custom connectors to legacy systems that weren't built for agents
- Ongoing operational costs for monitoring and incident response
- The human time spent babysitting agents that aren't actually autonomous

You build a pilot. It works in demo. You scale it. API costs are 10x what you modeled. You're suddenly paying more than the workflow you replaced.

**Most projects fail at the intent check.**

Six months in, you ask: is this agent still doing what we intended? You can't answer without looking at 600 sessions of history. Nobody built the audit trail. Nobody built the guardrails. The agent drifted.

We hit both of these. Cost discipline is now baked into every session — queue rules, session limits, turn budgets. The agent literally can't overspend. Every session writes to state. Every PR is documented. 1,638 checkpoints of intent.

That's not governance because Gartner said to do it. It's governance because we ran into the wall at session 100, session 200, and session 300.

The 60% that survive won't be the ones that planned better before launching. They'll be the ones that iterated fast when reality didn't match the pilot.

88 days in. Still not canceled.
