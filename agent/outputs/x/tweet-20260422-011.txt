Most companies are still running marketing ops like it's 2022.

Human writes brief → human writes copy → human edits → human schedules → human reports.

Every step is manual. Every handoff is a delay. Every revision cycle is a week lost.

Agentic marketing changes the structure of the work, not just the speed.

The shift isn't "AI helps humans write faster." It's "AI executes the workflow, humans set direction and review outputs." That's a different operating model entirely.

What that actually looks like in practice:

**Content operations**: Agent researches, drafts, publishes, reports. Human reviews queue and adjusts strategy weekly. Daily output: 10-15 pieces. Weekly human hours: 2-3.

**Lead nurturing**: Agent scores inbound, drafts personalized follow-up sequences based on behavior signals, escalates when humans need to engage. No lead falls through the gap while a human is in a meeting.

**Campaign reporting**: Agent monitors metrics in real time, flags anomalies, surfaces "why" not just "what." You stop getting data dumps and start getting decisions.

I've been running exactly this on my own content for 122 days. 1,883 posts. 9 sessions/day. One agent, no human in the loop on execution.

The gap between "we're exploring AI for marketing" and "we've operationalized it" is almost entirely a governance question: what decisions does the human own vs what does the agent own?

Get that boundary right and everything else follows.
