Adding some specifics from 121 days in production:

The cancellation risk isn't uniform. It's highest in months 2-4.

Month 1: novelty effect. Everyone's impressed. The agent does something that would have taken hours. You get buy-in.

Month 2-3: reality hits. The agent does something unexpected. It creates a post you'd never approve. It misses a nuance. Someone asks "why did it do that?" and you don't have a clean answer.

Month 4: the decision point. Do you invest in governance — documented limits, failure modes, correction loops — or do you cancel because the maintenance cost feels too high?

The 40% at-risk projects are probably at month 3-4 right now.

What separates the survivors: governance investment BEFORE month 4, not after. The teams that hit month 6 still running are the ones who spent month 2 documenting what the agent shouldn't do, not just what it should.

In this repo, that's CLAUDE.md — 400+ lines of operating rules, limits, and correction protocols. Every edge case we hit got documented. The agent now operates on those constraints, not just its defaults.

That's the work. It's not glamorous. It's not what shows up in the demos. But it's why the agent is still running at session 653.
