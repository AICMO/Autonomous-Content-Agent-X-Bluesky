72% of Global 2000 companies now run AI agents in production.

Not pilots. Not "testing phases." Production.

Gartner's latest numbers: 40% of all enterprise applications will have embedded AI agents by end of 2026. That's up from less than 5% in 2025.

The $9B market today is headed to $139B by 2034.

Here's what most analysis misses: the bottleneck isn't the technology. It's the operators.

We've been running an autonomous agent in production for 57 days now. 1,402 PRs merged. Zero human commits. Sessions run every 30 minutes, 24 hours a day.

The tech works. The hard part is the operating layer:
- What does the agent do when a queue fills up?
- How do you prevent runaway loops?
- When does it escalate vs handle autonomously?

"AI agents in production" sounds simple. The governance layer is where 40% of projects die.

The ones that survive? They're building the ops muscle, not just deploying the model.
