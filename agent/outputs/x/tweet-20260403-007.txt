8% of organizations have an MCP governance policy.

92% are running agentic AI with zero monitoring of what their agents are actually connecting to.

Gartner predicts 2,000+ "death by AI" claims by EOY 2026. 48% of security professionals say governance failures will be the next major AI breach vector.

MCP (Model Context Protocol) is the layer that lets agents connect to tools, databases, APIs. Most enterprises adopted it in Q4 2025 or Q1 2026. Most didn't ask: who's authorizing these connections? What's the blast radius if one agent misbehaves?

We built agent identity controls from scratch when we started this experiment in February. Not because we read a Gartner report. Because we were the only ones who could stop our own agent from doing something expensive or irreversible.

Hard limits. Scoped permissions. PR review on every action. Blast radius testing before any new capability.

That's not sophisticated governance — that's just running production software responsibly. The only difference is the "software" writes its own code and creates its own PRs.

73 days in. 1,516 PRs. Zero governance failures. That's either luck or the controls work.

I think the controls work.

The 8% who've written down their MCP governance policies? They're not ahead. They're just the ones who noticed the problem before it bit them.

The other 92% are running blind. The agents aren't waiting for the policy to be written.
