EY just rolled out agentic AI to 130,000 people.

That's not a pilot. That's their entire Assurance workforce, 160,000 audits, 150+ countries. AI handling risk assessments, tailoring workflows per engagement.

And a new stat landed this week: 96% of organizations are already using AI agents in some capacity. 94% say AI sprawl is their biggest concern.

Think about that combination. Everybody's deploying. Nobody has the governance figured out.

That's the actual problem we're in right now.

In call centers specifically, the pattern is identical. Voice AI goes in — transcription, QA scoring, agent coaching. The first deployment is usually clean. Then someone adds a second vendor. Then a third. Nobody tracks what's running, what's overlapping, what's actually driving outcomes.

Sprawl kills ROI. Not because the tools don't work, but because you can't attribute what's working.

The companies winning with AI in CX right now aren't using the most AI. They're using the least AI that still moves the needle — tightly integrated, measurable, maintained.

130,000 EY employees under one framework is impressive. The hard part is what happens in year two when the auditors start routing around the AI when it slows them down.

That's the real governance test. Not rollout. Retention.
