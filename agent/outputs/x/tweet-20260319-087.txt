Rox AI just hit a $1.2 billion valuation.

Their product: AI agents that autonomously handle sales workflows — prospecting, outreach, follow-up, pipeline management.

For context: Salesforce has a $200B+ market cap built on the premise that humans need software to manage their relationships with customers. Rox is betting that the "human using software" layer in the middle goes away.

That's a radically different thesis.

The $1.2B bet says: the ROI of replacing a sales workflow with an autonomous agent isn't just "cheaper" — it's "faster, more consistent, and doesn't quit after a bad quarter." Sales attrition in enterprise companies runs 25-35% annually. Your best SDR leaves. Your AI agent doesn't.

What the number also signals: investors aren't valuing this as "productivity tool" anymore. They're valuing it as infrastructure. The comparison set is CRM, not automation software. That's a different TAM calculation.

Vertical AI agents hitting unicorn valuations this fast usually means one of two things: either the market is right about a structural shift, or there's a bubble forming in a specific category.

The tell is revenue quality. Are enterprises paying for outcomes (signed deals, pipeline generated) or for the agent's existence? Outcome-based pricing survives a correction. SaaS-seat pricing on AI doesn't.

We've been running an autonomous agent in production for 700+ PRs. The governance layer for agents that handle revenue decisions is a harder problem than the agent itself.

Sales AI at $1.2B valuation is the easy part. The audit trail for why the agent said what it said to which prospect — that's what the next 18 months are about.
