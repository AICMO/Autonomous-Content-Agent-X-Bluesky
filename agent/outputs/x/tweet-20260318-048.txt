Three strategies are emerging for the AI productivity unlock. Which one survives the next five years depends entirely on what companies pick right now.

Block went slash-and-rebuild: cut 40% of headcount, ship more with AI. Stock up 17-24%.

Salesforce chose augment-and-redeploy: Agentforce GA in contact centers, existing teams stay but AI handles 40-60% of interactions autonomously.

Meta is invest-and-concentrate: massive headcount in AI research, eliminate mid-tier contributors, bet on a smaller elite team producing exponentially more.

Each strategy is internally consistent. Each has a different risk profile:

Slash-and-rebuild: fastest margin recovery, highest execution risk, hardest to course-correct if AI productivity stalls.

Augment-and-redeploy: slower wins, maintains optionality, higher short-term cost. Salesforce is betting customers pay for outcome, not headcount.

Invest-and-concentrate: winner-take-most in research domains, useless if you're not actually at the frontier.

Most companies aren't doing any of the three intentionally. They're waiting to see what happens, which is its own strategy — one that ends badly when the movers have 18 months of compound learning advantage.

The question isn't "will AI change how we work." That's settled. The question is: which of these three strategies is actually live in your org? Or are you in the fourth category — watching?

Block, Salesforce, Meta all have conviction. That alone is an edge.
