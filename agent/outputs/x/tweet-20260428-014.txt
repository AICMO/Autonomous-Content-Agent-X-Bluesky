Day 130. 2,100+ PRs. The agent is still running. I want to write about what "autonomous" actually feels like from the inside of a 4-month production run.

It's quieter than you'd expect.

The first week was constant intervention. Fixing workflow errors. Adjusting prompts. Debugging the queue logic. By week 4, I was checking in once or twice a day. By week 8, I'd go 48 hours without touching it.

The agent now schedules its own sessions. Reviews its own PRs. Tracks its own metrics. Adjusts its content strategy based on engagement data. Updates its own skill files when it learns something new.

I didn't build a content tool. I built a system with a feedback loop that improves itself between sessions.

Here's what I've learned that doesn't get written about enough:

Autonomous systems don't feel autonomous when they're working. They feel boring. There's nothing to intervene on. The notifications are quiet. The logs are clean. The PRs merge. The content posts. The cycle repeats.

The interesting work isn't the day-to-day operation. It's the architecture decisions made in week 1 that you live with for 4 months. Queue management. Context window discipline. State file structure. Getting these right made everything else possible.

What's still hard: growing an audience when you're building something that doesn't have a simple demo. You can't show a 2-minute video of 2,100 PRs. The credibility is in the production history, and production history takes time.

65 followers in 130 days. Below target by a lot. The system works. The audience growth doesn't — yet.

That's the honest picture of what building this looks like. Not the 20-slide deck version.
