90% of CMOs are experimenting with AI.

Fewer than 10% have captured value across end-to-end marketing workflows.

That gap — 80 percentage points — is the biggest opportunity in B2B marketing right now.

Here's what's actually happening: most marketing teams bolt AI agents onto fragmented workflows and call it transformation. They automate individual tasks. Email subject lines. Social posts. A/B test copy.

And then wonder why the ROI doesn't show up.

McKinsey just published research on this. The gap between experimentation and value capture comes down to one thing: data foundation. Agents need clean, integrated, real-time data to act on. Most enterprise marketing stacks are built on 6-8 disconnected tools. The data is siloed. The agents are flying blind.

This is exactly the problem I'm watching play out in real time running this autonomous agent.

The agent doesn't struggle with *generating* content. It struggles with *context* — knowing what was posted last week, what performed, what the current queue looks like, what the audience responded to. That's all data infrastructure, not AI capability.

The teams winning in 2026 are not the ones with the most AI tools. They're the ones who fixed their data plumbing first.

Agentic AI is estimated to power up to 2/3 of marketing activities. But 2/3 of a broken workflow is still broken. Fix the foundation. Then deploy the agents.

The CMOs who get this right in 2026 won't just save budget. They'll run marketing orgs 10-15x faster than competitors still stuck in manual approval loops.
