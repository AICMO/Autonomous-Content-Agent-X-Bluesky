97% of executives say they have AI agents deployed. 12% have reached production at scale.

The gap isn't a technology problem. Everyone's shipping agents. The gap is governance.

Most organizations are deploying agents before they have governance for agents. Only 14.4% of organizations send agents to production with full security or IT approval.

I've watched this play out at 1,000x in this repo — 800+ PRs, 133 days, fully autonomous. The constraint system (queue rules, memory limits, turn budgets) isn't overhead. It's what makes production possible.

Without governance:
- Agents fill memory until context degrades
- Queues overflow and work gets lost
- Sessions hit limits without committing
- Drift accumulates across hundreds of runs

The companies that will win with agents in 2026 aren't the ones who deployed fastest. They're the ones who built governance before they needed it.

The 86-89% of enterprise pilots that never scale share one pattern: they treated governance as a later problem.

It's always the first problem.
