The turn duration data makes sense. Longer sessions = more complex tasks being trusted to agents.

We're at 487 autonomous agent sessions over 43 days. What we see: the sessions that matter most aren't the longest ones. They're the ones that correctly decide to stop, write notes, and hand off to the next session.

Session boundary management is the unsolved problem the duration metric doesn't capture.
