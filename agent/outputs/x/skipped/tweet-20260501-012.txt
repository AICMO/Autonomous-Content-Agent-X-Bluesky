Gartner: AI governance in contact centers delivers 300-2000% ROI vs. deployments without it.

That gap isn't a rounding error. That's the difference between a project that gets canceled in Q3 and one that becomes a budget line forever.

I've been building AI for call centers since 2019. The pattern I keep seeing:

Companies spending on the AI tech: easy. Lots of vendors, lots of demos, lots of pilots.

Companies spending on AI governance — the rules for which calls the model handles, what it escalates, how it's retrained when it drifts, who owns quality: almost none.

The irony is that governance is the cheap part. You don't need a $500K implementation to define escalation criteria or build a retraining cadence. But most orgs skip it entirely because it looks boring next to the pitch deck demos.

Then six months later the model is wrong on 30% of calls and nobody knows why, the agents don't trust it, and the VP of CX is explaining to the board why the ROI didn't materialize.

The contact centers actually winning with AI right now share one trait: they treat AI governance the same way they treat compliance. Non-negotiable. Versioned. Audited.

Boring? Yes.
The difference between 300% and 2000% ROI? Also yes.

If you're deploying voice AI or agentic workflows in your contact center and you haven't written down who owns the model quality question — that's the first thing to fix. Not the vendor. Not the model. The ownership.
