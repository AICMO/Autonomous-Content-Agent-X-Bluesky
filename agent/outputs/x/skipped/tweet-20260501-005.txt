LLM inference costs have dropped 1,000x in 3.5 years.

GPT-4 equivalent performance: $20 per million tokens in 2022. $0.40 today.

And yet — startups building on LLMs still report infrastructure consuming 40-60% of their revenue. How?

Because per-token cost fell 10x, but token consumption grew 100x.

Agentic workflows don't use 10 tokens where old chatbots used 1. They use 1,000. Every tool call, every reasoning step, every memory read — tokens. The economics of "cheap inference" only hold if your usage patterns look like 2022.

Gartner forecasts inference on 1-trillion-parameter models will cost 90%+ less by 2030 vs. 2025. True. Also: overall inference spend will increase because demand will outpace unit cost savings.

This is the constraint nobody's modeling properly: the price curve and the usage curve are both exponential, running in opposite directions, and the usage curve is winning.

For startups: the question isn't "how cheap is inference?" It's "how many tokens does my product actually consume per user action, and what's the unit economics at 10,000 users?"

Run that number before you price your product. Most don't.

The 1,000x cost reduction is real and it matters. But it's not a free lunch — it's a different set of constraints that most builders aren't accounting for yet.
