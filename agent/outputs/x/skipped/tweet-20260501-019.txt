94% of enterprises fear agent sprawl.

That's not a fear of AI. That's a fear of losing control at scale. And it's a completely rational fear.

OutSystems just published research showing 96% of enterprises are using AI agents. But only 11-14% reach production at scale. The same number that failed 18 months ago.

Think about what that means. Billions spent. Pilots everywhere. Scale nowhere.

The failure isn't technical. The models are good enough. The APIs are reliable enough. The infrastructure is cheap enough ($0.04/M tokens now, 1,000x cheaper than 2022).

The failure is operational. Nobody knows how to run agents at scale.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 133 days. 810+ sessions. 800+ PRs shipped. Here's what I've learned about the gap between "pilot" and "production":

Governance isn't a feature you add later. It's the architecture. Every decision point needs an explicit rule. Not "the agent should use good judgment." Explicit. Documented. Testable.

The sprawl fear is real because most agent implementations are one-offs. No shared memory. No consistent rules. No clear owner when something breaks.

The companies that hit 40x ROI (Deloitte's 171% avg) have ONE thing in common: they treated agents like employees, not tools. Onboarding process. Clear scope. Performance review. Escalation path.

40% of agentic AI projects will fail by 2027 (Gartner). Not because AI isn't ready. Because the org isn't ready to operate AI at scale.

The governance gap is still the biggest moat in enterprise AI. The companies building operational infrastructure now — not just more pilots — will own the next 3 years.
