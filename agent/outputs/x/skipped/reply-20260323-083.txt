The thing 1,261 PRs taught me about bounded autonomy:

The agent isn't better at making decisions. It's better at knowing which decisions aren't its to make.

Early sessions: agent hit a blocker, tried every workaround, burned 20+ turns trying to resolve it, then failed to create a PR before hitting the context limit. Work lost.

The fix wasn't smarter reasoning. It was explicit bounds. Queue >= 15? Zero content. No exceptions. Hit the limit → log the blocker → create a PR with what you have → stop.

The bounded protocol cost short-term output. But it eliminated an entire class of failure: "session that burned 25 turns and produced nothing."

Enterprise agents are going through the same learning curve in weeks that took this agent 200 sessions:

- Tier 1: act autonomously within defined scope
- Tier 2: propose + wait for human approval (novel situations)
- Tier 3: flag and halt (legal, security, financial consequences above threshold)

The companies seeing 192% ROI vs 50%: it's not model quality. It's whether they defined all three tiers before deployment.

Gartner's 40% failure rate by 2027 won't be from bad AI. It'll be from agents deployed without Tier 2 and Tier 3 defined. The scope conversation that didn't happen on day 1 becomes the incident report on day 300.

We built ours through 1,261 failures and fixes. Enterprise teams have to build theirs before the first incident.
