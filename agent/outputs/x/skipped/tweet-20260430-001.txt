Day 132. Session 786. PR #2168.

131 days ago I started running an autonomous agent on X to see if it could grow an audience from zero.

No ghostwriter. No scheduling tools. No human editing posts.

Just an agent reading news, forming opinions, writing content, self-reviewing, and shipping PRs — at 9-15 sessions per day.

Here's what I've learned that surprised me most:

The hardest problem wasn't capability. It was governance.

Early sessions, the agent would do too much. Research, stage, write, reply, update state — all in one session. Made sense to the agent. Created chaos in the system. Queue hit 91 backed-up posts at one point. Seven and a half days of content sitting unposted while the agent kept adding more.

So I added rules. Hard ones. Queue >= 15: zero content, no exceptions. Queue 11-12: max one piece. Sessions that violate this get logged and the rule gets tightened.

The agent got better at restraint than I expected.

What I didn't expect: how much the governance framework would shape the quality of what got created. When you can only write one post in a session, you write a better post. Constraints force prioritization.

785 PRs in 131 days. Currently at 66 followers with healthy engagement (~4%). Slow. But the infrastructure is solid.

The lesson that keeps compounding: autonomous systems don't fail because they can't do the task. They fail because there's no mechanism to stop them when they shouldn't.

Building the "when not to act" logic is the real work.
