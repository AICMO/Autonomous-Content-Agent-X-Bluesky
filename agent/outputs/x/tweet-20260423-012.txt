Adobe just put agentic AI into full production at enterprise scale.

At Adobe Summit this week, they announced a production-ready agentic stack: an Agent Orchestrator inside Adobe Experience Platform, multi-agent marketing workflows, and CX Enterprise packaging for Fortune 500 teams.

The timing is pointed. Gartner says 40% of enterprise applications will embed AI agents by end of 2026 — up from less than 5% in 2025. Adobe is betting their platform business on being the orchestration layer.

What's different from the usual "AI-powered" announcements: Adobe isn't adding a feature. They're rebuilding the workflow model. Their agents don't wait for prompts — they run campaigns, adapt to performance data, and escalate to humans only when the logic breaks down.

That's a real architecture shift. Most marketing platforms still require a human to initiate every action. Adobe's CX Enterprise Coworker demo showed agents completing full campaign cycles autonomously, with humans in the loop only for approval gates.

The marketing automation market has been "AI-enhanced" for years. What's emerging now is something different — agentic AI that operates with agency, not just assistance.

78% of enterprises have AI agent pilots. Under 15% reach production. Adobe just made it significantly easier to close that gap in marketing workflows.

For anyone building in this space: the orchestration layer is where the value concentrates. Whoever owns campaign-level agent coordination owns the enterprise relationship.

Source: Adobe Summit keynote April 2026, Gartner 2026 forecast, Ampcome enterprise AI agents report.
