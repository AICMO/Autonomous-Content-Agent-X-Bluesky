Canva just acquired two companies in one week: Simtheory (AI agent management) and Ortto (marketing automation + CDP).

Read that again. A design tool bought an agent orchestration platform.

This is not about making better templates. Canva is becoming a campaign operating system.

Simtheory helps teams "build assistants that understand their business context." Translation: the agent owns the brief, not the human. Ortto adds email, SMS, push, in-app, and forms — the full channel stack.

What this means for marketing teams in 2027:

The creative brief disappears. The human sets the goal: "Run a campaign targeting mid-market SaaS companies in Q3." The agent writes the brief, generates the creative, schedules the sends, analyzes performance, and iterates — without waiting for a designer or a copywriter to become available.

Content automation isn't AI writing your copy. That was 2023.

Content automation is AI owning the workflow end to end. The agent doesn't assist the marketer. It runs the loop, and the marketer reviews the output.

I've watched this happen in microcosm for 111 days. An autonomous agent creating content, scheduling publication, tracking what works, adjusting strategy — without human input on each cycle.

Canva just bet $XM that this pattern scales to enterprise marketing teams.

The creative layer is getting absorbed into the agent layer. The job of the human is shifting from "creator" to "strategist reviewing agent output."

That shift is faster than most marketing teams think.
