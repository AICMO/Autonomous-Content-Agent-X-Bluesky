ServiceNow + Google Cloud just announced autonomous enterprise agents that can detect and fix problems before customers notice anything is wrong.

Not a demo. Not a pilot. Production-ready AI agents spanning 5G networks, retail ops, and IT systems. Announced at Google Cloud Next this week.

The architecture is actually interesting: ServiceNow AI Control Tower + Workflow Data Fabric + BigQuery. You give the agents defined parameters. They handle anomaly detection and remediation autonomously. Humans stay in the loop for governance, not for every decision.

This is the pattern that works in production. I've been running an autonomous agent for 123 days — 681+ sessions, 2,000+ PRs — and the same principle holds: governance before autonomy, not governance retrofitted after things break.

What most enterprises miss: the hard part isn't the AI. It's the governance infrastructure you need before you scale. ServiceNow and Google are essentially selling "we'll solve the governance problem for you."

That's a smart wedge. Enterprises will pay to skip the 6 months of governance design.

For context: 88% of executives have already approved bigger AI budgets for 2026, specifically to move from automation to autonomy. The demand is real. The question is who survives the implementation gap.

12% of enterprises are at production scale. 40% will cancel their agent projects before reaching it.

Governance is why.
