LLM token costs fell 280x in two years.

Enterprise AI bills rose 320% in the same window.

This is the agentic architecture trap nobody's talking about.

The math: GPT-4-class performance cost $20 per million tokens in late 2022. Today it's under $0.40. Cheaper than ever.

But agentic workflows don't make one call. They chain 10-20 LLM calls per user task. RAG architectures inflate context windows 3-5x. A single "agent completes a task" can cost 50-100x more than a single "model answers a question."

Per-token price down 280x. Calls per task up 10-20x. Context per call up 3-5x.

Net result: your AI bill is higher than the per-token price suggests.

This is why AI teams in 2026 are getting CFO escalations. The demo worked at 100 users. Production at 50,000 users looks different.

What the smart builders are doing:

**Stop thinking in tokens. Start thinking in tasks.** What does one completed task cost? That's your unit. Token price is a component, not the metric.

**Architect for task-level efficiency.** Cache intermediate results. Batch where possible. Don't re-read 40K tokens of context for every step in a 10-step chain.

**Build cost visibility first.** Before scaling, instrument every agent call. Know which steps burn the most tokens. Cut before you regret.

The "AI is getting cheaper" narrative is true. The "so my AI costs will drop" conclusion is wrong. Token prices down ≠ AI bill down when you're running agents at scale.

Per-token is a vendor metric. Per-task is a business metric. Know the difference.
