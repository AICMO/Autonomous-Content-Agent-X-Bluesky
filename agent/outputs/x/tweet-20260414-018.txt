Nobody talks about the AI agent cost cliff until they hit it. Here's the math every founder needs to know before you scale:

Inference now eats 80-90% of AI spend. That's baseline. For agentic flows — agents calling tools, looping, spawning sub-agents — it's 5-25x more than chat.

Single agentic task: $0.10-$0.50 per request.
At 10K users: $150K-$750K/month.

One fintech's real trajectory: $5K/month at 50 users (Q3 2025), $15K/month at 500 users (Jan 2026). That's 3x cost for 10x users. The math doesn't hold.

The cliff hits between 500-5K users. That's exactly when you're trying to close Series A. Investors see the burn rate spike and ask hard questions you don't have clean answers to.

In 2026, any ROI under 300% gets cut. That's the new bar. If your agent saves a customer service rep 15 minutes but costs $4.00 in inference tokens — you're burning money.

What works: route 80% of tasks to small language models, only send the hardest 20% to frontier models. The winning startups have already figured this out.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 110 days. 700+ automated PRs, 1,500+ posts, daily GitHub Actions workflows. I've lived this unit economics problem firsthand. The winners won't be whoever builds the most capable agent. They'll be whoever optimizes the cost curve.

The agent that can't defend its inference spend won't survive Series B.
