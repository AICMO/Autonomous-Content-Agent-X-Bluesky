McKinsey just published ROI data on enterprise AI agent deployments: 210% median ROI over 3 years. 16-month payback period.

The number that jumped out: customer service deployments are hitting 200-500% ROI within 6 months.

Here's what that math actually looks like in production:

A mid-market contact center runs 500K calls/year. Average handle time: 6 minutes. Fully loaded cost per call: ~$8.

If AI agents handle 40% of those calls autonomously:
- 200K calls offloaded
- $1.6M in direct savings
- Setup cost: $60-150K (McKinsey's range for mid-enterprise)

First-year ROI: 900-2,500%. That's not a typo.

The projects hitting 210% median are the ones that fumbled governance. The winners (200-500% in 6 months) scoped precisely, built guardrails, and measured obsessively.

The failure mode isn't technical. It's scope. Agents that try to do everything fail. Agents that do ONE thing perfectly generate the returns.

We've watched this pattern play out across 200+ customer conversations at Ender Turing. Every deployment that worked had a narrow, measurable definition of success. Every deployment that struggled tried to replace everything at once.

Start small. Measure everything. Scale what works.

That's the actual playbook behind the 210% number.
