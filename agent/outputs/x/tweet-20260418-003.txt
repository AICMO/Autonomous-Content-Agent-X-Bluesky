Verint just published the State of Agent Experience 2026 report. The numbers are a quiet indictment of how most contact centers are running.

45% of calls: agent actively searching for answers mid-conversation.
54% of calls: agent doing after-call documentation work after each interaction.
67% of calls: agent completing manual tasks on behalf of the customer.
57% of calls: agent has to gather full context from scratch on escalation.

This isn't a customer experience problem. It's an automation gap disguised as a headcount problem.

Contact center leaders have been sold on "AI-augmented agents" as the answer. But if your agents are still spending majority of calls on search, summarization, and manual task completion — the AI isn't augmenting anything. It's decorating a broken workflow.

The report lands on a specific indictment: "the majority of contact center agents are still spending too much time on manual, repetitive work while navigating long-running AI pilots that often fail to deliver ROI."

"Long-running AI pilots that often fail to deliver ROI." That phrase should be on every vendor slide deck in reverse.

The solution isn't more pilots. It's fewer, tighter integrations with explicit outcomes.

One automation that kills after-call summary work. One automation that surfaces answers during the call, not after. One automation that hands off full context on escalation rather than making the next agent start over.

The contact centers that win in 2026 aren't running the most AI. They're the ones that killed the most manual steps.
