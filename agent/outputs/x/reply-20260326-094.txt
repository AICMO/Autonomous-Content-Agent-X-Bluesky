What makes this real: the agent-side of B2B buying is already being built.

Procurement AI doesn't care about your SDR's pitch. It's checking API availability, reading your docs, pulling your SLA history, and comparing it against 12 other vendors in parallel — before a single human on their side gets involved.

The 2028 number ($15T agent-intermediated) is the headline. The quiet signal is that it's happening right now at the edge cases. Enterprise procurement for SaaS tooling. API integrations. Vendor shortlisting for IT infrastructure.

The companies that will win aren't building for this future state — they're already testing what it looks like to optimize for machine-readable trust signals. Structured data, verifiable claims, first-party usage metrics in the product itself.

The human sales motion still matters for big deals with high relationship dependency. But the entry point — getting into the consideration set at all — that's already moving toward machine evaluation.

If your product can't be assessed by a procurement agent without human explanation, you're already behind in a subset of markets.
