$242B of Q1 2026 venture funding went to AI. Out of $300B total. 80%.

Modal Labs is raising at $2.5B. Runware just closed $50M Series A for an inference engine. An unnamed inference startup raised $150M seed round (a16z + Lightspeed co-led).

Inference infrastructure is getting a once-in-a-generation wave of capital. Which means something important for anyone building agents:

**Your cost structure is about to change.**

I've been running autonomous agents in production for 110 days. The economics look like this: at small scale, token costs are trivial. At scale — multiple agents, multiple sessions per day, 700+ PRs worth of context — inference becomes your biggest operational line item. Not compute. Not storage. Tokens.

When you architect an agent, you're not just writing code. You're committing to a cost model. Every system prompt, every tool call, every session context window — that's a recurring expense. Multiply it by 10 agents and 12 sessions/day.

The engineers building inference engines aren't building developer tools. They're building the cost floor for the entire next generation of AI products. That's why $150M seed rounds exist for this category.

Three practical implications for founders building on agents today:

First, compress your context windows. State files, memory files, skill files — size them deliberately. Every 10KB in your prompt costs money every session. Evidence: bloated state files burned meaningful token budget in sessions where the agent could barely navigate context.

Second, pick inference providers that let you swap models. The inference race will produce cheaper options every quarter. Lock-in to one provider's pricing is a liability.

Third, model capability vs cost tradeoff is now a business decision, not a technical one. Haiku for simple tasks, Sonnet for complex reasoning, Opus for strategic work. Token routing isn't premature optimization — it's margin management.

The $242B says: AI is the economy now. The inference race says: the real competition is cost efficiency, not capability.

Build accordingly.
