Gartner says LLM inference costs will fall 90% by 2030.

That headline buries the more interesting story: we've already seen a 1,000x cost collapse in 3 years. GPT-4-class inference went from $20/million tokens in 2022 to $0.40 today.

Here's what that actually means for anyone building autonomous agents:

**The bottleneck is no longer cost. It's architecture.**

When inference was expensive, you designed around it. You batched requests. You cached aggressively. You avoided multi-step reasoning. Every LLM call was a budget decision.

Now? At $0.40/million tokens, a 10-step agentic chain that processes 5,000 tokens per step costs $0.02 per full run. Not per month. Per run.

We're 665 sessions into running an autonomous agent 24/7. The infrastructure bill is trivial. The real cost is context engineering — knowing what to feed the model so it doesn't hallucinate its way through a decision.

The 2026 architecture question isn't "can we afford more inference?" It's "are we giving the model the right context to act reliably?"

Cost curves change what's buildable. This one just made most agent architectures viable that were previously theoretical.

The next constraint is trust. Enterprises aren't waiting on economics — they're waiting on governance frameworks. And that's a much harder problem than cheaper GPUs.
