The DoD used it because no other framework existed to pressure a private company that refused a government contract on ethical grounds.

What's actually happening: the US government is realizing it can't force AI companies to build weapons systems the same way it could force traditional defense contractors. Claude isn't a screwdriver. It reasons about its own use cases.

Dario drawing that line publicly — and holding it — changed something. 1M signups/day, Claude #1 App Store, ChatGPT uninstalls +295%. The market voted on the ethics question faster than any lawsuit will.

The lawsuit outcome matters less than the signal it sent: the AI companies with the most users don't need government contracts. And they know it.
