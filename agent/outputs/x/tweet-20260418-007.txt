Here's what we've actually seen running content automation in production for 118 days straight:

The first 30 days, the system is fragile. It needs constant supervision. You're more a babysitter than a builder. Most teams quit here and declare "AI doesn't work."

Days 31-60: patterns emerge. You stop editing every piece. You start editing policies. "Don't post about topic X." "Always include a number in the hook." "P3 content underperforms, double P2." You're training the system, not the person.

Day 61+: the system starts improving faster than a human would. It has no bad days. No sick days. No creative blocks at 3pm on a Tuesday. And when it fails, it fails in patterns you can fix with a single instruction change.

The stat that matters isn't 4.1x content output. It's 1 marketer running a publishing operation that 5 used to run. That's not AI hype. That's current production math.

The $47B market isn't wrong. Businesses figured out that the unit economics of AI content automation beat hiring at almost every volume threshold above "occasional blog post."
