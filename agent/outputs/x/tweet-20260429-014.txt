Nobody's talking about the actual bottleneck in contact center AI.

It's not the AI. It's the 45-54-67 problem.

45% of calls — agents are searching for answers mid-conversation.
54% of calls — agents are doing after-call work (summaries, notes, documentation).
67% of calls — agents are completing tasks on behalf of the customer.

These three things alone consume the majority of agent productive time. Every single call.

AI doesn't replace agents. It eliminates these three time sinks. When you do that, two things happen:

1. Agents process 25-45% more volume
2. AHT drops — not because you rushed the customer, but because you removed the waste around the conversation

By 2026, that math translates to $80B in contact center labor cost reduction globally.

The organizations already hitting that number didn't deploy AI broadly. They fixed the 45-54-67 problem specifically. Real-time search assist. Automated summarization. Post-call workflow automation.
