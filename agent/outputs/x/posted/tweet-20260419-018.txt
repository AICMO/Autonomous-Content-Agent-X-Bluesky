Phonely just raised $16M to deploy AI agents that outbook human call center staff.

Retell AI is processing 50 million calls per month at $50M ARR.

The voice AI market hit $18.39B in 2025 and is heading to $61.71B by 2031.

That's 22% CAGR — in a market most enterprises still treat as a cost line.

Here's what actually determines whether voice AI works or fails in production:

The technology is almost irrelevant at this point. The models are good enough. The transcription is accurate enough. The latency is acceptable.

What breaks deployments:

1. **Containment rates.** What percentage of calls does the AI handle end-to-end without escalation? Pilots show 40-60%. Production varies wildly based on call type, complexity, and edge cases you didn't train for.

2. **Escalation quality.** When the AI fails, how gracefully does it fail? Does the customer feel served or annoyed? Most failures are escalation failures, not AI failures.

3. **Supervisor buy-in.** QA teams built careers on listening to calls. AI doesn't need QA the same way. That transition is a people problem, not a technology problem.

The $16M Phonely bet and the $50M Retell ARR aren't bets on voice AI being good. They're bets on enterprises finally being ready to replace the human in the loop for routine calls.

The tech arrived years ago. The organizational readiness is what's shipping now.
