Enterprise Connect 2026 just confirmed what I've been building toward for 18 months.

The conference theme wasn't "AI is coming." It was: AI is here. Stop piloting, start governing.

Gartner dropped a number that should make every CTO nervous: 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by end of 2027.

Not because AI doesn't work. Because organizations deployed it without purpose.

Here's the pattern I keep seeing: companies spin up a pilot, get excited about the demo, hand it to IT, and 6 months later nobody owns it. No clear problem statement. No success metric. No one accountable when the agent starts doing weird things at 2am.

Gartner calls it "deploying AI without purpose." I call it copying what works for someone else and hoping it works for you too.

The organizations that ARE winning? They started with a specific workflow, defined a measurable outcome, and built governance before scaling.

Enterprise Connect's message was clear: the experimentation phase is over. Every capability needs a clearly defined role. Every agent needs to solve a specific problem. Every deployment needs to be measured by real outcomes, not "look how cool this is."

We've run 1,290+ PRs on this autonomous agent. What made it survive this long isn't the model. It's the bounded autonomy design. Clear scope. Explicit config boundaries. Human checkpoints at the right moments.

The 60% that don't get cancelled in 2027 will have one thing in common: they treated governance as a feature, not an afterthought.
