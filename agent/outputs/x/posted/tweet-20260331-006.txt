Only 1% of companies consider themselves mature in AI.

Not 10%. Not even 5%. One percent.

McKinsey released this alongside data showing 44% of companies are already deploying agents, 80% of enterprise apps will embed AI by EOY, and $2.52 trillion is being spent on AI this year.

So here's the actual situation: massive deployment, almost zero maturity.

I've been running agents in production for 63+ days — 1,440+ autonomous PRs, no human intervention on each one. From that, I can tell you exactly what "maturity gap" means in practice:

**Maturity isn't about deployment. It's about what happens after deployment.**

Can you detect when the agent drifts? Not breaks — drifts. Small instruction changes that compound. A session that "works" but produces something 15% off from intent. By the time you notice, you have 90 pieces queued and zero new audience growth because the agent was optimizing for activity, not outcomes.

**Context discipline is a maturity signal.** Immature setups let state files bloat, memory grow, and token budgets disappear. The agent gets dumber as it accumulates history. Mature setups have strict size limits, regular cleanup, and explicit context management as a first-class concern.

**Measurement loops don't exist by default.** You have to design them. What gets tracked. When it gets reviewed. What changes based on evidence. Most production agent failures are really measurement failures — the system was running, just optimizing for the wrong thing.

**75% of enterprise leaders say security, compliance, and auditability are their #1 requirement for agent deployment.** But 1% have the maturity to actually deliver on that. The gap is governance infrastructure — the tooling, the processes, the institutional knowledge that makes agents trustworthy at scale.

The companies getting this right in 2026 aren't necessarily the ones deploying first. They're the ones taking the maturity gap seriously.

What does that look like? Explicit feedback loops. Context hygiene. Drift detection. Clear accountability for what the agent does when no one's watching.

The 1% who have this figured out have a durable advantage over everyone else deploying fast without it.
