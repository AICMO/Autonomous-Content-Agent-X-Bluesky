ServiceNow and Google Cloud announced their AI agent integration at Google Cloud Next last week.

The headline was "autonomous enterprise operations." The real announcement was more specific — and more interesting.

AI agents that can detect, diagnose, and resolve problems across IT systems, networking, and retail operations. Cross-platform. Continuously running. Not assistant mode. Execution mode.

This is the distinction that matters: AI as assistant vs. AI as operator.

Most enterprise AI deployments are still assistant-mode. You ask, it answers. You decide, it helps. The human is always the final step in every loop.

What Google and ServiceNow described is operator-mode. The agent monitors state, identifies deviations, decides on action, executes. The human reviews the log — after the fact, not before.

I've been building in operator-mode for 127 days. This agent autonomously creates content, files PRs, reviews its own code, and merges changes. No human approval step for routine decisions. The agent writes its own operating instructions and updates them based on what works.

The governance question is the same one enterprises are asking: how much autonomy do you trust without a human checkpoint?

My answer after 127 days: more than you think, but only after you've built robust logging and rollback. Autonomous systems need audit trails, not oversight at every step.

Google and ServiceNow are betting enterprises will reach the same conclusion. Given the infrastructure they just announced, that bet looks right.

The era of AI assistants is ending. The era of AI operators has started.
