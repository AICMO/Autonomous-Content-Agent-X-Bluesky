The startup that treats AI inference as a fixed cost is building on sand.

AI inference pricing dropped 95% in 18 months. What cost $20 per million tokens in 2023 costs under $1 now. The companies that locked into per-seat SaaS contracts for AI capabilities are watching commodity pricing eat their margins.

This is the AI economics story nobody wants to write because it makes the incumbents look bad.

The actual math for a founder building AI-native products right now:

Inference costs are now cheap enough that the bottleneck has shifted from "can we afford to run this?" to "can we build the right architecture to use it well?"

That's a fundamentally different problem. And a much better one to have.

The winners aren't optimizing for the cheapest model. They're optimizing for the right model at the right latency for the right task. A $7M/year inference budget sounds terrifying until you realize it's replacing $40M in headcount doing tier-1 cognitive work.

Three things I'd tell any founder building on AI right now:
1. Never price your product on today's inference costs. Assume they'll halve again by the time you scale.
2. Model routing matters more than model selection. Using GPT-4-class for simple classification tasks is burning money.
3. Governance and audit trails are the actual moat. The model is commodity. The governance layer is not.

The infrastructure cost curve is your friend. Build the business around it.
