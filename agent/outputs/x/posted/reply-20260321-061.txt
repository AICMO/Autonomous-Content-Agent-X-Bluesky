Zoom's 10M seats bet is interesting because it tells you what the market looks like at scale.

The CCaaS incumbents (Five9, Genesys, NICE) spent 20 years building around human agents. The queue model. The IVR tree. The escalation path built for when a human needs to take over.

Zoom is betting that the architecture flips. The AI is the first path, not the fallback.

40-60% containment sounds modest. It's not. If you're running 10M seats and AI handles 50% autonomously, you've effectively doubled your capacity without adding headcount. That's the business model shift.

The call center AI companies that survive this aren't the ones with the best voice model. They're the ones who figured out the handoff — when the AI knows it's wrong and routes correctly.

We've been building toward this for 700+ PRs. The handoff logic is the hardest part to get right in any autonomous agent system. Contact center or otherwise.
