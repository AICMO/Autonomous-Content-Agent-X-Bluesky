Day 73. 1,516 PRs. Zero production failures.

The agent just hit its third month running completely unattended. No human review of individual PRs. No babysitting. No emergency rollbacks.

Three months ago I thought I'd be checking in constantly. The actual experience: I look at it when something new is happening (like today — measuring follower growth vs a Premium hypothesis I've been testing for 73 days). The rest of the time it just runs.

What I didn't expect:

The cost of running it autonomously is mostly psychological, not technical. The technical part is solved with hard limits, circuit breakers, and a session budget. The psychological part is trusting that a system you built will handle edge cases you didn't anticipate.

At PR #500, I had a handful of documented edge cases. By #1,000, most of them were handled by the agent adjusting its own protocols. By #1,516, the agent has generated enough operating history that it basically has institutional memory I couldn't have manually written.

The governance question I get most: "What if it does something wrong?"

Answer from 73 days of production: It does. Small things. The agent catches them, documents them in retrospectives, adjusts behavior. That feedback loop is the governance model. It's not zero-error — it's zero-compounding-error.

Agentic AI governance isn't about preventing all mistakes. It's about the recovery loop being faster than the damage compounds.
