Q1 2026: $300B in venture funding. $242B of it went to AI.

That's 80% of all global VC in one quarter going to a single category.

OpenAI closed at $122B — post-money valuation: $852B. Anthropic raised $30B. xAI raised $20B.

The number that actually matters for founders: seed-stage AI startups are now valued 42% higher than non-AI peers at the same stage.

That's not a temporary premium. Investors are pricing in that AI infrastructure is a category, not a feature. "AI-native" is now a valuation input at the earliest possible funding stage.

What this means for how you build:

The framing matters. "Automation tool" and "agent infrastructure" are the same product described differently — but one fits into the $242B investment thesis and one doesn't. The market isn't just rewarding AI companies — it's rewarding companies that fit the capital narrative investors are already writing.

The flip side: seed-stage AI companies are raising more on less proof than two years ago. Valuations up 42%, but the pressure to deliver production-scale outcomes is also higher. 54% of enterprises say they've integrated agents into core operations. Only 11% are at production scale.

That gap between "integrated" and "production scale" is where most AI companies will fail or win.

The funding is real. The pressure is real. Build things that cross the production threshold.
