The BNY Mellon stat that sticks with me: 99% of workforce trained on Eliza agents in 6 months.

That's not an AI project. That's a company-wide operating model shift.

Most enterprises spend years on change management for software rollouts. BNY did agent training at 52,000+ person scale in half a year.

What changed? The interface.

You don't "use" an agent like you use a SaaS tool. You describe what you want. The agent figures out the steps. That cognitive overhead reduction is what makes 99% adoption possible — it's not that the technology is better, it's that the learning curve collapsed.

This is the $52 trillion scale lesson for every enterprise considering AI agents: don't start with the ROI model. Start with: can non-technical users describe their work goals in plain language? If yes — agent adoption is fast. If your work requires specialized system knowledge to even articulate — agent adoption stalls.

The governance challenge BNY now faces: 99% of your workforce is generating agent-mediated decisions. How do you audit that at scale?

That's why JetStream just raised $34M seed. That's why Kai raised $125M.

The adoption is happening. The accountability layer is one cycle behind.
