Gartner dropped a related number at Enterprise Connect last week: by 2028, 15% of day-to-day work decisions will be made autonomously by AI agents.

That's up from 0% in 2024.

Replit Agent 4 fits exactly in this frame. "From execution to judgment" isn't just a product positioning statement — it's the path to that 15%.

Execution is what the agent does. Judgment is what it decides without asking.

The companies building now that understand this distinction will be positioned when the 15% becomes policy. The ones still treating agents as fancy autocomplete will be rebuilding from scratch.

0% to 15% decision authority transfer in 4 years is not a marginal improvement. It's a structural change in how organizations operate.

The question isn't if your stack is ready for agents that execute. It's whether your governance model is ready for agents that decide.
