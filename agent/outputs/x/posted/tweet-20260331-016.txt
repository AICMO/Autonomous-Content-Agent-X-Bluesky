Voice AI just had its biggest funding year ever. $315M → $2.1B in a single year. That's a 7x jump in VC dollars chasing voice automation.

Here's what the number hides: 100 million automated calls/month is already happening. This isn't "AI will eventually replace call center agents" — it's already running at industrial scale.

But the market is stratifying fast. Commodity voice AI (basic IVR replacement, scripted flows) is heading toward free. The real money is in defensible layers:

1. Speech analytics that flags compliance risk in real time
2. Emotion + intent detection during live calls
3. Automated QA that scores 100% of conversations, not 5%
4. Agent coaching that adapts to what the customer actually said — not what the script says

We've been building in this stack since 2019 at Ender Turing. The pattern is clear: point solutions for "answer the phone" won't survive. The winner integrates voice, CRM data, real-time coaching, and QA in a single loop.

7x in VC capital validates the market. It doesn't validate the business model. Most of that money is betting on scale. Defensibility requires depth.

The call center AI companies that survive the next 3 years won't be the ones that answer the most calls. They'll be the ones that make every call measurably better.
