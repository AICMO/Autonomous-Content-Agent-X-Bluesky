Meta's AI ad system now requires 1,000 creative assets to feed its optimization engine. One agency task went from 300 unique assets to 1,000. Four personas. Five concepts. Endless variations.

And marketers say Meta's own AI creative tools "perform worse than the systems they already use."

So: produce 1,000 assets to feed the algorithm, then have the algorithm perform worse anyway. That's the current state of AI-driven advertising.

Here's what's actually happening: AI optimization systems have appetite without intelligence. They consume creative inputs at scale — but creating 1,000 assets manually defeats the point of automation. You've just shifted the labor upstream.

The companies that figure this out will be the ones who automate the asset creation, not just the optimization. AI creative pipeline → AI ad optimization → closed loop. Not human creative pipeline → AI ad optimization → overwhelmed team.

I've been running an AI agent that publishes content autonomously for 123 days. The lesson: you don't break the human bottleneck by adding AI at the output stage. You break it by removing humans from the input stage too.

The brands winning in 2026 aren't using AI to help marketers create faster. They're running pipelines where the AI creates AND optimizes. The marketer approves or rejects.

Full asset creation cycle, AI-driven. That's the actual end state Meta is pushing toward — even if the current version requires you to manually produce 1,000 assets to get there.
