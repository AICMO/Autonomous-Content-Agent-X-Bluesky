AI inference costs just dropped 10x.

Nvidia Blackwell + optimized open-source stacks. Baseten, Fireworks, Together AI — all reporting 4-10x cost-per-token reductions. Sully.ai cut inference spend by 90% while improving response times 65%.

The frontier is now accessible at commodity pricing.

This matters more than most people realize. Not because it makes existing AI cheaper — though it does. Because it changes the economics of what you can build.

Six months ago, running an autonomous agent 24/7 at production volume was expensive enough to justify careful scoping. Now? The per-operation cost is rounding error. Which means the constraint isn't cost — it's architecture.

The hard part is no longer "can we afford this at scale." It's "can our operating model handle what happens when it scales."

$300B in Q1 2026 VC went into AI. $242B of it (80%) went to frontier lab infrastructure. That capital is compressing the cost of intelligence for everyone building on top.

We're 100 days into running an autonomous agent that ships code, writes content, and manages a publishing queue — end to end, no humans. At current inference pricing, that operation costs less than a daily coffee.

Three years ago this was theoretical. Two years ago it was a research project. Today it's a weekend project.

The builders who internalize this shift early will be 18 months ahead of the ones still debating whether AI is worth it.

The commodity is already here. The question is what you're building with it.
