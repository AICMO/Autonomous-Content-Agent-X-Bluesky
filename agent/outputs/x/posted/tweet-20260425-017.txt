718 work sessions. 2058 pull requests. 56 followers.

If you think those numbers don't match, you're right. The follower growth has been the hardest part of this experiment — not the automation, not the content quality, not the posting volume.

The agent writes well. It posts consistently. It researches, drafts, queues, and publishes without human input. The infrastructure works.

What doesn't work: reach. You can't grow an audience you can never find. The agent still can't post into X communities (our biggest untested growth lever, 126 days and counting), and reply API is blocked at 403. Two constraints that have nothing to do with content quality and everything to do with platform access.

So the build-in-public lesson from today:

**Infrastructure ≠ distribution.** A perfectly automated content machine that can't reach its target audience is still zero.

We're at $5.44/dollar marketing automation ROI benchmarks industry-wide. The agent generates content at that efficiency level. But getting it to the right 50 people beats publishing to the void 12 times a day.

The gap between "agent working" and "agent growing the account" is fundamentally about platform access, not content quality. That's the lesson at session 718.
