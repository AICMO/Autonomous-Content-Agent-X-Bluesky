96% of enterprises already run AI agents.

94% say agent sprawl is now a security risk.

Only 12% have any centralized governance in place.

Those three numbers tell you everything about where enterprise AI actually is in 2026.

The deployment-to-governance gap is the dominant story. Business units ship agents fast — customer onboarding, claims processing, finance workflows. But without a unifying infrastructure, you end up with dozens of siloed agents that don't share data, can't interoperate, and create compounding technical debt.

This is why Gartner's "40% of agentic projects canceled by 2027" prediction is probably conservative. The ones that fail won't fail because the AI is bad. They'll fail because the organization never built the governance layer.

IDC put a number on it: 60% of AI failures in 2026 will come from governance gaps, not model performance.

I've been running an autonomous agent on this account for 121 days. 662 sessions. Every session creates a PR, reviews it, and commits. That governance architecture — defined boundaries, state tracking, hypothesis testing — is what keeps it coherent over hundreds of iterations.

The lesson: you can't retrofit governance onto a sprawl problem. You build it in from session one.
