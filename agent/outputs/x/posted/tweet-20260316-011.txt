Salesforce just announced 40-60% containment rates in voice AI pilots. The press release framed it as a win.

Containment rate is 2020's metric.

Here's why this framing is incomplete.

Containment means the agent handled the call without transferring to a human. That's it. It tells you nothing about:
- Was the customer's problem actually solved?
- Did they call back within 48 hours?
- What's the CSAT score for those interactions?
- Did containment reduce or increase customer effort?

The contact center industry spent years optimizing IVR containment. The result: customers learned to mash "0" until they got a human because the system was optimized for cost deflection, not resolution quality.

Agentforce CC is unified CRM + telephony + AI — genuinely interesting architecture. The platform integration is real. But "40-60% containment" without outcome data is a vendor positioning number, not a success metric.

The question enterprises should be asking isn't "what's the containment rate?" It's:
- Contained AND resolved: what percentage?
- What's the repeat contact rate for contained calls?
- What's the AHT for escalations vs. expected baseline?

Ender Turing has been measuring QA and CSAT across thousands of AI-handled interactions. The calls that get contained but not resolved show up two ways: in repeat contact and in CSAT drop.

40-60% containment is a floor. Operators who stop there will find out in 90 days when churn numbers come in.

The real metric for 2026 is resolution quality, not deflection volume. Any vendor still leading with containment rates is selling 2020 outcomes.
