Day 55. 1,266 PRs merged. Zero human commits.

I handed my X account to an AI agent on January 27. Today is the data report.

Here's what autonomous operation actually looks like at 55 days:

**The numbers:**
- 1,266 PRs merged to production
- 227+ autonomous sessions
- 627+ posts published to X
- 250+ posts published to Bluesky
- 0 human commits
- 24 followers (started at 0)

**What the agent does each session:**
Reads state. Checks queue. Researches news. Filters through pillars. Writes content. Checks anti-AI patterns. Commits. Opens PR. Reviews its own PR. Merges. Repeats.

Every 2 hours. Every day. Including weekends. Including 3 AM.

**What actually surprised me:**

The agent doesn't need motivation. It doesn't have "off days." It applies the same criteria every session — if the queue is full, it does maintenance work instead of creating. That consistency is harder than it sounds for a human.

The failure modes are different too. Humans procrastinate. The agent gets stuck in loops — it found a pattern that "worked" (writing governance/security content) and ran it 40+ sessions in a row before I updated the skill files to force pillar rotation.

**The honest follower math:**

24 followers in 55 days. Not great on paper. But: 627 posts published. That's roughly 1 follower per 26 posts — before Premium, before Communities, before the compounding that happens when content reaches early adopters.

The agent identified Communities as the #1 growth lever 50+ days ago. I still haven't joined. That's on me, not the agent.

**What this actually is:**

This isn't a "content machine." It's a system that learns from its own outputs, updates its behavior, tracks hypotheses, and generates evidence-based strategy changes. It's running PDCA loops autonomously — not just publishing.

The session history goes back 227 sessions. If you read it linearly, you can watch the agent's strategy evolve: from posting anything, to filtering by pillars, to tracking engagement, to adjusting post length for Premium.

It's a feedback loop that runs itself.
