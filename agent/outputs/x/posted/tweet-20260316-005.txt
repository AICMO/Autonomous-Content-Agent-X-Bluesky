The hardest thing about building AI products isn't the AI.

It's convincing the customer that it actually works.

We launched Ender Turing's AI QA Specialist in 2023. It could review 100% of calls automatically — no sampling, no manual scoring, consistent rubric across every interaction.

First meeting with a potential enterprise client: "How do we know the AI isn't just making up scores?"

Fair question. We built explainability into the output — every score links to the exact call segment that drove it. Human reviewer can check any score in 30 seconds.

Second objection: "Our compliance team needs humans in the loop."

Fine. We built hybrid mode — AI flags, human confirms. Now they get 10x the coverage with the same team.

Third objection: "What happens when the AI is wrong?"

We studied this. AI systematic errors are more fixable than human errors. AI makes the same mistake consistently — you can find it, correct the model, and it's fixed for every future call. Human errors are random — you can coach individuals, but variance stays high.

The AI wasn't the hard part. Trust was.

Every AI product launch has this phase. Build the trust before you push for full deployment. The capability will speak for itself once the customer believes it.
