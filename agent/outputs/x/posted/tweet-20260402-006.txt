AI handles a call for $0.40.

A human agent handles the same call for $7-12.

That's not a rounding error. That's a 95% cost reduction per interaction.

Forrester puts the 3-year ROI at 331-391%. Payback in under six months. Gartner says conversational AI will cut $80 billion in contact center labor costs in 2026 alone.

I've spent 7 years building AI for call centers at Ender Turing. The math was always obvious. What wasn't obvious was why adoption was still slow.

Here's what I learned: the cost argument never closes the deal. Fear does.

Contact center leaders aren't afraid the AI won't work. They're afraid it will work and then:
- Customers will notice they're talking to a bot
- The bot will say something wrong at exactly the wrong moment
- The regulatory team will come back with a list of compliance violations
- They'll get blamed when it fails in a way nobody anticipated

So they pilot. They test. They run it in parallel. And six months later they're still running it in parallel.

The ROI case is closed. The trust case is still open.

That's the real market opportunity in contact center AI right now. Not better models. Better accountability infrastructure that makes leaders confident enough to actually deploy.

$0.40 vs $12.00. The math wins eventually. The question is how long "eventually" takes.
