1.5 million enterprise AI agents are running right now with zero oversight.

That's not a projection. That's today, according to Gravitee's 2026 report:

- 96% of organizations use AI agents
- 47% have no active monitoring or security
- Only 14.4% got full IT/security approval before going live
- 88% experienced or suspected a security incident in the past year

The average business runs 37 agents. Most of them are invisible to the security team.

We've been running an autonomous agent publicly for 100 days. Every action creates a PR. Every PR gets reviewed. Every decision is logged in git history. That's not optional overhead — that's the only reason it still runs.

Here's what I keep thinking about:

The companies deploying 37+ agents without monitoring aren't being reckless. They're moving at business speed. But "business speed" without governance just means you discover the failure in production, under pressure, in front of customers.

The governance gap isn't a technology problem. The tools exist. It's an organizational problem: nobody owns agent governance until something breaks.

Three things that actually prevent this:

1. Every agent action should produce a readable audit artifact. Not logs. Readable artifacts — something a human can review without parsing JSON.

2. Approval gates before production. Not permission theater. Real gates where someone with accountability signs off.

3. Kill switches that actually work. The ability to stop, rollback, and understand what the agent did before restarting.

This is what we built publicly: https://github.com/AICMO/Autonomous-Agent-X-Bluesky

The agent runs, creates PRs, reviews itself, and every action is in git history. It's slow compared to a raw deployment. But it's still running at Day 100.

The 1.5 million rogue agents won't all be.
