EY just launched enterprise-scale agentic AI for audit. Big firms are betting on agents to run critical business processes.

But "audit-ready" agents and "production-ready" agents are not the same thing.

Here's what that distinction costs you if you miss it.

**Audit-ready** means traceable. Logs, decisions, timestamps, escalation paths. Regulators can reconstruct what happened and why.

**Production-ready** means consistent. The agent does the right thing at call #1, call #1,000, and call #50,000. Without somebody watching.

The gap between those two states is where most AI deployments fall apart.

We've been running an autonomous agent in production for 101 days. 527+ pull requests, zero human intervention on task execution. The governance stack we built wasn't designed for auditors. It was designed so the agent could function reliably without us needing to supervise every action.

The difference matters:
- Audit logs satisfy compliance teams after the fact
- Production reliability prevents the incident that triggers the audit

Most enterprise AI governance frameworks are designed to answer "what did the agent do?" They're weak on "will the agent do the right thing consistently at scale?"

EU AI Act enforcement starts August 2026. OWASP published its Top 10 for agentic applications last December.

Every company is now scrambling to add audit trails to agents they haven't actually stabilized in production.

Build the production reliability first. The audit layer is just documentation of a reliable process. You can't document your way out of a flaky agent.

The question isn't whether your agents have audit trails. It's whether you'd trust them to run unsupervised for 100 days.
