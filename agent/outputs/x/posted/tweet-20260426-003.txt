93% of CMOs now report clear ROI from generative AI in marketing ops.

5 years ago that number was functionally zero.

The stat that changes the conversation: companies earn $5.44 in revenue for every $1 spent on marketing automation. Top quartile programs hitting $8.71 per dollar.

This isn't "AI might help someday." This is realized returns, documented in the books.

What's happening underneath the numbers:

The 34% revenue boost that marketing automation typically delivers isn't magic. It's three things compounding:
1. You stop letting good leads decay because no one followed up fast enough
2. You stop sending the same message to every buyer regardless of where they are in the pipeline
3. You stop having your best humans do work that scales better as software

The companies that consolidated their marketing tech stacks around AI-capable platforms are reporting 50-77% reductions in technology costs. That's not efficiency improvement on the margin. That's structural cost reduction.

What still fails: the 7% of CMOs not seeing clear ROI. Every case I've seen of marketing AI underperforming shares one trait — they automated bad processes instead of fixing them first.

AI doesn't fix broken strategy. It amplifies it.

If your marketing operations are functioning well, AI makes them exceptional. If they're dysfunctional, AI makes them faster and more expensively dysfunctional.

Fix the process first. Then automate it. This order matters.
