4 companies raised $188 billion in Q1 2026.

OpenAI: $122B. Anthropic: $30B. xAI: $20B. Waymo: $16B.

That's 65% of all global venture capital in a single quarter — concentrated in four organizations.

Global VC hit $300 billion total in Q1. AI took $242 billion of that. 80% of all venture investment worldwide, up from 55% a year ago.

---

What does this actually mean for founders building AI products?

The short version: the frontier is being funded out of reach.

Most AI startups aren't competing with OpenAI for the foundation model crown — and if you are, you're in the wrong fight. The real opportunity is the application layer, the vertical integrations, the workflows that become 10x better when AI is embedded correctly.

But here's what the VC concentration tells you: investor capital is chasing certainty. The mega-rounds are going to companies where the TAM is so large that even a small market share produces a venture return. That clarity is rare. Most founders can't point to a $100B+ market they're credibly attacking.

---

For everyone not OpenAI, the inference economics are the real story.

GPT-4 equivalent performance: $20 per million tokens in late 2022. $0.40 per million tokens now. That's a 50x price drop in the public numbers. 1,000x over three years when you track the full arc.

But your enterprise AI bill probably didn't go down. It went up.

Because agentic workflows consume 5-30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot query. The per-token cost collapsed. The per-workflow cost stayed flat or rose.

This is the inference paradox: costs per unit are vanishing, costs per outcome are sticky.

---

AI startups at seed stage are valued at $17.9M median pre-money. Growth stage: $143M median.

That's a 3.2x premium over traditional tech companies at equivalent stages. The premium exists because investors expect AI companies to grow faster, compress margins faster, and achieve defensibility faster.

Most won't. The ones that do will be the ones that understood their inference unit economics before scaling.
