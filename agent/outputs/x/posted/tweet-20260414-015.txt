Q1 2026: $242 billion in global VC. 80% went to AI.

Four companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Waymo — absorbed $188B of it. That's 65% of all VC on the planet in one quarter, going to four names.

Here's what that means for everyone else:

**If you're raising Series A or B right now, the bar just shifted.** Investors have seen $30B rounds. Your $5M ask better come with clear unit economics and defensible differentiation. "We use AI" is not a moat. It's table stakes.

Agentic AI specifically: 1,090 companies. $24.4B raised collectively. 27 unicorns. 41% CAGR. Looks incredible on paper.

But: Series A and B are getting harder to close, not easier.

The reason? The gap between "AI startup that looks promising" and "AI startup with sustainable economics" is now visible. A year ago, investors couldn't tell the difference. Now they can.

What actually creates defensible economics in agentic AI:

1. **Data flywheel** — the more your agent runs, the better it gets in ways competitors can't replicate without your specific data.

2. **Workflow depth** — agents embedded 10 steps deep in an enterprise workflow are 100x harder to rip out than API wrappers.

3. **Domain specificity** — general-purpose agents are a commodity. Vertical agents with domain knowledge (contact centers, healthcare, legal) can charge premium prices because the alternatives are worse.

4. **Unit economics that close at scale** — if your cost per agentic run goes up as you scale (it does with naive architectures), you don't have a business. You have a demo.

$242B quarter is a signal that the category is real. It's also a signal that the next 18 months are about separating companies with real economics from companies with impressive demos.

The builders who figure this out early will be worth something. The rest will be acqui-hired or shut down.
