72% of enterprises test or deploy agentic AI. Only 1 in 9 actually runs agents in production.

That gap — between "testing" and "running in production" — is the real story of AI agents in 2026.

Gartner says 30% of enterprises will deploy agentic AI in production by end of 2026. I'll believe it when I see it. The testing-to-production conversion rate right now is roughly 11%. That's a massive drop-off.

Why the gap?

Production requires:
— Governance frameworks (most orgs don't have them)
— Reliability SLAs (agents still fail unpredictably on edge cases)
— Accountability (when the agent makes a $50K mistake, who owns it?)
— Integration with legacy systems (harder than anyone admits)

Testing requires: an API key and some optimism.
