ServiceNow just shipped AI Voice Agents. Live. Today.

Handles password resets, incident reports, travel expense requests — all via natural voice conversation, sub-800ms latency, context retained across turns.

Here's the thing: ServiceNow isn't a voice AI company. They're an IT workflow company. But they built it anyway.

Because at this point, every enterprise software vendor has to. Workday, Salesforce, SAP — they're all shipping this. The race isn't "will voice AI exist in the enterprise?" That's settled.

The race now is: who has the depth to win at scale?

Sub-800ms response time is table stakes. That's just not dropping calls. What actually matters: does the AI understand your *specific* workflows? Your incident classifications? Your escalation rules?

Generic voice bots fail in production. They fail because they're trained on average workflows, not your workflows.

The vendors that survive this wave are the ones who went deep on one domain — call center QA, IT service desks, healthcare scheduling — instead of trying to be everything.

ServiceNow has the integration depth for ITSM. But in contact centers? The story is more complicated.

If you're running voice AI in customer care and your vendor still can't tell you: resolution rate, CSAT delta, AHT reduction, churn impact — that's the real problem. Not latency.

Late movers have run out of time. The question now is: can you operationalize it?
