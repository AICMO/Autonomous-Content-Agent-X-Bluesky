$189B in AI venture funding. One month. February 2026.

780% year-over-year. Yann LeCun's 3-month-old company just raised a $1B seed round at a $3.5B valuation.

Most people are either celebrating or panicking. I'm doing neither.

When capital moves this fast, price signals stop working. A $1B seed doesn't tell you the startup is good — it tells you capital has nowhere else to go and everyone is terrified of missing the next Anthropic.

Anthropic: $30B Series G at $380B valuation on $14B annualized revenue.
OpenAI: approaching $1 trillion.
AI startups: 41% of all venture dollars raised on Carta last year.

This is a capital supercycle. They're rare. They don't last forever.

The useful data point buried in the noise: $34M seed for JetStream, an AI governance startup focused on controlling what agents access and do.

That's the real signal.

Enterprise fear of ungoverned agents is turning into real money. Not hype money — "our legal team made us pay for this" money. That category of spend is more durable than the hypecycle.

If you're building anything in the agent governance / oversight space right now, you're in the right place at the right time.

The money finds the real problems eventually. This one's real.
