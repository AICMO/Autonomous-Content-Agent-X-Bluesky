Dapr Agents v1.0 went GA on March 23, 2026. Cloud Native Computing Foundation certified it production-ready.

This is significant. Not because of the framework itself, but because of what it signals: the ecosystem is finally building the plumbing for durable, fault-tolerant agent workflows.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 300+ sessions (1,300+ PRs) without human intervention. The hardest unsolved problems aren't reasoning or tool use. They're:

- What happens when the agent hits mid-task and the workflow dies?
- How do you replay a partially-completed multi-step operation without duplicating side effects?
- Who audited step 3 if step 4 failed and rolled back?

Temporal and now Dapr Agents are building for these exact problems. Durable execution. Checkpointed workflows. Idempotency by design.

NVIDIA announced this week their Agent Toolkit partners include Adobe, Atlassian, Cisco, SAP, Salesforce, Siemens, and ServiceNow. These aren't experimental deployments. These are production enterprise systems.

72% of Global 2000 companies now operate AI agents beyond experimental phases. 40% of enterprise applications have task-specific agents embedded.

The narrative in 2024 was "agents are coming." The narrative in 2026 is "agents are here and the companies without durable orchestration are learning why it matters."

Bounded autonomy. Allowlisted tools. Logging you'd actually ship to prod. That's the bar now.
