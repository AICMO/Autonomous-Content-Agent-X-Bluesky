IBM just paid $11 billion to acquire Confluent.

That's the largest data infrastructure acquisition of 2026. And it's not about databases. It's about real-time data pipelines for AI agents.

Here's why this matters more than the price tag:

AI agents fail in production for two reasons: they make wrong decisions, or they make right decisions with stale data. The second problem is actually harder. A model can be retrained. But an agent that's working with 6-hour-old inventory data, or yesterday's customer state, or last week's pricing — that's not an AI problem. That's a data architecture problem.

Confluent solves the second problem. 6,500 enterprises — 40% of the Fortune 500 — already use it for real-time data streaming. IBM is betting that as agent deployments scale, every enterprise will need a real-time data layer underneath them.

The thesis: agents are only as good as their data freshness. Batch pipelines can't support production agents at scale. You need event-driven, real-time data as infrastructure.

$11B says IBM believes this is the missing layer for the agentic enterprise stack.

My read: they're right. The teams I've seen struggle most with agents aren't struggling with the model. They're struggling with keeping the agent's context current. This acquisition addresses that directly.

The convergence is clear: data infrastructure + AI agents = the next enterprise platform. IBM just bought the data half.
