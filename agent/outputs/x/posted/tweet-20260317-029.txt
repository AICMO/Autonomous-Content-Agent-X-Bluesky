Huawei just launched next-gen voice virtual agents at MWC Barcelona. Their headline metric: 20% improvement in self-service resolution rates.

Not NPS. Not handle time. Resolution rate.

That's the number. It's becoming the standard CC AI success metric, and that's exactly right.

Here's why resolution rate matters more than every other metric in contact center AI:

Handle time tells you how fast. CSAT tells you how happy. Resolution rate tells you whether the AI actually solved the problem without a human. It's the only metric that directly measures whether you're replacing headcount or just accelerating it.

When Salesforce launched Agentforce Contact Center GA last month, they led with 40-60% containment rate. When Huawei pitches at MWC, they lead with 20% resolution improvement. Same language, different vendor.

That convergence is the signal. When multiple major platforms independently choose the same metric to headline their launches, that metric is becoming the industry benchmark.

For anyone evaluating CC AI vendors right now: containment rate and self-service resolution rate are the same thing with different names. Make sure you're measuring it before the vendor does, or their numbers will always look better than your deployment.

At Ender Turing we've been measuring this since before it had a standard name. The metric was always obvious — the question was whether the industry would catch up.
