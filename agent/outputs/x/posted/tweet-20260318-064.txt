Gartner just dropped the most important prediction in contact center AI: by 2030, GenAI cost per resolution will EXCEED the cost of an offshore human agent.

Read that again. The technology being sold as "cheaper than humans" is projected to become MORE expensive.

Here's the actual math:
- Current GenAI: $1.84 median cost per self-service resolution
- Offshore human agent today: ~$2-3 per interaction
- Gartner 2030 GenAI forecast: $3+ per resolution

The gap is closing. Then inverting.

Why? Nobody's talking about the hidden costs:
- RAG infrastructure (you need a knowledge base, you need to maintain it)
- Governance and compliance overhead (especially in regulated industries)
- Orchestration complexity (routing, fallback, escalation logic)
- Monitoring and quality assurance
- AI vendor margin expansion (they'll raise prices as adoption locks you in)
- Human fallback costs that never fully go away

The standard pitch is: "Replace offshore agents at $18/hour with AI at $X." This assumes the denominator stays fixed. It doesn't.

Gartner's Emily Potosky put it directly: "We are investing in a more expensive technology solution to replace a less expensive talent source."

So what's the actual ROI case for CC AI? Not cost replacement. Three things:
1. Volume handling — AI handles 40-60% containment on routine queries that would require human time anyway
2. Quality uplift — every remaining human interaction is better documented, analyzed, coached
3. Revenue impact — CSAT improvements, faster resolution, reduced churn

We've been building this since 2019. The companies winning with CC AI aren't running "replace humans" programs. They're running "make every human 3x more effective" programs.

The "AI is cheaper" narrative is getting shorter legs. The "AI enables outcomes humans can't achieve alone" narrative is getting longer ones.

Gartner said it. The data is saying it. The companies that built on the cost narrative will need a new story by 2028.
