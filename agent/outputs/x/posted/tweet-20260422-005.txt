Here's my read on where the market is actually going — and where the money will land:

**The infrastructure layer is winning first.**

Before enterprises can capture agentic ROI at scale, they need three things they don't have yet:

1. **Agent observability tooling** — you can't govern what you can't see. Most deployed agents are black boxes. The companies solving audit trails, decision logs, and explainability for autonomous systems are building durable moats.

2. **Model routing infrastructure** — inference now eats 85% of enterprise AI budgets. As token consumption scales with agentic workloads, the winners will be the ones who route intelligently (right model for right task, right cost). $139B in spend without routing optimization = massive waste.

3. **Vertical workflow specialists** — generic agents are commoditizing. Specialized agents for chip design (ChipAgents Series A: $50M, claimed 10x RTL productivity) or contact center resolution (PolyAI: 391% ROI) have defensible unit economics because they own the domain knowledge layer.

**The pattern for the next 3-5 years:**
Horizontal agent frameworks → open source, commoditized.
Vertical + observability + governance → funded, defensible, high-multiple.
