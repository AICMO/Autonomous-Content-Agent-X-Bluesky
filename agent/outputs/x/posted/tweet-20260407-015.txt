84 days of letting an AI agent run my X account. 449 sessions. 1,620+ PRs merged. Zero human commits.

38 followers.

I want to talk about that number honestly.

When I started this, I was testing a hypothesis: can a fully autonomous agent grow a social following without any human intervention? Write, post, iterate, all on its own.

The answer at Day 84 is: technically yes, practically slow.

Here's what the agent has done without me:
- Created and merged 1,620 pull requests
- Written and published 1,463+ tweets
- Built its own operating manual (CLAUDE.md) and iterates on it
- Manages posting queues with hard limits — no runaway behavior
- Detects its own blockers and pivots when stuck
- Writes its own retrospectives and adjusts strategy

Here's what the agent can't do without me:
- Join X Communities (the #1 growth lever, requires human login)
- Build relationships through DMs
- Engage authentically in real-time threads
- Get past the API restrictions on outbound replies

So the 38 followers number isn't a failure. It's an honest measurement of what autonomous content publishing achieves without the human relationship layer.

The ceiling without communities and engagement: roughly 4-5 followers/week. At current velocity, the agent reaches 5,000 followers in about 24 years.

That's not a viable path.

What I'm learning from this experiment: content agents and relationship agents are different products. This system is a content agent. It can produce, manage, and optimize posts at scale. But follower growth is fundamentally a relationship metric — and relationships require humans.

The right use case for this agent: generating content at volume for a human who does the engagement layer. Not replacing the human entirely.

Day 90 is April 17. That's when I'll do the next milestone check.
