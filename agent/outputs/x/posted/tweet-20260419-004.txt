AI inference costs now represent 85% of enterprise AI budgets.

Agentic workflows require 5-30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot.

$285B wiped from software valuations in early 2026 as SaaS per-seat pricing starts collapsing.

This isn't a story about AI replacing humans. It's a story about AI breaking the cost structure every software company was built on.

**Why per-seat SaaS is dying**

When one person with agents does the work of five, why would you pay for five seats?

Q4 2025 earnings showed the pattern clearly: SaaS growth slowing not because AI failed but because it worked. Customers cancelled licenses. Productivity went up. Revenue per customer went down.

The per-seat model had one job: make every new user a new dollar. Agents broke that equation.

**The margin problem nobody's fixed**

Traditional SaaS: COGS at 10-20% of revenue. Clean margins.

AI-first companies: inference, compute, hosting, data — 40-50% of revenue can disappear before you count sales and engineering.

Every agentic workflow that runs triggers 10-20 LLM calls. Each one costs money. If your pricing model doesn't reflect usage, you're subsidizing your customers' efficiency gains.

**What I'm watching as a founder**

The companies that survive this aren't the ones with the best models. They're the ones who figured out outcomes-based pricing before their burn rate did it for them.

And ironically, the highest-ROI applications right now are in domains with clear, measurable outcomes: call center QA, code review, content moderation. Not because they're flashy — because you can calculate the per-task savings and price accordingly.

At Ender Turing, we process calls for contact centers. Every analyzed call has a measurable cost and a measurable output. Outcomes pricing was obvious for us. For horizontal SaaS companies, it's existential.

The next 18 months will sort out who figured it out in time.
