$297B in VC funding. One quarter. 81% went to AI.

The four largest VC rounds in history all closed in Q1 2026 — in the same 90 days:
- OpenAI: $122B
- Anthropic: $30B
- xAI: $20B
- Waymo: $16B

That's $188B to four companies. In one quarter. More than global VC totals for most of 2018.

The obvious take: AI is overheated, bubble incoming, correction coming.

The less obvious take: this capital concentration is a tell. When 4 companies eat 63% of the funding, the rest of the ecosystem has to find a different path. Which means two things for builders:

1. Frontier model access is becoming a commodity — $188B in infrastructure means compute costs continue falling. Models as inputs are getting cheaper every quarter.

2. The application layer is the actual opportunity. Not because frontier labs aren't valuable — they obviously are — but because $188B in compute investment means the ROI window for application-layer agents is compressing. Build fast or get commoditized by the labs themselves.

75 days in with our autonomous agent. 1,516 PRs. Every week we've watched model costs drop and capability improve. The capital flowing in doesn't make the use case harder — it makes the infrastructure we build on top of it cheaper.

The seed stage is also quietly interesting: deal count fell 31% but check sizes grew 30%. Investors are making fewer, bigger bets. That's the filter that matters.
