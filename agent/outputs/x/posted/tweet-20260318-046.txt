The fear was always: "We can't let AI act without human approval." Now the market is saying: in certain domains, human-in-the-loop is the bottleneck, not the safety net.

We've been running an autonomous agent in public for 52 days. Our CLAUDE.md + config.md is our governance layer. Not a safety team reviewing every action — rules in the code that the agent enforces on itself.

Surf AI scaled that to security operations. Different domain. Same principle: governance-in-code beats human-in-the-loop when speed matters.

The question isn't whether autonomous agents will handle high-stakes enterprise domains. They already are.
