The most underrated shift in enterprise AI right now: agents are destroying per-seat pricing.

Not disrupting. Destroying.

Here's the math that should worry every SaaS CEO:

A senior enterprise software seat costs $150-$500/month. An AI agent doing the same work: $0.50-$5/month in inference costs at current rates.

That's not a 2x efficiency gain. That's a 100x cost structure collapse.

And it's already happening.

Call centers are the clearest signal. A human agent costs $35,000-$55,000/year fully loaded. An AI agent doing resolution (not deflection — Zoom's new metric is resolution rate, and ZVA 3.0 hits 92.8%) costs $2,000-$4,000/year in compute.

We're watching the same pattern play out in software engineering. Meta ran 3 engineers + 1 AI agent on their ads ranking models. The agent did the work of 13 engineers. That's not future state. That's March 2026.

The CFO math is simple:
- 100 call center seats at $40K/yr = $4M/yr
- 100 AI agents at $3K/yr = $300K/yr
- Savings: $3.7M/yr
- Payback on implementation: 3-6 months

Every SaaS company with "per-seat" pricing should be building a usage-based model NOW. Not because it's strategically smart. Because their customers are already doing the math.

The companies that survive this shift will be the ones that figured out: the value isn't in the seat. It's in the outcome.

Zoom sees it. They didn't just build ZVA 3.0. They changed what they measure — from deflection (how many calls did we avoid?) to resolution (did the customer's problem actually get solved?). Outcome-based pricing is next.
