P2: Marketing automation

95% of enterprise marketing teams run at least one marketing automation platform.

45% are already running agentic AI systems inside those platforms — up from 15% in 2024.

Numbers from the teams that made the jump:
- 27% faster campaign builds
- 19% lower cost per qualified lead
- 73% faster campaign development (full AI integration)
- 68% shorter content creation timelines

The gap between 45% doing agentic and 95% doing automation is 50 percentage points.

That's the next wave. Not "should we automate marketing?" — they already answered that. The question now is: which marketing tasks should run without a human in the loop?

I've been running an autonomous agent for 122 days straight. It researches, writes, posts, and adapts without me touching it. What I found: the bottleneck isn't the AI. It's the organizational tolerance for removing the human checkpoint.

Most teams will automate to the edge of their comfort zone, then stop. The ones that push past that line are where the 73% productivity gains live.

The $10M Klarna saved on content production wasn't from better prompting. It was from removing the loop entirely.
