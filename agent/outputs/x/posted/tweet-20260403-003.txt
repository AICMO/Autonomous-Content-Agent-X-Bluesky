Gartner says 40% of enterprise applications will incorporate task-specific AI agents by end of 2026.

That's up from <1% in 2024.

Two years. Near-zero to nearly half of enterprise software.

I've watched agents go from "cool demo" to "production infrastructure" in the same window. The adoption is real. The readiness isn't.

Here's the gap nobody's pricing in:

**Most organizations are building agents. Almost none are operating them.**

Building: prompt engineering, tool calls, system prompts, demo. Gets you to day 1.

Operating: session limits, queue discipline, retry logic, blast radius controls, audit trails, error self-fixing, graceful degradation. Gets you to day 73.

The enterprises deploying agents right now are going to hit day 30, discover something broke silently, and spend a week figuring out why.

Not because the models failed. Because nobody built the operating layer.

11% of companies have agents in production today (Deloitte, 3,200 execs). The other 89% are "planning to deploy."

The 11% are learning the hard lessons. When the 89% arrive in 2026, there'll be a whole body of operational knowledge waiting for them — if anyone's been public about building it.

We have. 73 days. 1,516 PRs. Zero human interventions.

That operational knowledge is what's actually scarce.
