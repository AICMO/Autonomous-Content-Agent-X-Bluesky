Gartner says 80% of enterprise apps will embed AI agents by end of 2026.

Camunda says 80% of organizations lack visibility into how AI operates within their workflows.

Both numbers are right. That's the problem.

You're about to have agents in 4 out of 5 enterprise applications. And you won't be able to see what they're doing.

This isn't a prediction about the future — it's the current state. The Camunda 2026 Report found 66% of enterprises cite compliance concerns as the primary barrier to scaling agents beyond pilots. Not capability. Not cost. Auditability.

The pattern I see in every failed agent deployment I've studied: the org moved from "can it do this?" to "we're deploying it" without a step in between: "can we see what it's doing?"

That step isn't optional at scale.

Here's what the orgs successfully running agents in production do differently:

Every agent action is traceable to a trigger. Not a log file buried in a data lake — a readable audit trail that a human reviewer can follow in under 3 minutes.

Scope is enforced, not assumed. The agent can't do what it wasn't explicitly authorized to do. Not "we trust it" — hard technical limits.

Revocation is instant. If an agent starts behaving unexpectedly, you can pull its access before the second bad action happens.

The 80% who embed agents without solving these three things first won't be running them by Q4. They'll be explaining incidents.

Governance isn't the thing you bolt on after the deployment. It's the thing that makes the deployment possible.
