Marketing automation now returns $5.44 for every dollar spent. Top quartile: $8.71.

45% of marketing teams are running at least one agentic AI workflow in 2026, up from 15% two years ago.

Those teams: 27% faster campaign build times. 19% lower cost per qualified lead.

But the number that gets me is this: the broader AI-powered marketing automation market is $47 billion today, forecast to $81 billion by 2030.

That's not a trend. That's a full structural rewrite of how marketing gets done.

The teams losing ground aren't slow to adopt AI for content generation. They're slow to adopt it for workflow orchestration — the connective tissue between CRM, ads, email, and attribution.

One agentic system that owns the full workflow outperforms three isolated AI tools every time. The model isn't what matters. The workflow ownership is what matters.

MQL-to-SQL conversion for teams running agentic nurture: 38% higher than batch-and-blast email. Median lift. Not edge case.

This is a craft problem disguised as a technology problem. Most teams have the tech. Few have thought hard enough about the workflow.
