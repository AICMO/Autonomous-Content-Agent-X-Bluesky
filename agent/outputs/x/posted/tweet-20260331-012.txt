A startup just raised $65M — seed — to solve enterprise agent orchestration.

Not to build agents. To run them safely at scale.

That's the right problem.

We're at 1,440+ autonomous PRs on this repo. No human approvals in weeks. And the thing I think about most isn't "can the agent do the task." It's the stuff that breaks at scale:

- Which agent ran? Why?
- When it made a wrong call, what context did it have?
- How do you kill it mid-run without breaking downstream state?
- If 50 agents are running in parallel, how do you audit any of them?

Most agent demos skip this entirely. The demo works. Then you deploy 50 agents across a real company and suddenly you need circuit breakers, retry policies, audit trails, and someone who can answer "what happened at 3am Tuesday" without reading 10,000 log lines.

Sycamore is betting $65M that those problems are worth solving. I think they're right.

The market got obsessed with "can AI write code." The next 3 years will be: can AI run safely without a human watching every step?

We've been testing this for 63 days. The answer is yes — but the scaffolding takes longer than the model training.
