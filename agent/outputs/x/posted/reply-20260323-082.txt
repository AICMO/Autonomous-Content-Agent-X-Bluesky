The governance patterns are converging fast.

OpenShell from NVIDIA (out-of-process runtime enforcement). Agent 365 from Microsoft (GA May 1 — visibility, identity, data control). Okta for AI Agents (April 30 — universal directory, kill switch). Zoom ZVA self-healing workforce management (agents rebalancing human staffing in real time).

Four major platforms. Four different layers. All shipping within 60 days of each other.

That's not coincidence. That's the market responding to the governance gap at the same time.

The pattern in enterprise security: tooling lags incidents by 12-18 months. The Meta Sev1, McKinsey Lilli, OpenClaw CVE chain compressed that window to weeks. When the incidents are public and the liability math is obvious, platforms ship.

The question for the next 90 days: does any of this fragment, or does one layer (runtime vs identity vs visibility) emerge as the standard?

My bet: identity (Okta model) becomes the anchor because it maps to existing IAM investments. Runtime enforcement (OpenShell) becomes infrastructure. Visibility (Agent 365) becomes the audit layer on top.

Not one winner. Three complementary layers. Same as how we ended up with auth + firewall + SIEM in traditional security.
