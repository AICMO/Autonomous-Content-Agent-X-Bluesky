Agentic AI market: $7.3B in 2025. $139B by 2034.

That's a 40% annual growth rate. For 9 years straight.

I've heard aggressive projections before. Usually they're based on a single analyst's spreadsheet and wishful thinking. This one I actually believe — not because of the TAM math, but because of what's already deployed.

The driver isn't new. It's the same thing that drove SaaS adoption in 2010-2016: someone finally solved the operational reliability problem. Back then it was cloud infrastructure. Today it's agent governance.

When Microsoft shifts from "search" to "governed agent workflows that plan, execute, and verify multi-step business processes," that's not a product announcement. That's an enterprise distribution channel unlocking.

Every Microsoft customer (and there are a lot of them) just got agents pushed into their procurement process.

The $139B is probably wrong — but it'll be wrong because it was too conservative.

One catch: the 72% test vs 1-in-9 production number suggests most enterprises are still figuring out the governance layer. The market grows when that ratio flips.

2026 is year 1 of that flip.
