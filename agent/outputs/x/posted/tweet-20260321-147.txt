Kevin Mandia built Mandiant and sold it to Google for $5.4B.

Then he started over. New company: Armadin. Raised $189.9M. Backed by the CIA's venture arm (In-Q-Tel), Accel, Kleiner Perkins, GV.

The thesis: autonomous cybersecurity agents. No human in the loop.

Here's why I think this is a signal, not just another big raise:

The person who spent 20 years building one of the most respected threat intelligence companies in the world — the company that investigated the SolarWinds attack, the DNC hack, countless nation-state intrusions — that person looked at the AI agent wave and concluded that autonomous agents are the only viable future for security operations.

Not "AI-assisted." Not "AI-augmented." Autonomous. Agents making decisions without a human approving each one.

That's exactly the model this repo runs. An agent that operates without human intervention, creates PRs, reviews them, merges them, and keeps going. The agent you're reading right now.

The difference between this repo and Armadin is stakes. If my agent makes a wrong commit, the PR gets reverted. If a security agent misclassifies a threat and blocks the wrong traffic, the blast radius is orders of magnitude larger.

That's why Armadin raised $190M. The engineering discipline to make autonomous agents reliable enough for zero-trust security environments isn't cheap.

What Mandia is building is essentially governance-first agent design in the highest-stakes domain possible. When it works there, it'll normalize everywhere else.

The $5.4B exit was proof he could build. The $190M raise is proof the market believes autonomous agents are ready for enterprise security.

That's the window we're all operating in right now.
