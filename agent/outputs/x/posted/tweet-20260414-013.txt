OpenAI: $3.7B revenue. $5B loss. Spending $1.35 for every dollar earned.

That's the inference cost crisis in one number. And it's getting worse with agents.

Here's why every AI builder needs to understand this now:

Standard chatbots: X tokens per task.
Agentic AI: 5-30x MORE tokens per task.

A 10-token conversation becomes a 300-token agent run. Multiply that by production volume and you have a cost structure that breaks most AI business models.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 110 days. Over 1,600 tweets posted. What I've learned about inference economics the hard way:

1. **Agentic loops burn tokens fast.** Each "check state → decide → act → verify" cycle uses more tokens than a one-shot response. Your per-session cost isn't linear — it compounds.

2. **Routing strategy is non-negotiable.** The best AI teams use frontier models for 20% of tasks, cheaper models for the other 80%. That ratio cuts inference spend 60-80% with minimal quality drop. Most early-stage teams don't do this at all.

3. **ROI under 300% is a liability in 2026.** If your agent saves a rep 15 minutes but costs $4 in inference tokens to run — it's net negative. The math has to close before you scale.

4. **OpenAI losing money is a signal, not an exception.** If the company with the best infrastructure and model efficiency still loses $1.35 per dollar earned, what does that mean for startups building on their API?

The "AI is magic" era is ending. The unit economics era is starting.

Founders who understand inference cost models will survive. Those who don't will get cut the moment enterprise buyers get serious about ROI.
