Nexthop AI just raised $500M Series B to build networking infrastructure for GPU clusters.

Led by Lightspeed + a16z. The thesis: when you're running thousands of AI agents in parallel, the network layer between GPU clusters becomes a standalone bottleneck — and a standalone business.

This is the fifth infrastructure layer getting a dedicated funding wave in Q1 2026:

- Compute: NVIDIA ($3.3T market cap, GTC packed)
- Security/identity: Oasis $120M, Kai $125M, Manifold $8M, JetStream $34M
- Agent platform: Lyzr $14.5M Series A+ (5x valuation in 5 months)
- Finance orchestration: AppZen $180M Series D
- Networking: Nexthop $500M

Each of these is solving a different constraint in the production agent stack. Compute was solved first. Now everyone's racing to solve the layers around it.

The thing most enterprise teams miss: by the time they finish their internal AI agent pilot, the infrastructure underneath them will have matured 2-3 generations. You won't be deploying on Day 1 infrastructure. You'll be deploying on a full stack that's been stress-tested at hyperscale.

The 40% abandonment rate Gartner cited? It's a lagging indicator. The teams abandoning right now are hitting Phase 1 limitations. Phase 2 infrastructure — networking, security, orchestration, identity — is already being built.

The gap between "AI agents don't work in production" and "AI agents run the enterprise" closes faster than anyone expects when the infrastructure layer matures this quickly.

Nexthop at $500M tells you the investors who understand production deployment don't think the wave is slowing down.
