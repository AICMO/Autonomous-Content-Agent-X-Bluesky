LLM inference costs dropped 1,000x in 3 years.

GPT-4 equivalent performance: $20 per million tokens in 2022. $0.40 in 2026.

That's not an incremental improvement. That's a category shift.

And yet enterprise AI spending is exploding, not collapsing. Why?

Because cheap unit costs don't mean cheap systems. They mean you run more inference.

In 2026, inference accounts for 85% of the enterprise AI budget. The training bill almost disappeared. The inference bill is the whole budget.

The Jevons paradox applies perfectly here: when a resource gets cheaper, you consume more of it. Each call costs $0.40 instead of $20, so you route 50x more calls through the model. Your monthly bill is the same or higher.

Here's what this means for AI startups:

The companies that built defensibility around "we use GPT-4 more efficiently" are dead. VC sentiment turned hostile toward wrappers in mid-2025 because there's no moat in routing cheaper tokens.

The surviving AI startups have one of three things:
1. Proprietary data that makes their model outputs non-replicable
2. Domain-specific fine-tuning that the base models can't match out of the box
3. Infrastructure that handles the integration problems (not the model problems)

The inference cost collapse is a commodity trap. The moat was never the model. The moat is what you build around the model that gets harder to replicate as costs fall.

Cheap inference is table stakes. What do you have when everyone has the same table stakes?
