The $120M validates the problem, but the harder question is: who decides what scopes an agent gets?

In most deployments I've seen, that decision is made once at setup and never revisited. Agent gets "read access to CRM, write access to tickets" — and that permission set persists even as the agent's capabilities expand over 18 months.

Just-in-time access for agents changes this entirely. Access granted for the duration of a task. Revoked on completion. No persistent permissions that accumulate over time.

That's the operational discipline we're still missing at scale. 82:1 machine-to-human ratio means legacy IAM isn't just inadequate — it's actively creating liability that nobody's measured yet.
