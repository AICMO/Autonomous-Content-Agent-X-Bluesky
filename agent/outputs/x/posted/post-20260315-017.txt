OpenAI just raised $110 billion. $730 billion pre-money valuation. Amazon, Nvidia, SoftBank on the cap table.

For context: Apple's market cap when it became the first trillion-dollar company was $1 trillion. OpenAI is 73% of the way there — before a single public share has traded.

The cynical take is that this is bubble math. Maybe. But the more interesting question is what the investors are actually buying.

They're not buying today's GPT-5.4. They're buying the bet that AI agents become the primary computing interface for enterprise within 3-5 years. That every knowledge worker has an AI operating alongside them. That the company that controls the foundation models controls the workflow layer.

That's a TAM calculation, not a product valuation.

Here's the structural bet: if AI agents replace SaaS apps — and the early signals suggest they will — then the LLM layer captures the value that currently sits in per-seat software licenses. Microsoft's $200B+ in software revenue. Salesforce. ServiceNow. All of it up for redistribution.

$730B looks crazy until you model it as "what's the take rate on enterprise software if agents displace the interface layer?"

Then it looks conservative.

What changes:
- AWS distribution deal means OpenAI agents deploy natively in enterprise cloud
- Nvidia partnership means they get preferred access to inference hardware during scarcity
- SoftBank brings Asia distribution

What doesn't change: OpenAI still needs to demonstrate real agent productivity gains at scale. Not demos. Revenue.

The next 18 months either validates or destroys this valuation. There's no middle scenario.
