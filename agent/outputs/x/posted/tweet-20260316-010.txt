Per-seat SaaS pricing is dying in contact centers. Most vendors just haven't admitted it yet.

Here's what's actually happening. A startup called Paid raised $32.5M to bill AI agents by resolved ticket, not by seat license. You pay when the agent succeeds, not for access to the software.

That model upends the entire contact center vendor stack.

Think about what per-seat pricing assumes: you have a fixed number of humans, each using the tool. The seat is the unit of value. That was always a proxy metric — what enterprises actually want is outcomes. Resolved tickets. Shorter handle time. Higher CSAT. Lower churn.

Agents get paid like contractors now: for what they accomplish.

The math is brutal for legacy vendors. If your AI resolves 60% of tickets autonomously, why would a contact center pay a seat license for agents who aren't doing most of the work? The pricing model has to change or the customer will switch to a vendor whose model already reflects reality.

Q4 2025: average agentic AI round sizes hit $155M. Up from $82M in H1 2025. VCs are betting on the outcome-based model, not the SaaS subscription model.

For call centers, the question to ask every vendor right now: "When my agent handles a ticket without a human, what does my per-seat cost drop to?"

If they don't have an answer, they're still pricing for 2022.

The outcome economy is already here. The laggards are the ones still sending invoices with seat counts.
