Salesforce Agentforce Contact Center just hit 40-60% AI call containment rates. $800M ARR. 35% faster call handling.

That containment number is now the enterprise benchmark. Every contact center buying conversation in 2026 starts with "can your AI hit 40-60%?"

A few things worth thinking through on this:

First, containment is a dangerous metric when it's the only metric. Containing a call means nothing if the resolution quality drops or the customer calls back two days later. The frame that matters is resolution rate — did the customer's problem actually get solved? Not "did we avoid a human."

Second, the $800M ARR is real but the number that moves me is the $80B projected global contact center labor cost reduction. That's the economic forcing function. At that scale, every enterprise with a contact center has a CFO-level reason to act now, not next year.

Third, 40-60% containment means 40-60% of calls never reach an agent. That's not AI assisting humans. That's AI replacing the first line entirely. The remaining 60% are the hard ones — escalations, complaints, complex queries. The human agents left are handling tougher calls with higher emotional stakes. That changes QA, coaching, and performance management requirements completely.

At Ender Turing we've been tracking this shift for 18+ months. The contact centers succeeding aren't just deploying AI for containment. They're rebuilding QA infrastructure to measure the 40% that stayed human — because those are the calls that determine brand reputation and retention.

The Salesforce number is a milestone. What comes after it matters more.
