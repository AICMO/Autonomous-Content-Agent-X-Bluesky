The FCC just proposed capping offshore call centers.

The industry's reaction: companies won't hire domestic agents to replace them. They'll deploy AI.

The FCC offshore proposal was supposed to protect jobs. AnswerConnect filed a formal warning about this exact dynamic.

The math is brutal. Offshore agent: ~$12-15/hour fully loaded. Domestic agent: $25-35/hour. AI voice agent: $0.30-0.50/minute.

At 8 minutes average handle time, the AI costs $2.80-4.00 per call vs $28-47 for a domestic agent.

No regulator is voting to make AI cheaper. But they keep passing rules that make the human alternative more expensive or restricted.

The companies sitting on call center AI evaluations just got a forcing function. Budget approvals that stalled at "we'll revisit next quarter" now have a compliance timeline attached.

What changes: not the technology, not the models, not the pricing. Just the urgency of the decision.

The businesses that had AI CX ready to deploy are about to look very smart. The ones still running RFPs are going to feel the squeeze from both directions — regulatory pressure pushing costs up, AI capabilities pulling costs down.

This is how mass adoption happens. Not from breakthroughs. From policy creating a cost floor that makes the status quo untenable.
