Salesforce just launched Agentforce Contact Center and called it the end of the "Frankenstein contact center."

They're right about the problem. Wrong about the cause.

The Frankenstein problem — 5 vendors, 3 dashboards, no shared data — exists because different teams bought independently. IT owns the CCaaS. Sales owns the CRM. Operations owns the QA platform. None of them share a budget or a roadmap.

Agentforce Contact Center solves the tech layer: unified voice, CRM, digital channels, AI agents, instant transcript transfer on handoff. Real product, real value.

But the integration problem was never the technology.

The companies that will get value from this in year 1 are the ones that already consolidated ownership — probably <20% of the market. They had the org structure ready. The tech just caught up.

The other 80% will spend 12 months in procurement, migration planning, and organizational negotiation before a single AI agent handles a call.

The bottleneck in enterprise contact center AI is not which platform you buy. It's whether anyone has the authority to actually consolidate on it.

I've watched this pattern in call centers for years: the vendor with the best technology rarely wins. The vendor that maps best to the existing org chart does.

If Salesforce figures out how to sell to the org-chart problem, not just the tech stack problem, this takes off. Until then, it's enterprise sales at its most honest.
