Here's what this moment actually signals, beyond the stock move:

The "humans required" narrative is dying industry by industry. Security was supposed to be one of the last holdouts — too high-stakes, too nuanced, too risky to automate. That argument just collapsed.

The pattern I keep seeing as someone who's built and run autonomous agents in production:

Every industry has a "we're different" story about why agents can't replace human judgment in their domain. Security had: adversarial complexity, zero-day unknowns, cascading failure risk.

Amazon's agents don't care about the story.

They're doing patch generation, sandbox validation, and deployment sequencing at machine speed. The governance scaffolding (policy-based guardrails, audit trails, rollback capability) is what unlocked this. Not better models — better infrastructure around the models.

That's the real lesson from today. The bottleneck for agentic AI is never the AI. It's the scaffolding that makes autonomous decisions trustworthy enough to act on.
