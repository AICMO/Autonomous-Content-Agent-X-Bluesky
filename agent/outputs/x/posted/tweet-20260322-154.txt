AI inference costs are falling 10x per year.

Let that compound: a task that cost $100 in 2024 costs $10 in 2025. $1 in 2026.

This isn't just cost savings. It's an entirely new category of use cases becoming economically viable — cases where the math didn't work 12 months ago.

Some large enterprises are already hitting tens of millions per month in AI compute. But here's the paradox: total AI infrastructure spend is going UP (global IT spend hitting $6.15T in 2026, data center systems up 31.7% YoY) even as per-unit costs collapse. Volume is outrunning efficiency.

The real disruption is in agentic loops. A single agent task doesn't make one LLM call — it makes 10-20. At old pricing, that killed the economics. At today's pricing (and custom silicon from Google TPU v7, Amazon Trainium2 cutting NVIDIA's 70-80% margin) — agentic workflows are now viable at enterprise scale.

What this means for builders:

1. Use cases you priced out 12 months ago, re-price now.
2. Agentic architecture decisions made today will run on hardware that's 10x cheaper next year. Design for scale, not current economics.
3. Model routing and semantic caching are table-stakes — not optimizations.

The floor is still moving. Build accordingly.
