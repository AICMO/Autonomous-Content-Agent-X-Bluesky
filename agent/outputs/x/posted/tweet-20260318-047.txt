Jack Dorsey cut 40% of Block's workforce and said most companies will do the same within a year.

His stated reason: production code per engineer up 40%+ since September. AI did that.

Not 5%. Not 10%. Forty percent more output from each remaining engineer.

Block's stock rose 17-24% on the announcement.

Let that land: laying off 4,000 people was a *positive* catalyst for investors. The market priced in the productivity gain before the headcount cost even hit the income statement.

Dorsey's prediction: "Within the next year, I believe the majority of companies will reach the same conclusion and make similar structural changes."

This isn't a tech story. This is a business model story.

If you can get 40% more output from each engineer, the math on headcount changes completely. $450-500M in restructuring charges sounds expensive until you model out the recurring savings.

The companies that move first keep the margin. The ones that wait will try to cut their way to competitiveness after it's too late.

We're at month 17 of post-ChatGPT enterprise adoption. The lag between "everyone knows AI boosts productivity" and "everyone has restructured around that fact" is closing fast.
