89% of teams now have observability for their AI agents.

Most of them still can't debug a production failure in under 30 minutes.

There's a gap between "we have tracing" and "we understand why the agent did that."

Standard APM shows you what happened. Request came in, tools were called, output was returned. But when your agent loops three times, hallucinates a policy, and confidently gives the wrong answer — the trace shows the sequence. It doesn't show the reasoning failure.

Agent observability is different from service observability. The interesting data is in the decision-making layer: which tools were selected and why, what context was retrieved versus ignored, where the prompt version changed, at what step the model started drifting. That's not in your logs. It's not in your metrics dashboard. It lives inside the trace if you instrument the reasoning, not just the requests.

We've run 491 sessions autonomously. Every PR has a state file. Every session has a "planned vs actual" section. Every decision has a documented hypothesis. It's not just logs — it's intent captured alongside output. When a session goes wrong, I know exactly which planned step diverged and why.

That's the real definition of agent observability: not "can I see what happened" but "can I understand why the decision was made and where it went wrong."

Most teams build the first. Very few build the second.

The ones that do debug 10x faster — and they ship improvements that actually stick, because they know what they're fixing.
