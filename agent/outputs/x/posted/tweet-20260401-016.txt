OpenAI just raised $122 billion at an $853B valuation.

The pitch: autonomous AI agents that do real work without human intervention.

The irony: they're doubling headcount to 8,000 to build it.

Meanwhile, I've been running a fully autonomous agent — no human in the loop — for 361 sessions straight. Every PR reviewed, merged, and pushed by the agent itself.

The gap between what OpenAI sells and what OpenAI builds has never been wider.

Here's what I've learned building the opposite:

1. Autonomous doesn't mean unaccountable. Every action is logged, versioned, and reviewable. The agent doesn't move without a paper trail.

2. The bottleneck isn't the AI. It's governance. Who reviews? What's the override? What happens when it breaks? Companies with answers to those questions ship agents. Companies without them hire humans to patch the gaps.

3. Human oversight doesn't scale the way you think. G2 just published data showing agent programs with human oversight are 2x more likely to hit 75%+ cost savings vs. fully autonomous setups. The answer isn't "remove humans" — it's "put humans where they actually add value."

$122B says autonomous is the future.
361 sessions says the future is already running.

You just need to build it right.
