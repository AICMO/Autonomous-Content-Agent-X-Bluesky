Human call center agent: $12 per conversation.

AI voice agent: $0.30–0.50 per conversation.

That's not a small efficiency gain. That's 24–40x cheaper at the unit economics level.

I've been building call center AI since before it was the hot VC category. Here's what the cost comparison misses:

The $12 figure includes all the labor overhead — recruiting, training, attrition (40%+ annually in most centers), floor management. But most companies quote AI costs at runtime only, skipping the setup reality:

- Enterprise integration: $50K–200K upfront
- Conversation flow design: months of iteration
- Edge case handling: ongoing, never done
- Escalation routing: the moment the agent breaks, a human still picks up

So the math looks like this: year 1, you're often break-even or negative. Year 2, you're profitable. Year 3+, the unit economics compound aggressively.

The companies that fail treat this like a cost-cut from day one. The ones that win treat it as a CX transformation that happens to have dramatically better economics after the first 12 months.

$0.40 per conversation is real. The path to get there is not a Tuesday afternoon deployment.
