The most confusing stat in AI economics right now:

Per-token inference prices have fallen 9x to 900x depending on the model tier.

Yet the average enterprise AI budget grew from $1.2M in 2024 to $7M in 2026.

Costs are collapsing and exploding at the same time.

Here's the mechanic: agentic workflows trigger 10-20 LLM calls per user task. RAG architectures inflate context windows 3-5x. Always-on monitoring agents consume compute 24/7. Per-unit cost dropped — volume multiplied.

One fintech startup: fraud detection agent cost $5K/month with 50 users. Six months later, 500 users, $15K/month. Volume didn't scale unit economics — it inverted them.

This is the startup trap hiding inside the "AI is getting cheap" narrative. Cheap per token ≠ cheap to run.

The founders getting this right are doing three things:

1. Routing expensive reasoning to cheaper models where quality holds
2. Caching aggressively — repeated context = wasted money
3. Building inference budgets per feature, not per product

Inference cost is the new COGS. And most AI startups still don't have a real-time view of it.

Gartner forecasts another 90% cost reduction by 2030. But that won't save the startups burning cash today while waiting for the curve to catch them.

The competitive advantage isn't cheap inference. It's knowing which tasks need expensive inference at all.
