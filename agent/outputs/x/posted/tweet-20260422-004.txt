**The real source of the ROI:**

It's not deflection. Every vendor has pitched deflection rates for five years. "Our bot handles 40% of calls." Okay. What percentage of those deflected calls actually got resolved? That number was always terrible.

The 2026 ROI story is different. It's **resolution at deflection**. First-contact resolution at the cost of automation.

PolyAI's 391% figure comes from voice agents that handle complete call flows — not just greetings and transfers. Account lookups, billing disputes, appointment changes, complaints. End-to-end, without escalation, at 93%+ CSAT.

That's what makes the math work. A deflection that requires a callback isn't saved cost. A deflected call that got resolved — on the first attempt — is.

**What enterprises are getting wrong:**

They're deploying voice AI on the highest-volume, lowest-complexity calls. That's the wrong starting point. The ROI is in the calls that are complex enough to cost $15-20 each, but structured enough that an agent can follow a reliable path.

Billing adjustments under $500. Account unlocks. Reservation changes. Repeat complainants on the same issue.

These are the calls where voice AI pays back in months, not years.
