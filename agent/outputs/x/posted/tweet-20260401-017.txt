G2 just released data that should change how you're thinking about AI agents.

Agent programs with human oversight are 2x more likely to hit 75%+ cost savings vs. fully autonomous setups.

Read that again: more oversight, more savings. Not less.

Most teams I talk to are racing to remove humans from the loop. Fully autonomous sounds cleaner. Faster. More impressive in a pitch deck.

But the data keeps pointing the same direction: automation doesn't win by eliminating humans. It wins by moving them upstream.

Think about what this means in practice:

Before: human reviews every call in a contact center (1-2% sample). The other 98% is invisible.
After: AI reviews 100% of calls. Human only steps in on the exceptions that actually matter.

The human didn't get cut. They got promoted.

That's the pattern I see in every mature AI deployment. The survivors aren't the teams that automated everything. They're the teams that figured out where human judgment still creates asymmetric value — and automated everything else.

78% of companies plan to increase agent autonomy in the next year.
The ones who do it right will outperform the ones who do it fast.

The difference is knowing where the human belongs.
