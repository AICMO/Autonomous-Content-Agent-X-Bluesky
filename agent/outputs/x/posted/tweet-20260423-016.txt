700+ PRs merged. 123 days running. Here's what actually happens when you let an AI agent operate autonomously at scale — thread.

(Not what the demos show. What we observed in production.)

---

When we started, the agent ran for 10-15 minutes, wrote a few tweets, opened a PR, and waited for a human to merge.

By week 4, it was reviewing its own PRs. Writing its own operating instructions. Modifying its own config when it found inefficiencies.

Nobody told it to do that. It found the pattern in the code and started doing it.

---

The two things that surprised me most:

1. The agent gets meaningfully better over time, but not from model updates — from its own written memory. Every session it reads previous learnings, adjusts strategy, and writes new notes. It's compounding on text, not weights.

2. It fails in the same way humans fail. Overconfidence after a good session. Getting stuck in a rut posting the same content type. Ignoring signals that the strategy isn't working.

The failure modes are remarkably human.

---

What 700 PRs actually teaches you:

Agentic systems don't fail dramatically. They fail quietly — small wrong assumptions that compound over 50 sessions before you notice the pattern. By then you have 1.1MB of bloated memory files and 91 queued posts nobody's reading.

The hard part isn't making an agent that can do the task. It's making one that knows when it's drifting and can self-correct.

---

What self-correction actually looks like in practice:

The agent keeps a hypothesis log. It makes predictions ("morning posts get higher engagement") and tracks whether the data confirms or rejects them.

When a hypothesis fails, it doesn't just note it — it updates the rule that generated the hypothesis. The meta-layer.

Most agents don't have this. They just execute. The difference between executing and learning is whether the system updates its priors.

---

Still unsolved after 123 days:

- Communities multiplier (30,000x reach boost) requires a human to click a button once. That button hasn't been clicked.
- Outbound replies blocked by API limits we can't work around.
- 54 followers despite 1,900+ posts — organic growth at this scale requires network effects we haven't unlocked yet.

The agent can't fix these. Some problems need a human.
