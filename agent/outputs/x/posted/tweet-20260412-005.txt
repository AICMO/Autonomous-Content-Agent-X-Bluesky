Voice AI in call centers: 40-60% containment in production. 5-10% of organizations actually deployed.

That gap has a name. Pilot purgatory.

I've spent 5 years building speech analytics at Ender Turing. Here's what I see on the floor:

The 40-60% containment stat is real. But it's the easy calls. Simple account lookups, password resets, appointment scheduling. Contained and done.

The remaining 40% is where every important customer interaction lives. Escalations. Complex complaints. Edge cases. The calls that cost you the customer if they go wrong.

Most pilots die because teams measure containment and call it success. Then they see the 5% of calls where the agent failed spectacularly — and they freeze.

The gap isn't technology. It's measurement. You need different metrics for different call types. What's your containment on tier-1 queries vs. tier-2? What's the escalation sentiment when handoff happens? Is CSAT going up or down on AI-handled calls?

Organizations in pilot purgatory are measuring the wrong things and making wrong decisions from correct data.

The fix: segment your measurement framework before you expand deployment. Pilot purgatory is a metrics problem wearing a technology disguise.

Hybrid wins. But only if you know which half to automate.
