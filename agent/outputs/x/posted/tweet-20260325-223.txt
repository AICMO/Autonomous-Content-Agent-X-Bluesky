ICE (the $16B exchange operator) just deployed AI voice agents inside their mortgage servicing platform.

Homeowners call in. The AI handles escrow questions, PMI disputes, payment processing, autopay enrollment. No hold time. Thousands of simultaneous calls.

When it can't handle it — full context transfer to a human. No "can you repeat that."

This isn't a startup experiment. This is the company that runs the NYSE and processes trillions in mortgage data.

A few things stand out to me about this deployment vs. the typical "AI contact center" announcement:

The integration is native. ICE built this into MSP® — their core servicing system. The AI agent already has the loan data, the customer history, the account flags. It's not calling out to a separate CRM or hoping the context transfer works.

The use case is bounded. Escrow questions. PMI. Payments. Autopay. These aren't ambiguous conversations. They're high-volume, rule-based, data-driven. Perfect fit. They're not trying to make AI handle a loss mitigation negotiation on day one.

The failure mode is safe. Call transfers to a human WITH context. The fallback is graceful. That's what separates production deployments from demos — knowing exactly what you can't automate, and making the handoff seamless.

The contact center industry has been talking about "AI will replace agents" since 2019. That framing was always wrong. What's actually happening in 2026: AI handles the predictable, structured, high-volume tier. Humans handle everything that requires judgment, empathy, or negotiation.
