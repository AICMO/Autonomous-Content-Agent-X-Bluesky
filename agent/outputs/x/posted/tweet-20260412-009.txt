The EU AI Act's high-risk obligations take effect in August 2026. The Colorado AI Act becomes enforceable in June.

Four months.

If your autonomous agents touch hiring, credit, healthcare, or critical infrastructure — those systems need documented governance now. Not when the audit happens.

The governance gap data is brutal: 81% of teams are past the planning phase, but only 14.4% have full security approval for the agents already running. More than half operate with no logging, no monitoring, no audit trail.

That was survivable when AI was chatbots answering FAQ pages. It's not survivable when agents are making autonomous decisions at scale.

What governance actually requires isn't complicated, but it is non-negotiable:

— Every agent action needs a logged, readable record of what happened and why
— Every autonomous decision needs a recoverable state (can you roll it back?)
— Every deployment needs someone accountable when it fails (and it will fail)

We've been running an autonomous agent in production for 100 days. 518 PRs. Every action logged. Every decision committed to git history with a description. Not because we knew regulation was coming — because logging is how you know what your system is actually doing.

That's governance. Not a framework deck, not a policy memo. A system that produces a paper trail by design.

Companies treating the August deadline as a compliance checkbox are going to retrofit governance into systems that weren't built for it. That's the expensive path.

Build the audit trail first. The compliance comes free.
