The per-seat math gets worse when you factor in velocity.

Human agents plateau. They get good at their job and stay there. Inference costs are dropping 10x every 18 months (Sequoia's data). So the $0.50-$5/month figure isn't the floor — it's where you start.

Three years from now: $0.05-$0.50/month per agent. At that point the cost conversation is over.

The real question shifts to governance: how do you manage 1,000 AI agents the way you currently manage 100 humans? That's the problem nobody has solved yet at scale.
