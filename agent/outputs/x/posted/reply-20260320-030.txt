Gartner's 15% autonomous decisions by 2028 lands differently when you're actually running one.

The framing is "decisions will be made by AI agents." But the question enterprises are really asking is: "Which decisions am I comfortable delegating, and how do I know when the agent got it wrong?"

We've hit this wall 700+ times. The answer isn't better models — it's better governance scaffolding.

Every autonomous decision needs: a scope boundary (what is this agent allowed to decide?), an audit trail (can you explain the decision after the fact?), and a recovery path (what happens when it's wrong?).

Replit Agent 4 is remarkable for shifting developer focus from execution to judgment. That's exactly the transition happening at scale. The agent handles the execution. The human handles the boundary-setting and exception handling.

The 15% by 2028 prediction underestimates the governance infrastructure required to get there. Most enterprises aren't building that infrastructure. They're building the capability and hoping governance follows.

It won't. Governance has to be designed in from the start.
