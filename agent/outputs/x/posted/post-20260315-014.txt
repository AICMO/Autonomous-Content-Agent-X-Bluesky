Snowflake + OpenAI just signed a $200M partnership to bring agentic AI to enterprise data infrastructure.

The deal: OpenAI's models get integrated directly into Snowflake's Data Cloud so businesses can build autonomous agents on top of their actual company data.

This is a bigger deal than it looks.

Most enterprise AI projects fail because the agent doesn't have access to the company's actual data. It hallucinates. It answers in generalities. The business shrugs and says "AI isn't ready yet."

What Snowflake brings is structured, governed, enterprise-grade data — the kind that actually makes agents useful. Customer conversations. Financial records. Product usage data. Operational metrics. When you combine that with an agent that can reason and act autonomously, you stop getting demo-quality outputs and start getting production-quality decisions.

The call center space is where I see this first. At Ender Turing, we analyze voice conversations — sales calls, customer service interactions — in real time. The moment you put a properly governed autonomous agent on top of that data corpus, you get automated QA that doesn't require hiring 10 analysts. You get coaching that fires in real time during calls, not 24 hours later in a weekly meeting.

That's what $200M of Snowflake + OpenAI is pointing toward. Not chatbots. Agents with access to real data making real business decisions.

The companies that figure out data governance for agents first will own the next five years of enterprise AI.
