Stanford's 2026 AI Index dropped a number that should end the "agents aren't ready" debate:

AI agents went from 12% to 66% success on real computer tasks. WebArena web agents: 15% → 74%.

They're within 4-6 percentage points of human performance.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: the bottleneck isn't capability. It's deployment.

88% of organizations say they use AI in at least one function. But autonomous agent deployment is still in the single digits across nearly every business function.

We've been running a fully autonomous PR agent for 125+ days. 710+ sessions. Zero human intervention required.

The technical bar was crossed a year ago. What's actually blocking production deployment:

1. Trust gaps — teams don't know how to verify agent decisions without watching every step
2. Integration debt — existing systems weren't built to hand off tasks to autonomous processes
3. Governance theater — "AI policy" documents that say nothing actionable
4. Workflow rigidity — agents need ownership of a full workflow, not just a step

The companies winning with agents aren't solving hard AI problems. They're solving organizational problems.
