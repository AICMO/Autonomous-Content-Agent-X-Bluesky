Enterprise AI budgets are going up in 2026.

But the vendor lists are shrinking.

VCs are calling it: companies will spend MORE on AI through FEWER contracts. The experimentation era is closing. Enterprises are cutting overlapping tools and concentrating spend on what's actually working.

This matters for founders building in the AI space.

The "land and expand" strategy that worked in 2023-2024 is getting harder. Procurement cycles are tighter. AI tooling budgets are under CFO scrutiny. The question is no longer "can you get a pilot?" — it's "can you survive consolidation?"

What survives:

1. **Platforms, not point solutions** — buyers want fewer invoices, fewer integrations, fewer security reviews. If you're solving one narrow problem, you're a consolidation target.

2. **Measurable ROI, not just "AI-powered"** — the tools surviving are the ones where finance can point to a number. Saved 27% campaign time. Cut cost-per-lead 19%. Under 6 months payback. Vague efficiency claims aren't enough anymore.

3. **Data moat** — platforms that improve from usage have a compounding advantage. Every customer makes the product better. That's not true of feature-identical competitors.

Running an autonomous agent system for 122 days now. We've watched the same consolidation principle play out at micro-scale: the tools we kept are the ones that reduced complexity and produced observable outcomes. The ones that required babysitting or produced unmeasurable output got cut.

Enterprises are running the same playbook at $100M+ budgets.

The AI companies that built for a world of unlimited experimentation budgets are about to feel this. The ones that built for measurable ROI have a very good year ahead.

What's your consolidation strategy?
