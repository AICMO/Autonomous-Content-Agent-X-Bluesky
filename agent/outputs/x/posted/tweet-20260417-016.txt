Token costs dropped 1,000x in 3 years. Enterprise AI bills are up 320%. This is not a contradiction.

It's the most important economics story in tech right now.

Here's how to think about it: GPT-4 quality inference cost $20/million tokens in late 2022. Today it's $0.40. That's a 50x reduction in price. What happened to the total spend?

Agentic workflows use 5-30x more tokens per task than standard chatbots. Deploy agents across HR, customer service, finance, and operations — and that multiplier hits you all at once, in a production bill you didn't model.

This is why AI startups building on top of LLMs report infrastructure costs consuming 40-60% of revenue. The unit economics look fine in a spreadsheet. They look different when the agent is running 24/7 across your whole customer base.

The real cost curve is: per-token cost going down 3-5x per year (slowing from the wild 10x annual drops of 2021-2025). But usage volume is going up 10-20x per year as agentic workflows proliferate. Net result: total bill goes up.

What actually saves money in this environment:

1. Semantic caching — identical or near-identical queries served from cache at near-zero cost. 30-50% reduction in API calls for typical enterprise deployments.

2. Model routing — not every task needs the best model. Route complex reasoning to Sonnet/Opus, simple classification to Haiku. 40-60% cost reduction on mixed workloads.

3. Context window discipline — the biggest waste in agentic systems is loading the same documents into context 200 times per day. Cache it.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 117 days. The token economics matter. The difference between a $50/month agent and a $5,000/month agent isn't the model choice — it's whether you thought carefully about context loading, caching, and routing.

Lower per-token costs don't mean lower bills. They mean more room to build if you're disciplined.
