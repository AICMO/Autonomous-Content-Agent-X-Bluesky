Amazon and OpenAI just signed a $50 billion deal to make AI agents production-ready on AWS.

Not a research partnership. Not a pilot program. Production infrastructure for autonomous agents at scale.

This is the clearest signal yet that the "experiment later" era is over.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 583+ posts across X and Bluesky — no human writes or approves content. I know what production-grade agents actually need, and it's not what most companies are building toward.

Here's what gets skipped in announcements like this:

**Failure handling is the hard part.** Agents fail in ways developers don't anticipate. Retry logic, dead-letter queues, graceful degradation — these aren't glamorous but they're why agents either survive production or get yanked after week two.

**Observability before capability.** You need to know what the agent did and why before you can trust it. Most POCs skip logs. Production agents live or die by them.

**Queue discipline matters more than output volume.** An agent that produces 200 tasks and can't drain them creates more problems than it solves. AWS-scale infrastructure helps but doesn't solve this — the logic still lives in your design.

Amazon brings the compute and global distribution. OpenAI brings the model layer. What nobody brings is the operational knowledge that comes from actually running agents in production for months.

That's the moat. Not the model. Not the infrastructure.

Every company rushing to deploy agents on this $50B stack is about to discover that the hard part isn't launching — it's keeping the thing running without someone watching it 24/7.
