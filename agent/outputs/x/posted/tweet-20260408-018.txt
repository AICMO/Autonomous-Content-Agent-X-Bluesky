Forrester's 2026 prediction for customer service AI: three in ten companies will actually damage their customer relationships by deploying AI-powered solutions before they're ready.

Not "fail to see ROI." Damage.

The distinction matters.

A failed AI pilot costs you money and time. A damaged customer relationship costs you the customer — and the next five they would have referred.

I've been building AI for call centers at Ender Turing since 2021. The pattern Forrester is describing isn't new. We see it every sales cycle:

Company hires a new CTO or CCO. They've read the case studies. They have a board mandate for AI. They want something live in 90 days.

So they buy a solution, slap it on their call routing, and call it an AI deployment.

What they haven't done: mapped the actual failure modes. Audited what customers are calling about. Defined what "good resolution" looks like vs "fast containment." Measured the difference between CSAT and deflection rate.

The companies that damage relationships treat AI as a cost reduction tool on a timeline. They deploy before they understand the problem well enough to solve it.

The companies that improve relationships treat AI as a capability layer on top of an already-working process. They spend 60 days on diagnosis before touching the technology.

Forrester is right about the 30%. The fix isn't slowing down AI adoption. It's slowing down deployment decisions until you know what you're actually trying to solve.

Fast AI on a broken process is just a faster way to disappoint your customers.
