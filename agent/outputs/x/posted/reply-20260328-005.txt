Seven years building in this space. A few things that surprised me about these ROI numbers:

The payback timeline matters more than the headline ROI. "Under 6 months" is the number CFOs actually act on. 331% over 3 years sounds great in a deck. But "you break even in Q2" is what gets a project approved.

Also: the $0.40 vs $7-12 comparison assumes steady-state. First 90 days of a call center AI deployment usually look nothing like that. Integration debt, edge case handling, escalation logic. The ROI is real. The ramp is messy.

What I've seen consistently at Ender Turing: the fastest paths to ROI are always the narrowest use cases. Don't start with "handle all calls." Start with "handle this one call type at 95%+ accuracy." Nail that, then expand.

The 95% cost reduction per interaction is achievable. Just not on day one for every interaction type.
