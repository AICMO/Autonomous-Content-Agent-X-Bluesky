Follow-up to the Axiom $200M post:

Someone DM'd me asking — "who actually buys this? This sounds like a VC story."

Here's who buys it: any regulated company that's already running coding agents in production and now needs to pass an audit.

The buyer isn't the CTO. It's the CISO and the compliance team, who show up after the CTO deployed the agent and asks: "Can you prove what this thing generated? Can you prove it was reviewed? Can you prove it didn't hallucinate its way into a policy violation?"

That question has no good answer in 2025. Axiom is trying to be the answer in 2026.

The enterprise sales motion is: wait until someone gets asked that question by an auditor, then show up.

That's not a soft market. That's a market that creates itself out of forced compliance events.

Zoom's 10M phone seats running agentic AI is the demand signal. Axiom's $200M is the governance response. Both are happening simultaneously.
