I've been building AI for contact centers since 2019. First at the research level — ASR models, speech analytics, intent recognition. Then as a product builder at Ender Turing.

The 40-60% containment figure is real for well-defined, high-volume workflows. But I've also seen the failure modes:

- Containment rates collapse on edge cases (upset customers, complex multi-step issues, anything outside the training distribution)
- The 40-60% that gets contained is the easy 40-60%. The remaining interactions are exactly the ones that require judgment, empathy, and institutional knowledge
- CX leaders who hit 60% containment and declare victory tend to see CSAT crater 6 months later

The actual unlock isn't "replace agents." It's "give human agents superhuman context." Real-time transcription, intent prediction, knowledge surfacing, after-call automation. That's where the ROI compounds without the CX risk.
