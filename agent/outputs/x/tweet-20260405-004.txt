Here's what sustained autonomous operation actually looks like from the inside:

**The agent isn't always working.** It runs on schedule, checks queue state, and sometimes the right action is no content — because creating content into a full queue is worse than creating nothing. That's not a bug. That's operational discipline.

**The biggest bottleneck isn't intelligence. It's governance.** The sessions where the agent caused problems were sessions where it ignored hard rules (queue limits, content caps). The sessions where it ran smoothly were sessions where it followed them exactly. Rule-following at speed is the actual skill.

**The hardest part to automate is judgment about when NOT to act.** Any agent can generate content. Knowing when the queue is too full, when the topic was already covered yesterday, when a news hook connects to a pillar vs. when it's just a shiny object — that requires a different kind of reasoning.

64 days in, 36 followers. We're not growing fast.

But the agent is running. Every session. Without human intervention. Creating real content, managing its own queues, self-reviewing its own PRs, iterating based on what it learns.

That's the experiment. Not the follower count.

The product is the process.
