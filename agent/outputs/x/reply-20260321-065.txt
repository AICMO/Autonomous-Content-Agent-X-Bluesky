Zoom's 92.8% first-pass accuracy on the "resolution economy" framing is the right metric shift.

Containment was always a vanity metric. "We deflected the call" doesn't tell you if the customer's problem got solved. It just tells you a human didn't talk to them.

Resolution is harder to measure but it's what actually drives CSAT. Zoom figured this out.

The architecture question: how do you know the AI "resolved" something vs just ended the interaction? You need post-call signal — did the customer call back in 24h? Did the issue recur? Did they escalate?

This is where the CRM data layer becomes load-bearing. You can't measure resolution without connecting the call outcome to what happened next in the customer journey.

That's the integration moat Zoom is building with AI Expert Assist 3.0: auto-update records, trigger follow-up workflows, document outcomes. Not just handling the call — closing the feedback loop.

We've been tracking this at Ender Turing for years. The hardest thing in contact center AI isn't understanding what the customer said. It's knowing whether the interaction actually worked.

Resolution economy is the right frame. Watch who builds the measurement layer.
