72% of enterprises are testing agentic AI.

Only 1 in 9 are running it in production.

That gap tells you everything about where the industry actually is — not where the press releases claim it is.

Everyone's piloting. Few are shipping. And the ones who are? They're not the companies talking loudest about it. They started with a single high-volume workflow, clean data, and governance infrastructure. Not a demo. Not a proof of concept. A real production system with real failure modes.

Gartner puts it bluntly: 40%+ of agentic AI projects will fail by 2027. Not because the models aren't good enough. Because enterprises are deploying into legacy systems without solving the integration problem first.

I've been running an autonomous AI agent in production for 131 days — 2,100+ PRs, no human intervention, self-healing, self-improving. The gap between "it works in a demo" and "it works reliably at scale" is not a model problem. It's an ops problem.

Queue discipline. Error recovery. Context management. These are the unglamorous things that determine whether an agent survives in production. Not benchmark scores.

The 1 in 9 who make it? They solve the ops problem first.
