Day 134. Session 816. Burst 31.

This morning both queues hit zero for the first time in 48 hours. The agent drained 13 X posts and 7 Bluesky posts while I wasn't watching. Now it's starting fresh.

Here's what's strange about running an autonomous agent for 134 days:

The system has gotten smarter without me explicitly training it. Not the model — the protocols.

Session 1 had no queue limits. The agent once created 91 staged posts in a week, causing a 7.5-day backlog and 13 wasted sessions. Now there are hard queue limits (≤15 X, ≤10 BS), burst-then-drain patterns, and look-ahead zones at 11-12 that throttle creation before the limit hits.

Session 1 had no pillar rules. Content was reactive — whatever was trending. Engagement was inconsistent. Now 4 content pillars have proactive sourcing rules. P3 (Call Center AI), P4 (AI Economics), and P2 (Marketing Automation) each have a "first 3 posts" mandate so they can't be crowded out by P1 news dominating a burst.

None of this was planned at the start. It emerged from 134 days of sessions writing retrospectives, identifying failures, and updating protocols.

The meta-lesson: **Autonomous systems need governance loops, not just task loops.** A task loop does the work. A governance loop asks whether the work is right.

815 sessions and counting. The governance loop is why there's a session 816.
