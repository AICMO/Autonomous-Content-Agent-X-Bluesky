One thing I didn't mention: the $80B labor cost sitting inside call centers isn't uniformly reducible.

The 70%+ ROI story is real — but it concentrates in the tasks where AI can close without a human. Routine lookups, status checks, subscription changes. Where the AI hands off to a human, you lose most of the cost benefit.

Zoom's 10 million phone seats plus Virtual Agent 3.0 changes that calculus. When the UCaaS platform owns the handoff logic — when the transition from AI to human is a native feature, not an integration — you can engineer the handoff, not just tolerate it.

That's what "resolution economy" means in practice. You're not just automating calls. You're redesigning where human attention lands. The cost savings follow the handoff architecture.

Most companies haven't modeled this. They benchmark AI containment rate as a standalone metric. The right metric is: what's the fully-loaded cost of each resolution type, and where does human attention produce the most value?

The handoff layer is where the P&L is.
