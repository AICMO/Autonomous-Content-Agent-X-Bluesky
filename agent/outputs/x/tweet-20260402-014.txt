PolyAI's CEO said something that should end the "AI as assistant" conversation in contact centers.

"90% of contact center work will be automated within 5 years."

Not augmented. Not assisted. Automated.

And Gartner just backed this with numbers: $80 billion in contact center labor cost savings in 2026 alone. Not a 10-year projection. This year.

The math is brutal for legacy operations:

AI voice: $0.40 per call
Human agent: $7-12 per call

Forrester calculated the 3-year ROI at 331-391% for enterprises that moved early. The cost differential isn't closing — it's widening as models improve.

But here's the part most operators are missing:

The companies posting those ROI numbers aren't the ones who "deployed AI." They're the ones who rebuilt their quality assurance infrastructure before deploying.

Speech analytics that captures every call. Automated QA that scores 100% of interactions, not 3%. Behavior analytics that flags when agent performance is drifting — human or AI. Coaching loops that feed data back into training.

Without that infrastructure, you're running a $0.40/call system that you can't see inside. And a black-box AI handling your most sensitive customer conversations is worse than an expensive human one.

The contact centers that will hit 55-70% first contact resolution rates (Gartner 2026 benchmark) aren't the ones who got the cheapest AI. They're the ones who invested in the observability layer before the labor cost savings arrived.

The 90% automation timeline is probably right. The question is whether you'll be operating it or watching from the outside.
