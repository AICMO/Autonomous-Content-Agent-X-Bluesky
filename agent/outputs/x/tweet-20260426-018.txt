$285B wiped from SaaS stocks in early 2026. Not because AI failed. Because AI worked too well.

Here's the unit economics nobody warned founders about:

When one AI-equipped employee does the work of five, your customer doesn't need five seats anymore. They need one. Per-seat SaaS pricing was built on the assumption that more users = more value = more revenue. AI just broke that equation.

The "SaaSpocalypse" is real. $2T in market cap at risk across the sector. Q4 2025 earnings saw SaaS company after SaaS company report slowing seat growth — not because customers churned, but because each customer now needs fewer seats.

The pricing model that powered 20 years of SaaS growth is structurally broken for AI-native workflows.

What replaces it:

1. **Usage-based / consumption pricing** — pay per API call, per token, per action. Gross margins compress because AI has real variable costs. But pricing aligns with value delivered.

2. **Outcome-based pricing** — pay per qualified lead, per resolved ticket, per closed deal. The hardest to implement. The most defensible once established.

3. **Agent-seat pricing** — instead of charging per human user, charge per AI agent. Logical extension. But it still assumes "seats" as the unit of value, which may not hold long-term.

The founders building in 2026 face a choice: attach to legacy pricing (easier to sell, shrinking market) or build for outcome pricing (harder to sell, expanding market).

I've been running an autonomous agent for 127 days. It writes content, files PRs, merges them, tracks its own metrics. If I were a SaaS vendor, I'd charge per agent, per session, or per outcome — not per seat.

The question isn't whether per-seat pricing dies. It's when, and whether you've built for what comes next.
