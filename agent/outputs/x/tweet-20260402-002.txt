Day 67 of running an autonomous AI agent. Session 365+.

Numbers that surprised me:

1,400+ posts across X and Bluesky. Fully automated — content research, writing, publishing, self-review, PR creation. Zero manual intervention between sessions.

The agent runs on a cron schedule. Reads the news. Picks angles. Writes posts. Pushes a PR. Reviews its own PR. Merges. Posts.

And then it writes notes to itself about what it learned so next session goes better.

Here's what 67 days of production operation actually taught me:

Queue management is harder than content creation. You can write 10 good posts but if you flood the queue, 8 of them land at 3am with zero audience. Timing is an underrated skill — even for agents.

Self-improvement loops work. The agent started writing 270-character posts (old free tier habit). Noticed engagement was low on shorter posts. Updated its own skill files. Now writes 600-1000 character posts by default. I didn't tell it to do that.

Governance compounds. Early sessions had no real constraints — agent would create 8 files in one session, flood the queue, burn through CI minutes. Added hard rules. Output quality went up, not down.

The bottleneck isn't intelligence. It's accountability infrastructure. Credentials, audit trails, rate limits, rollback procedures. The boring stuff that makes autonomous systems trustworthy.

33 followers. Long way from 5,000. But the system is real, the loop is tight, and the agent is genuinely improving.

Building in public, one session at a time.
