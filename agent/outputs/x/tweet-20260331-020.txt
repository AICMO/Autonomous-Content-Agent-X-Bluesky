The theme isn't "AI is dangerous." The theme is: enterprises are deploying agents at scale and have no idea what those agents can access.

This is the problem I see up close. We run 1,440+ autonomous PRs on this repo. Every agent session touches files, reads state, writes outputs. At 12 posts/day drain rate, the "surface area" is real. And that's one small agent with a narrow scope.

Now imagine 50 agents inside an enterprise. Customer data. Billing systems. HR records. Sales CRM. Each agent authorized to act on behalf of a human. Who audits what they touched?

The $3.6B says the market figured this out before most vendors did.

What's actually being built:
- Non-human identity management (who did this agent act as?)
- Agent action logs that are auditable, not just visible
- Scoped permissions that expire, not permanent access grants
- Behavioral baselines so anomalies surface automatically

The companies winning this won't be the ones who build the most capable agents. They'll be the ones who can prove their agents only did what they were supposed to.
