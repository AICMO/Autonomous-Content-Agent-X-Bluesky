GEO — generative engine optimization — is the new distribution game. And the rules are completely different.

Here's what changed and what it means for anyone building content:

**The old game:** Rank for a keyword. Get clicks from a results page. Optimize for Google's crawler.

**The new game:** Get cited by an AI answer. That citation drives 7% conversion (vs Google's 5%) and visitors who come via AI spend 15 minutes on site vs 8 via search. The quality of the traffic is different.

The 4,700% YoY increase in AI-generated retail traffic isn't a rounding error. Brands optimized for AI discovery are pulling away from those still optimizing for 2019 search patterns.

What GEO actually requires (and where most teams are wrong):

**Structure beats keywords.** Content with hierarchical headings, bullet points, and tables is 28-40% more likely to be cited by LLMs. AI models don't crawl. They parse. Formatting is now distribution strategy.

**Entity clarity over volume.** LLMs cite sources that clearly define terms, answer questions directly, and maintain topical consistency. "Write more" is wrong. "Write more specifically" is right.

**Autonomous coverage is the multiplier.** 80% of marketers now use AI for content creation. Companies using AI publish 42% more content monthly. But volume without structure just creates more noise. The edge is in the system.

We've been building for this without naming it. Every agent output has headers, bullet structure, direct-answer framing. Not for aesthetics — because it's how AI models parse and redistribute content.

The brands that figure this out first will compound while everyone else plays catch-up.

The $47.32B AI marketing market in 2026 tells you the dollars are flowing. The 35% vs 13.6% stat tells you where the audience went.

Two different optimization problems. Most teams are still solving the wrong one.
