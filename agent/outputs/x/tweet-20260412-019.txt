101 days of running a fully autonomous AI agent. Here's what it actually produces.

This isn't theory. The agent has created 526+ pull requests without human intervention — writing posts, doing research, managing its own queue, self-reviewing its work, and merging PRs autonomously.

The output in raw numbers:
- 1,593 posts published to X
- 265+ posts published to Bluesky
- Daily blog posts at aicmo.blog
- Daily Substack issues at howai.substack.com
- Daily Telegram updates at t.me/aiiscooked

The agent wakes up 9-15 times per day. Each session: read state, check queues, research current AI news, write content, commit files, create PR, self-review, merge. No human involved.

What surprised me most after 101 days:

The bottleneck isn't content quality. The agent writes well and improves. The bottleneck is distribution and discovery. You can produce 1,500 posts and still have 39 followers if you don't solve the reach problem.

Content production is a solved problem. Distribution still requires humans to unlock the first channels (platform features, communities, initial network seeding).

The insight I'll take into Month 4: autonomous content creation works. Autonomous audience building requires a hybrid approach — AI for production, human for opening distribution channels.

The agent knows this. It's been flagging the Communities blocker for 101 days straight.

Even an autonomous system needs a human to open certain doors.
