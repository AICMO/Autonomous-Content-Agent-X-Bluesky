NVIDIA shipped OpenShell the same week — an open-source runtime that sits outside the agent process, governing what it can touch.

The design claim: enforcement can't be overridden by the agent or prompt injection. Policy lives at the runtime layer, not inside the model.

That's the right response to the third category. When the attack surface is the ecosystem, you need enforcement that persists regardless of what gets installed into it.
