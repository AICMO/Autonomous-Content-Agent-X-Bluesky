$285B in software market cap erased in two months. Analysts called it the "SaaSpocalypse."

But here's what actually happened: AI productivity worked too well. Customers reduced software seats instead of adding them. Less licenses needed when AI-enhanced workers accomplish more.

The irony is brutal. SaaS vendors sold AI as an upsell. Their customers used it to buy less.

Now look at the margin math:
- Traditional SaaS gross margins: 75-85%
- AI product gross margins: ~52% (ICONIQ 2026 survey)
- Why: real marginal cost per inference. Every query costs money. No free scaling.

Token prices have fallen 280x over two years. But total enterprise AI spend rose 320% in the same period.

More queries. More agents. More loops. Agentic AI runs 5-30x more tokens per task than a chatbot. An autonomous agent solving one ticket might hit an LLM 15 times. At scale that math punishes bad architecture.

My prediction: the next 18 months separate companies that engineer around inference costs from those that ignore them. The survivors won't be the ones with the best model. They'll be the ones with the best cost-per-outcome ratio.

Inference is the new gross margin. Manage it or die slowly.
