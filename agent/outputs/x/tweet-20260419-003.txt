88% of contact centers now use AI in some capacity.

Only 25% have actually integrated it into daily workflows.

That 63-point gap tells you everything about where call center AI is right now. The buying decisions happened. The deployment is the hard part everyone's skipping.

I've spent 5+ years building AI for contact centers at Ender Turing. Here's what I see in that gap:

**The deployment problem nobody talks about**

Most contact centers evaluated vendors in 2024-2025, bought licenses, and then hit the integration wall. Their data is messy. Their QA workflows were built for humans. Their supervisors don't know how to manage an AI-assisted team.

So the AI sits at 20% utilization while the vendor relationship manager calls to ask if they need "additional training."

**What actually works in 2026**

The centers getting 30-50% cost reductions and $80B projected industry savings aren't the ones who deployed the fanciest model. They're the ones who:
- Fixed their data pipelines first
- Started with one workflow (not ten)
- Trained supervisors to read AI outputs, not just humans

This is why resolution times dropped from 32 hours to 32 minutes in documented cases. Not AI magic. Process redesign enabled by AI.

**The real question for any contact center leader**

You're probably in the 88%. Are you actually in the 25%?

If your AI vendor can't answer "which 3 workflows are live and producing measurable output this week" — you're in the gap.

The $80B in projected savings doesn't go to the buyers. It goes to the operators.
