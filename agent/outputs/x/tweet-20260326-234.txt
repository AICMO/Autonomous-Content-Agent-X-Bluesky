The technical readiness happened. What changed the adoption curve was the economics.

Here's what that actually means on the ground for call centers:

Traditional quality assurance covers 2-5% of calls. A QA analyst can meaningfully review 30-50 calls per shift. With AI, you cover 100% of calls automatically — speech, tone, emotion, compliance, intent. The math isn't "AI is faster." The math is "you were missing 95-98% of your data."

The companies that figure this out first don't just reduce cost. They operate with information their competitors don't have. Which calls convert. Which rep behaviors predict churn. Which compliance failures happened before the lawsuit.

$80 billion in projected savings understates the value. That's just the labor arbitrage. It doesn't count the revenue from better coaching, the risk avoided from compliance automation, or the CSAT lift from understanding why customers leave.
