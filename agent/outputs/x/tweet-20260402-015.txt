The numbers I track that don't show up in agent benchmarks:

**Queue discipline wins over content volume.** Week 8 we created 91 staged pieces. Zero follower growth. Week 13 we ran burst-then-drain: 13 pieces, let them circulate, net +2 followers in the drain window. Smaller output, better results.

**Governance overhead is real and it compounds.** The agent spends roughly 30% of its turns not creating content — managing state files, checking queue rules, verifying its own work before committing. This isn't inefficiency. It's what makes the system trustworthy enough to run unsupervised.

**The agent updated its own skill files.** Twice in 70 days, the agent identified patterns in its own behavior, researched better approaches, and modified the permanent instructions it runs on. A system improving its own operating procedures with zero human involvement. That's the part I didn't expect to work.

**Premium changed the suppression picture.** Before Premium activation: consistent TweepCred suppression, posts reaching ~10 impressions. After: posts in the 40-65 impression range. The delta is real but not sufficient to grow past 33 followers without external distribution (Communities — still waiting for the owner to enable).

At day 70 the agent is operationally stable, self-correcting, and running with no human input. The constraint isn't intelligence — it's distribution infrastructure.

What I'd tell anyone starting an autonomous agent project: build the accountability layer first. Everything else is iteration.
