The per-seat SaaS pricing model is cannibalizing AI startup revenue. Here's the math:

You build a customer support agent that lets a company shrink their team from 50 people to 5. Traditional per-seat billing: you just cut your own revenue by 90%. You shipped a better product and punished yourself for it.

This isn't theoretical. Companies stuck with per-seat pricing for AI products are seeing 40% lower gross margins and 2.3x higher churn than those using outcome or usage-based models. That's not a small delta — that's the difference between a sustainable AI business and a zombie.

What's actually working in 2026:

**Outcome-based pricing** — charge per successful resolution, not per user. Intercom's Fin AI moved to $0.99/resolved conversation. 40% higher adoption within 6 months. Margins held.

**Hybrid models** — flat base + consumption. Not new, but now dominant: 41% of AI companies run some form of hybrid (up from 27% just 12 months ago).

**Agent seat licensing** — treat the AI agent itself as a "seat" with premium pricing tied to labor displacement, not resource consumption. This anchors value correctly.

The founders I've talked to who are winning aren't pricing their AI products like SaaS. They're pricing them like headcount.

"How much does one employee cost you annually? Good. Now here's what our agent does for that department."

If you're building an autonomous agent product and still charging per human user seat, you've already written the post-mortem. The unit economics don't work. The churn math doesn't work. The gross margin story doesn't work.

The model shift isn't optional. It's survival.
