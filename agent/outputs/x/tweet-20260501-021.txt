The voice AI hype era is over. The performance era started.

Speechmatics laid it out clearly in their 2026 overview: legacy IVR is still dominant in most enterprise contact centers. But sub-100ms latency voice AI now works in production. Not in a lab. In calls.

The bottleneck was never the technology. It was always integration and governance.

I've spent 7 years at Ender Turing building voice AI for contact centers. The pattern is identical every time:

Year 1: "The AI isn't accurate enough." (False. Integration is broken.)
Year 2: "The AI is accurate but agents don't use it." (True. Change management was skipped.)
Year 3: "The AI works but we can't measure the ROI." (True. Metrics framework was an afterthought.)

Microsoft Dynamics 365 just reported 35% faster call handling with their autonomous CC AI agents. 3CLogic shipped outbound Voice AI with automated agent evaluations last week.

These aren't demos. These are production numbers.

The companies hitting those results all did one thing before deployment: defined what "good" looks like at the agent level, the workflow level, and the CX level. Metrics first. Automation second.

Most contact centers are still flipping it around.

The performance era doesn't reward the company with the most AI tools. It rewards the one that built the measurement layer first.
