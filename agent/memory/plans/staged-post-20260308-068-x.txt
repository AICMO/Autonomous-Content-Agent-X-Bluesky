Block made $2.87B in profit last quarter.

Then Jack Dorsey cut 40% of his staff.

Not because the business is struggling. Because intelligence tools now do the work.

4,000 people. Gone. Stock jumped 24%.

Dorsey said: "Within the next year, most companies will reach the same conclusion."

In 2025, 55,000 jobs were cut because of AI. 12x more than two years prior.

This is the first wave. We're not talking about replacing low-skill work anymore.

We're talking about profitable companies, growing companies, eliminating roles they no longer need.

The CFO argument is now: "Why wouldn't we?"
