### S2: Entro Security Launches AGA — Agentic Governance & Administration
- Pillar: Autonomous Agents (P1)
- Hook: Entro Security launches AGA — a new IAM/IGA category built for AI agents, not humans. Inventory, ownership, least privilege, auditability, enforcement — all redesigned for agents with machine-speed access.
- Key fact: Traditional IAM tools were designed for human users on human timescales. AI agents with write access to databases, APIs, and financial systems operate at machine speed — making legacy governance frameworks obsolete.
- Our angle: The governance gap is the real risk, not the capability gap. Every enterprise that's running agents without redesigned access controls is operating with human-speed guardrails on machine-speed actors. The 82:1 machine-to-human identity ratio (Palo Alto Networks) was never in the original security model.
- Date: 2026-03-19
- Source: Help Net Security

### S3: HiddenLayer 2026 AI Threat Report — 1 in 8 Companies Hit by Agentic AI Breaches
- Pillar: Autonomous Agents (P1)
- Hook: HiddenLayer's 2026 AI Threat Landscape Report: 1 in 8 companies report AI breaches linked to agentic systems. 76% cite shadow AI as a definite or probable problem (up from 61% in 2025).
- Key fact: As AI systems evolve from assistive tools to autonomous agents capable of independent action, the attack surface has expanded dramatically. Companies are deploying agents faster than they're securing them.
- Our angle: Shadow AI is just unsanctioned agents. 76% of companies have shadow AI problems — meaning three-quarters of enterprises are running agents they didn't officially approve. That's not a technology risk. That's a governance risk that looks like a technology risk.
- Date: 2026-03-19
- Source: HiddenLayer / IT Security News

### S4: Gartner 2028 Prediction — 15% of Work Decisions Made Autonomously by Agents
- Pillar: Autonomous Agents (P1) / AI Economics (P4)
- Hook: Gartner (via Enterprise Connect 2026): at least 15% of day-to-day work decisions will be made autonomously by AI agents by 2028 — up from 0% in 2024.
- Key fact: This isn't a productivity claim — it's a structural shift in decision-making authority. In 4 years, 15% of enterprise decisions move from human to machine. The companies building governance infrastructure NOW are buying options for that future.
- Our angle: 0% → 15% in 4 years is the fastest transfer of decision authority in business history. The constraint isn't whether agents can make decisions — we're already doing it (700+ PRs autonomously). The constraint is whether organizations trust the governance infrastructure enough to let them.
- Date: March 2026
- Source: Kenway Consulting / Enterprise Connect 2026 recap
