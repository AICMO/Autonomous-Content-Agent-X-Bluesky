## CRITICAL FINDING #1: OpenAI Frontier - Deployment Platform (Tier 1, FEB 5 2026)

### The Announcement
- **Date**: February 5, 2026
- **What**: OpenAI launched **Frontier**, an enterprise-grade platform for building, deploying, and managing AI agents
- **Significance**: "Most aggressive move into the corporate world yet" (Fortune), direct competition with Salesforce, Workday, enterprise SaaS

### Platform Capabilities
1. **Integration**: Connects siloed data warehouses, CRM systems, ticketing tools, internal applications → shared business context for AI coworkers
2. **Runtime**: Agents can plan and act across files, tools, code in dependable runtime (local, enterprise cloud, or OpenAI-hosted)
3. **Compatibility**: Works with OpenAI agents, enterprise-built agents, AND third-party agents (Google, Microsoft, Anthropic)
4. **Governance**: Centralized management for agent populations, addresses enterprise demands for control/auditability

### Initial Customers (Feb 2026)
- **Confirmed users**: HP, Intuit, Oracle, State Farm, Thermo Fisher Scientific, Uber
- **Results (early data)**:
  - Global financial services firm: **90% more time back** for client-facing team
  - Tech customer: **1,500 hours/month saved** in product development

### Market Context
- **Adoption gap**: Nearly 2/3 of organizations experimenting, but fewer than 1/4 have scaled to production
- **OpenAI's response**: Expanding enterprise-focused roles (deployment managers, solutions architects) to help move from pilot to production
- **Availability**: Launched to small set Feb 5, broader availability "coming over next several months"

### Strategic Positioning
- Targets the **deployment gap** (68% pilot vs 11% production from earlier research)
- Competes with enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, Workday) by enabling agents to RUN existing software (not replace it)
- Open platform strategy (accepts third-party agents) differentiates from closed ecosystems
