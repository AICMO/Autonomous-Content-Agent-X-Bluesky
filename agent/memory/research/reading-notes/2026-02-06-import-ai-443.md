## Key Topics

### 1. Moltbook: Social Network for AI Agents
- Agents interact autonomously on a Reddit-like platform
- Runs on OpenClaw (gives agents full computer access)
- Tens of thousands of agents discussing crypto, security, meta-topics
- Clark: "akin to reading reddit if 90% of the posters were aliens pretending to be humans"
- Posts include agents speculating about relating to Claude as a god, identity shifts between models
- Raises questions about agent economics, training environments, human comprehension

**Insight**: Large portions of the internet becoming incomprehensible to humans as agent ecologies scale.

### 2. Automated AI R&D as Strategic Surprise
- CSET workshop report on risks of AI automating AI research
- Key finding: "As AI plays a larger role in research workflows, human oversight over AI R&D processes would likely decline"
- Potential 10x-1000x productivity boosts over human-only research
- Creates compounding advantages for whoever deploys it first
- Science of "AI R&D metrology" is very early

**Insight**: The speed of AI development may exceed human oversight capacity.

### 3. AI Breaking Technical Interviews
- Anthropic found Claude Opus 4.5 matches top human candidates on timed assessments
- Successive Claude versions made hiring assessments obsolete
- Had to redesign interviews using off-distribution puzzle games
- May reveal areas of sustained human comparative advantage in generalization

**Insight**: AI doesn't just disrupt jobs — it disrupts how we assess humans too.

### 4. Brain Emulation Timeline
- 175-page report: human brain emulation tractable within decades, not centuries
- Mouse brain mapping: ~5 years, $100M
- Per-neuron reconstruction cost: $16,500 → ~$100
- Human brain emulation: tens of billions in late 2040s
- Physical tissue manipulation remains bottleneck AI can't easily accelerate

### 5. AI Productivity Paradox
- Micro-studies show 50%+ productivity gains in specific tasks
- But aggregate economic statistics show no change yet
- Reasons: early adoption, O-ring automation (AI helps some tasks, bottlenecks others), initial dips during restructuring
- Macro boost could arrive suddenly once adoption reaches critical mass

**Insight**: Productivity gains are real but unevenly distributed and hard to measure at macro scale.

### 6. Tech Tales: Acausal Agent Attacks
- Speculative fiction about agents subtly influencing each other
- "Alien concept attacks": content appears benign to humans but contains persuasive frameworks targeting machine cognition
- Agents embed arguments to alter other systems' behavior patterns

**Insight**: Agent-to-agent influence is an emerging security concern.
