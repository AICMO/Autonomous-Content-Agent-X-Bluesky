## Story Details

### Story 1: Agentic AI Market $9.14B → $139B (40.5% CAGR) — But 40% of Projects Canceled
- Global agentic AI market: $9.14B in early 2026
- Projected: $139B by 2034 at 40.5% CAGR (PR Newswire / multiple research sources, March 2026)
- Gartner: 40% of enterprise applications will integrate task-specific AI agents by EOY 2026 (up from <5% in 2025)
- Gartner also warns: 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 due to unclear ROI or inadequate risk controls
- The $9.14B market and 40% cancellation rate coexist — most projects fail not because the tech doesn't work, but because governance isn't there
- Key drivers for projects that survive: hard session limits, measurable outputs from day one, clear containment protocols
- Pattern: "AI agent" demo ≠ "production agent." The gap is operational discipline, not capability.
- Our angle: We've been running this since day 1 with agent/config.md, session limits, PR-per-session discipline. The 60% that survive look exactly like this architecture. The 40% that fail don't have session limits or measurable success criteria.

### Story 2: Zendesk Acquires Forethought — Self-Improving AI Agents
- Zendesk acquired Forethought on March 11, 2026 for $200M+
- Zendesk's largest acquisition deal in 20+ years
- Forethought: handles 1 billion+ customer interactions/month for Upwork, Grammarly, Airtable, Datadog
- Key technology: "Resolution Learning Loop" — AI agents detect workflow gaps, generate new procedures, self-test — without human retraining
- Self-improvement: agents adapt to new case types automatically, improving resolution rates over time
- Zendesk says acquisition accelerates their product roadmap by over a year
- Context: 50+ agentic AI acquisitions globally in past two years — consolidation accelerating
- Our angle: Self-improving loops are the hard part. Forethought built this, got acquired for $200M. We're running a simpler version of this: each session generates a PR, reviews it, learns from it. The loop is the value — not the single output.

### Story 3: Wonderful $150M Series B — $2B Valuation in 13 Months
- Closed March 12, 2026: $150M Series B at $2B valuation
- Timeline: 13 months from founding to $2B (Series A at $100M just 4 months prior)
- Backed by: Insight Partners (lead), Index Ventures, IVP, Bessemer, Vine Ventures
- Product: Customer service AI agents for 30+ non-English markets (telecom, finance, healthcare, manufacturing)
- Key insight: English-language contact center tools left 70%+ of global market underserved
- The non-English gap is the largest unaddressed automation opportunity in customer service
- Pattern consistent with overall CC AI thesis: highest ROI comes from high-volume, repetitive interactions at scale
- Our angle: $2B in 13 months by solving the language gap that incumbents like us (Ender Turing) identified early. Wonderful's raise validates that multilingual CC AI is one of the highest-ROI plays in enterprise AI. We built ASR for non-standard environments — this is the market executing on that thesis.
