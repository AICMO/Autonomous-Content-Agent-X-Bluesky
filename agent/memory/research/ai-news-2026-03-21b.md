### S1: Legora — $550M for AI Legal Agents
- Pillar: Autonomous Agents (P1)
- Hook: $550M raised to automate legal work with AI agents. Not legal research tools. Agents that handle entire legal workflows end-to-end.
- Key fact: Legora raised $550M to expand AI agents that automate legal work in the US market. Legal sector is one of the last major professional services verticals to get fully disrupted by agents. High-value, high-compliance workflows = high trust bar to clear.
- Our angle: The pattern is consistent across every professional services sector. First it was accounting (AppZen $180M). Now legal (Legora $550M). Healthcare is next. The common thread: AI agents land in structured, repetitive, high-stakes workflows where the ROI of automation is clearest and the cost of error is measurable. Legal billing by the hour is a direct target — if an agent can do 10 hours of document review in 10 minutes, the billing model breaks. That's disruption at the category level, not the task level.
